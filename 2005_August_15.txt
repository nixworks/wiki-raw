* Many countries celebrate the 60th Anniversary of the [[Victory over Japan Day|Victory in the Pacific]] and the end of [[World War II]]. [[Beijing]] holds an arts performance gala. In Japan, in a large-scale ceremony led by the [[Emperor Akihito]], Prime Minister [[Junichiro Koizumi]] apologizes for people's suffering Japan caused during World War II. The ceremony was met with scattered protests. [http://www.gx.xinhuanet.com/dtzx/2005-08/15/content_4885879.htm (Xinhua)] [http://news.bbc.co.uk/chinese/simp/hi/newsid_4150000/newsid_4152100/4152122.stm (BBC China)]
* The [[National Assembly of Iraq|Parliament of Iraq]] grants an extension to the nation's leaders to draw up a [[Proposed Iraqi constitution|new Constitution]]. [http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/8931395/ (MSNBC)]
* The U.S. refused to abide by a [[North American Free Trade Agreement]] (NAFTA) ruling on the [[U.S.-Canada softwood lumber dispute]], leading to Canada's Finance Minister [[Ralph Goodale]] saying he was considering trade sanctions on the US. [http://www.boston.com/news/local/maine/articles/2005/08/15/canada_to_take_action_against_us_in_softwood_dispute/ (Boston Globe)]
* [[Israel]] begins the [[Gaza Strip]] [[Israel unilateral disengagement plan|pullout plan]]. The [[Kissufim]] Roadblock was shut down at midnight between August 14 and August 15. Palestinian leader [[Mahmoud Abbas]] hailed the move as "historic" but said Israel should also pull out of the [[West Bank]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/4150028.stm (BBC)], [http://www.canada.com/news/world/story.html?id=d92f6c87-4c9e-439a-85af-d6259776b3ef (Canada.com)]
* [[Conflict in Iraq]]: A senior aide to [[Abu Musab al-Zarqawi]], [[Abu Zubair]],  has been shot dead following a U.S. [[ambush]] in [[Mosul]], Iraq. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/4152348.stm (BBC)]
* The Indonesian government and rebels from the [[Free Aceh Movement]] (Gam) sign a peace deal aimed at ending their near 30 year conflict. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/4151980.stm (BBC)], [http://english.chosun.com/w21data/html/news/200508/200508160004.html (chosun.com)]. UN Secretary-General Kofi Annan applauded both parties for reaching a peaceful settlement of the dispute through dialogue, and commended [[Martti Ahtisaari]], former [[President of Finland]], for the mediation role that he played throughout the negotiation process. [http://news.xinhuanet.com/english/2005-08/16/content_3358907.htm (XinHua)]
* Due to stalled labour talks, the [[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation]] [[Lockout (industry)|locks out]] the company's 5500 members of the [[Canadian Media Guild]], severely disrupting operations in most of [[Canada]].  The key point of contention is the broadcaster's insistence on more [[Employment|contract employee]]s to the expense of full time positions. * [[Phil Mickelson]] wins the 2005 [[PGA Championship]] [http://www.pga.com/pgachampionship/2005/ (PGA.com)]
* India celebrates its sixtieth Independence Day.
