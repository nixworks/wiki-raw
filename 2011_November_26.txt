;Armed conflicts and attacks
*26 [[Pakistani]] soldiers are killed after a [[NATO]] helicopter [[2011 NATO attack in Pakistan|opens fire on a Pakistani checkpoint]], sparking outrage from the Pakistani government. [http://edition.cnn.com/2011/11/26/world/meast/pakistan-attack/index.html?hpt=hp_t1 (CNN)]
*[[Colombia]]n military forces raid a [[FARC]] rebel hideout in the province of [[Caquetá Department|Caquetá]], freeing police sergeant Luis Alberto Erazu, who had been held hostage by FARC for 12 years. Four other hostages are killed by the rebels during the raid. [http://www.miamiherald.com/2011/11/28/2522439/colombian-farc-hostage-describes.html (''Miami Herald'')]

;Politics and elections
*[[New Zealand general election, 2011|New Zealand general election]] and [[New Zealand voting method referendum, 2011|voting method referendum]]:
**Voters in [[New Zealand]] go to the polls for a general election and voting method referendum. [http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/6039845/Election-2011-Kiwis-have-their-say (NZ Stuff)]    
**The [[New Zealand National Party]], led by incumbent [[Prime Minister of New Zealand|Prime Minister]] [[John Key]], wins 60 seats, one short of the 61 required for a majority. [[ACT New Zealand]] and [[United Future New Zealand]] indicate that they will work with the National Party to form a [[coalition government]]. [http://tvnz.co.nz/election-2011/awesome-win-national-john-key-4573748 (Television New Zealand)]
**The [[Mixed Member Proportional]] voting system, on [[early vote]]s only, wins a majority of the vote in the [[New Zealand voting method referendum, 2011|referendum]] and will be kept. Full results will be released on 10 December 2011. [http://www.stuff.co.nz/dominion-post/news/politics/election-2011/6041261/MMP-holds-on-after-200-000-votes-counted (''The Dominion Post'' via New Zealand Stuff)]
