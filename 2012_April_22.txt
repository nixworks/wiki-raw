;Armed conflict and attacks
*[[2011–2012 Bahraini uprising|Bahraini uprising]]:
**Renewed protests against the regime break out overnight with police firing [[tear gas]] and [[stun grenade]]s at civilians. Chants of "Down with [[Hamad bin Isa Al Khalifa|Hamad]]," calling for the [[King of Bahrain]] to be ousted from power, are reported by witnesses. [http://www.rte.ie/news/2012/0422/new-clashes-in-bahrain-ahead-of-grand-prix.html (RTE)]
**[[King of Bahrain]] [[Hamad bin Isa al Khalifa]] issues a pledge of "reform and reconciliation" ahead of the [[2012 Bahrain Grand Prix]]. [http://news.sky.com/home/world-news/article/16213553 (Sky News)]
**Despite calls for the [[2012 Bahrain Grand Prix]] to be cancelled following the death of a man, the race is staged, under armed guard. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-17803310 (BBC)]
**Turnout is reported to be low, though [[Salman bin Hamad bin Isa Al Khalifa]], heir apparent to the Bahraini throne, is seen smiling and shaking hands with other spectators at the finish. [http://uk.reuters.com/article/2012/04/22/uk-bahrain-grandprix-idUKBRE83I0S720120422 (Reuters)]
**It is revealed that [[Force India]] tried to pull out of the race and return to Britain but, after the team's refusal to take to the circuit, there was a confrontation between [[Bernie Ecclestone]] and the Force India team. [http://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/formula-1/bahrain-grand-prix-force-india-802136 (''Daily Mirror'')]
**''[[The Sunday Telegraph]]''{{'}}s chief foreign correspondent Colin Freeman and his fixer and translator are arrested. Activist Ala'a Shehabi is arrested after reporting the arrest of Colin Freeman. ''The Guardian'' reports the arrest of Ala'a Shehabi. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2012/apr/22/bahrain-grand-prix-protests-live (''The Guardian'')]
**Calls for afternoon protests at the destroyed [[Pearl Roundabout]], the original site of the uprising against the ruling [[Al Khalifa family]], get underway as the race ends, while protests are also held outside the [[London]] offices of [[Formula One]] chief, [[Bernie Ecclestone]], with demonstrators chanting "down, down Bernie" and "shame on you Bernie". [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/middleeast/2012/04/2012422141637277957.html (Al Jazeera)]
**[[Anonymous (group)|Anonymous]], which last week took out the official website of [[Formula One]], posts partially redacted data concerning dozens of race ticket holders found on Formula One servers. The international group also posts a statement online saying it intends to carry out further action if imprisoned hunger striker [[Abdulhadi al-Khawaja]] or his family are harmed, promising to "respond with fury and rage the likes of which have never been seen." [http://www.npr.org/2012/04/22/151153644/protests-press-bahrains-formula-one-race (NPR)]
**British politician [[Peter Hain]] expresses dismay that the [[2012 Bahrain Grand Prix]] has been allowed to take place. [http://www.itv.com/news/wales/2012-04-22/peter-hain-angered-at-bahrain-gp/ (ITV News)]
**A [[United Kingdom]] [[Channel 4]] news team is arrested with a Bahraini driver reported as having been assaulted. [http://www.channel4.com/news/channel-4-news-team-arrested-in-bahrain (Channel 4)]
*One [[RKK separatist group|RKK]] (Runda Kumpulan Kecil) separatist group member and a [[Law enforcement in Thailand|Thai policeman]] are killed in a firefight in the village of [[Ruso, Thailand|Ruso]] in [[Thailand]]'s [[Narathiwat Province]]. [http://www.bangkokpost.com/breakingnews/289889/one-rkk-member-killed-in-narathiwat (''Bangkok Post'')]
*[[Sudan]] launches a fresh incursion into [[South Sudan]]. [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2012/04/2012422224749713350.html (Al Jazeera)]
