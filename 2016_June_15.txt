*[[November 2015 Paris attacks]]
**The father of  [[California State University, Long Beach|Cal State Long Beach]] student Nohemi Gonzalez, the only American killed in the November 13, 2015, [[Paris]] massacre, files suit in [[San Francisco]], [[California]], [[United States District Court for the Northern District of California|federal court]] against [[Twitter]], [[Facebook]], and [[Google]], alleging the companies provided "material support" to the [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|Islamic State]] and other extremist groups. While  generally free of liability under U.S. law  which  provides a legal ''[[Safe harbor (law)|safe harbor]]'' for content posted, this case targets  the behavior social media companies enable. The suit is very similar to a case brought against Twitter in January by the widow of a contractor killed in the [[Amman shooting attack|November 9, 2015, attack]] in [[Jordan]]. [http://www.sfgate.com/business/technology/article/Father-of-Paris-attacks-victim-sues-social-media-8208997.php (AP via ''San Francisco Chronicle'')] [http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-3643640/Father-Paris-attack-victim-23-sues-Google-Facebook-Twitter-claiming-let-terrorism-spread.html (''Daily Mail'')] [https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2016/jan/14/widow-american-killed-jordan-attack-sues-twitter-isis (''The Guardian'')]
