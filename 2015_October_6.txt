;Business and economy
*The [[European Court of Justice]] decides an [[Treaty|international agreement]],  generally known as a [[Safe harbor (law)|Safe Harbor]] rule, used by thousands of companies for moving people’s digital data between the [[European Union]] and the [[United States]] is invalid, effective immediately. The decision throws into doubt how global technology giants like [[Facebook]], [[Amazon.com|Amazon]] and [[Google]] can collect, manage, and analyze online information from their millions of users in the 28-member bloc. Decisions by this court, the highest legal authority in the EU, cannot be [[Appeal|appealed]].
