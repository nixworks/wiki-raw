;International relations
*''[[Philippines v. China]]''
**The [[Permanent Court of Arbitration]] rules in favor of the [[Philippines]] against [[China]] over [[Territorial disputes in the South China Sea|territorial disputes]] in the [[South China Sea]]. [http://www.nytimes.com/2016/07/13/world/asia/south-china-sea-hague-ruling-philippines.html (''The New York Times'')], [https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/jul/12/philippines-wins-south-china-sea-case-against-china (''The Guardian'')]
**The court unanimously says that China has "no historical rights" based on the so-called "[[nine-dash line]]" map. [http://www.cnn.com/2016/07/12/asia/china-philippines-south-china-sea/index.html?adkey=bn (CNN)], [http://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-china-36771749 (BBC)]
**China  rejects the tribunal's ruling, declaring it null and void. [[President of the People's Republic of China|Chinese President]] [[Xi Jinping]] reiterates the South China Sea has been Chinese territory since "ancient times."  [http://bigstory.ap.org/article/d6d1fe0ff98341aba8440b32b1c35ef6/china-rejects-ruling-south-china-sea-null-and-void (AP)]

[http://www.rferl.org/content/kyrgyzstan-activist-askarov-sentence-revoked/27853446.html (Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty)]
