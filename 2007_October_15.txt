* Montenegrin [[Prime Minister of Montenegro|Prime Minister]] [[Željko Šturanović]] signs a [[Stabilisation and Association process|Stabilisation and Association Agreement]] with the [[European Union]], saying that [[Montenegro]] is hoping to lodge an official application for EU membership in the first half of [[2008]]. [http://euobserver.com/9/24972 (EUobserver)]
* [[Battle of Las Anod]]: Troops from the breakaway [[Somalia|Somali]] republic of [[Somaliland]] seize a town inside [[Puntland]], a rival region loyal to the interim Somali government, killing at least 10 people. [http://english.aljazeera.net/NR/exeres/3C4CBB81-223B-4CC8-AABB-421D2DFDA68C.htm (Al Jazeera)]
* Sir [[Menzies Campbell]] resigns as the leader of the [[Liberal Democrats]] in the [[United Kingdom]], starting a [[Liberal Democrats leadership election, 2007|leadership election]]. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/uklatest/story/0,,-6998274,00.html (''The Guardian'')]
* [[Leonid Hurwicz]], [[Eric S. Maskin]] and [[Roger B. Myerson]] are announced as winners of the 2007 [[Nobel Prize in Economics]] for having laid the foundations of [[mechanism design]] theory. [http://nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/economics/laureates/2007/ (Nobelprize.org)] 
* [[Ibrahim Gambari]], the [[United Nations]] special envoy to [[Myanmar]], warns the [[State Peace and Development Council|Burmese government]] to stop arresting dissidents. [http://ca.today.reuters.com/news/newsArticle.aspx?type=topNews&storyID=2007-10-15T082807Z_01_BKK229851_RTRIDST_0_NEWS-MYANMAR-COL.XML (Reuters Canada)]
* [[Airbus]] delivers its first [[Airbus A380|A380]] aircraft to launch customer [[Singapore Airlines]] in a ceremony in [[Toulouse]], [[France]], almost 18 months behind schedule, but denies rumours that further delays to the programme are looming. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/business/7043812.stm (BBC News)] [http://investing.reuters.co.uk/news/articleinvesting.aspx?type=allBreakingNews&storyID=2007-10-15T081044Z_01_L15255936_RTRIDST_0_AIRBUS-UPDATE-1-PIX-TV.XML (Reuters UK)]
* [[Perth railway station|William Street underground platforms]] and [[Esplanade railway station|The Esplanade Station]] begin service in [[Perth, Western Australia|Perth]], [[Western Australia]]. Part of the [[New MetroRail]] project, they are the first stations to open on the new [[Mandurah Line]]. [http://abc.net.au/news/stories/2007/10/15/2059426.htm (ABC News Australia)]
* [[2007 New Zealand anti-terror raids|Police raids]] are made across [[New Zealand]] under the [[Terrorism Suppression Act]], with individuals supporting [[Māori people|Māori]] sovereignty targeted, and arrested, for their alleged involvement in paramilitary activities. Activist [[Tame Iti]] is among those arrested. [http://www.nzherald.co.nz/section/1/story.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10469938&pnum=0 (''The New Zealand Herald'')]
* The [[17th National Congress of the Communist Party of China]] opens in [[Beijing]], [[People's Republic of China|China]], marking the beginning of a 7-day conclave of China's top leadership. [[General Secretary of the Communist Party of China|General Secretary]] [[Hu Jintao]] delivers the work report for the past five years, and sets the party's direction in the coming five years. Hu also said in the keynote speech that the [[Communist Party of China|Communist Party]] has "fallen short of the people's expectations". [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/7044418.stm (BBC News)] [http://www.chinaview.cn/17thcpc/ Xinhua]
* [[Richard Brunstrom]], the [[Chief Constable]] of [[North Wales Police]], calls for all classified [[drug]]s to be legalised in the [[United Kingdom]]. He argues that the current policy of prohibition is not working, and is based on [[dogma]]. [http://news.independent.co.uk/uk/politics/article3061121.ece (''The Independent'')]
