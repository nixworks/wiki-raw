**[[Hurricane Joaquin]]
***Hurricane Joaquin passes over ocean with temperatures near 86°F (30°C) - the warmest since record keeping began in 1880. [http://www.nhc.noaa.gov/text/refresh/MIATCPAT1+shtml/300535.shtml (National Hurricane Center)], [http://www.upi.com/Top_News/US/2015/09/29/Tropical-Storm-Joaquin-could-threaten-East-Coast-early-next-week/2611443558262/?spt=hs&or=tn_us (UPI)], [http://www.reuters.com/article/2015/09/29/storm-joaquin-idUSL1N11Z2S420150929 (Reuters)]
***Hurricane Joaquin reaches maximum sustained winds of 115 mph and becomes a [[Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale#Category 3|Category 3 hurricane]]. The storm, with additional strengthening expected, should linger over the [[The Bahamas]] through October 2 before heading toward the U.S. [http://abcnews.go.com/US/hurricane-joaquin-sets-eyes-bahamas-eastern-seaboard/story?id=34151054 (ABC News)], [http://www.nhc.noaa.gov/text/refresh/MIATCPAT1+shtml/010249.shtml (NHC)]
***The hurricane is expected to pass [[The Bahamas]] tomorrow, bringing tropical-storm-force winds, storm surges, coastal flooding, and 5-10 inches of rain. While the European forecast model suggests Joaquin will avoid the [[East Coast of the United States|U.S. East Coast]], the American model predicts it will ram into [[Virginia]], [[Maryland]], or [[North Carolina]] this weekend. [http://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/hurricane-joaquin-intensifies-way-bahamas-n436581 (NBC News)], [http://www.nhc.noaa.gov/text/refresh/MIATCPAT1+shtml/302357.shtml (NHC)]
***[[Governor of Virginia|Governor]] [[Terry McAuliffe]] declares a [[state of emergency]] as [[Virginia]] prepares for potentially dangerous flooding from a separate [[Nor'easter|Nor'easter storm system]] and the possible landfall of Hurricane Joaquin. [https://governor.virginia.gov/newsroom/newsarticle?articleId=12936 (Virginia)], [http://www.insidenova.com/headlines/governor-declares-state-of-emergency-as-hurricane-joaquin-approaches/article_a54a59b8-67c3-11e5-acb6-1fd9c8d38e80.html (Inside Northern Va.)]
