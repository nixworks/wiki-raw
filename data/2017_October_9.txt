*[[Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences]]
**The [[Nobel Prize]] committee awards [[United States|American]] [[Richard Thaler]] the [[List of Nobel Memorial Prize laureates in Economics|Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel]] "for his contributions to [[behavioral economics]]." His work focuses on the psychology of the human conflict of what we plan for the financial present versus what we plan for the future. [https://www.nobelprize.org/ (Nobel Prize.org)], [http://www.bbc.com/news/business-41549753 (BBC)]
*[[Japan]]'s third-biggest steelmaker, [[Kobe Steel]], admits to falsification of product data. About four percent of the aluminum and copper products that it shipped from September 2016 to August 2017 were falsely labeled as meeting the specifications requested by 200 customers, including [[Toyota]] Motor Corp, [[Central Japan Railway Company]], [[Mitsubishi]] Heavy Industries, [[Mazda]] Motor Corp, and [[Subaru]] Corp. [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-japan-kobe-steel/kobe-steel-under-the-gun-after-firm-admits-data-falsification-idUSKBN1CF006 (Reuters)]

;Law and crime
*[[Bosnian War]]
**The presiding judge Šaban Maksumić of the [[Sarajevo]], Bosnian war crimes court acquits former [[Bosnia|Bosnian]] military officer [[Naser Orić]] of the charge of killing three [[Serbs of Bosnia and Herzegovina|Bosnian Serb]] [[prisoners of war]] during the Bosnian War. In July 2008, the Appeals Chamber of the [[International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia]] in the [[Netherlands]] acquitted Orić for failing to prevent the deaths of five Bosnian Serb detainees and the mistreatment of eleven other detainees from late 1992 to early 1993. [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-bosnia-warcrimes-srebrenica/srebrenicas-muslim-defender-cleared-of-crimes-serbs-protest-idUSKBN1CE0SK (Reuters)]
