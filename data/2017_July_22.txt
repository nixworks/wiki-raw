*[[Martial law in the Philippines]], [[Proclamation No. 216]]
**Both houses of the [[Congress of the Philippines]] have convened a joint special session to discuss and debate on [[President of the Philippines]] [[Rodrigo Duterte]]'s appeal for the extension of [[Proclamation No. 216]] (martial law on the whole of [[Mindanao]] in response to the [[Marawi crisis]]; set to expire 10 PM [[Philippine Standard Time|PST]]). A joint nominal majority vote (158 out of 314) is needed for the approval of the extension as well as the duration. [https://www.afp.com/en/news/824/philippine-mps-vote-martial-law-extension-bid (AFP)]
**After 7 hours of debate, a joint session of the [[Congress of the Philippines|Philippine Congress]] voted 261 in favor to 18 against (with 45 abstaining or declining to vote, including the senator [[Leila de Lima]] who was unable to vote because of her detention) regarding the motion to extend [[Proclamation No. 216]] until the end of the 2017. [[Liberal Party of the Philippines|Liberal]] senators [[Bam Aquino|Aquino]], [[Franklin Drilon|Drilon]], [[Risa Hontiveros|Hontiveros]] and [[Francis Pangilinan|Pangilinan]] were among the tiny minority that voted against. [http://cnnphilippines.com/news/2017/07/22/Congress-votes-martial-law-extension-Duterte.html (CNN Philippines)], [http://www.philstar.com/headlines/2017/07/22/1720428/congress-oks-martial-law-extension-overwhelming-majority (''The Philippine Star'')]
