*[[2015 Beirut bombings]]:
**[[Ministry of Interior and Municipalities (Lebanon)|Lebanese Interior Minister]] [[Nouhad Machnouk]] reports at least 43 people were killed and more than 239 wounded as two, simultaneous suicide bomb blasts hit a [[Shia Islam|Shi'ite]] community center and a nearby bakery  in the [[Beirut]] suburb of [[Bourj el-Barajneh]], a section of the [[Lebanon|Lebanese]] capital controlled by [[Hezbollah]]. The [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant]] claims responsibility for the attack. Machnouk said a third suicide bomber had been killed by one of the explosions before he could detonate his own bomb. The bombers struck as [[Parliament of Lebanon|Lebanese lawmakers]] held a legislative session for the first time in over a year. *[[Mario Draghi]], head of the [[European Central Bank]], tells the [[European parliament]] in prepared testimony that the outlook for inflation is "weakening." The comment was taken to suggest the ECB will soon take a more stimulative stance on interest rates or money quantity. [https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2015-11-12/draghi-says-inflation-weakening-as-december-policy-choice-nears (Bloomberg)]
*The [[Montreal|city of Montreal]] in [[Canada]] begins dumping 2 billion gallons of raw sewage into the [[Saint Lawrence River]], an action the mayor, [[Denis Coderre]], says is necessary to make repairs and improvements to the city's wastewater system. The move has caused outrage among residents and environmentalists, while the [[Hashtag|hashtag]] "''#‎flushgate‬''" is being used on [[Social media|social media]] sites to voice opposition to the dumping effort. [http://edition.cnn.com/2015/11/12/americas/canada-montreal-wastewater-dump/ (CNN)]
