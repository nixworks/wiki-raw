*[[Supreme Court of Israel|Israel's Supreme Court]] blocks the government's plan,  signed last December by [[Prime Minister of Israel|Prime Minister]] [[Benjamin Netanyahu]] with the U.S. [[Noble Energy]] and the Israeli [[Delek Group]] companies, to develop [[Israel|Israel's]] [[Natural gas field|offshore gas deposits]]. The court found the so-called "stability clause,"  that would prevent Israel from making significant regulatory changes for the next 10 years, undemocratically restricts the [[Knesset|Knesset's]] power. The government has one year  to come up with an alternative arrangement or the plan will be canceled. *[[2014–16 Venezuelan protests]]
**According to a new poll, two-thirds of [[Venezuela]]ns think [[President of Venezuela|President]] [[Nicolás Maduro]] should end his presidency this year or be removed via a recall referendum amid a worsening [[Economic policy of the Nicolás Maduro government|economic crisis]]. In the event of a referendum, 52.1 percent of Venezuelans would vote to remove him from office, up from 44.5 percent in January, according to the poll. *[[2015–16 protests in Moldova]]
**Thousands of people rally in the streets of [[Chișinău]], the capital of [[Moldova]], demanding [[Unification of Romania and Moldova|reunification]] with [[Romania]]. The rally is meant to mark the 98th anniversary since the former Romanian province of [[Bessarabia]] was unified with the [[Kingdom of Romania]] on March 27, 1918. The protesters say unification with Romania is the way for corruption-ridden Moldova to move forward and join the [[European Union]]. [http://www.rferl.org/content/moldova-rally-romania-unification/27638163.html (Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty)]
