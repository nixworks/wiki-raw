*[[December 26–28, 2015 North American storm complex|2015 Missouri  flooding]]
**Record flooding along the [[Mississippi River]] and its tributaries that has closed major highways, forced evacuations, and overflowed levees, continues to threaten the [[Midwestern United States]] where at least 24 have died. The Mississippi  is expected to crest in the next few days in [[Thebes, Illinois]], at 47.5 feet, breaking the 1995 record by more than a foot and a half (46 cm). And now periods of below-freezing air will cause some flooded areas to turn icy. [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-weather-storm-idUSKBN0UC0PD20151231 (Reuters)] [http://www.accuweather.com/en/weather-news/mississippi-ohio-missouri-illinois-arkansas-major-river-flooding-challenge-records-december-january-2016/54483549 (AccuWeather)] [http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/nationworld/midwest/ct-flooding-st-louis-i-55-20151231-story.html (''Chicago Tribune'')]

;Politics and elections
*The [[Supreme Tribunal of Justice (Venezuela)|Venezuelan Supreme Court]]  bars four incoming lawmakers (three from the [[Democratic Unity Roundtable|opposition]] and one from the ruling [[Great Patriotic Pole|Socialist party]]) from taking office, putting  the opposition's two-thirds legislative "[[supermajority]]" at risk, won via a [[Venezuelan parliamentary election, 2015|landslide victory in the December 6 election]] by just one seat. The court is yet to specify the quorum for the new House, which would determine the number of seats needed for a  two-thirds majority.  The new [[National Assembly (Venezuela)|National Assembly]] convenes Tuesday.
