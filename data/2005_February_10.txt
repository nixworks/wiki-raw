* [[Balance of trade]]: The U.S. [[Department of Commerce]] states imports exceeded exports by [[US dollar|$]]670 billion for 2004, beating the previous record by nearly 25%. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/business/4254369.stm (BBC)]
* [[September 11, 2001 attacks]]: A previously unreleased portion of the [[9/11 Commission]]'s report details that the [[Federal Aviation Administration]] received 52 intelligence reports on potential [[terrorism|terrorist]] attacks by [[al-Qaeda]] before September 11, 2001. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/4255755.stm (BBC)] [https://www.nytimes.com/2005/02/10/politics/10terror.html (NY Times)]
* [[Conflict in Iraq]]:
** [[Iraq]]i officials announce they must recount around 300 ballot boxes due to various discrepancies, delaying the final results.
** In [[Salman Pak]] south of [[Baghdad]], [[guerrilla warfare|guerrillas]] attack a police station killing at least eight Iraqi policemen and wounding more than 60.
** Three people are killed when a car bomb is detonated in Baghdad.
** Also in the capital, the bodies of 20 truck drivers are found and guerrillas ambush a convoy of [[Kurd]]ish party officials, killing one and wounding four.
** In [[Basra]], guerrillas kill an Iraqi journalist working for the [[United States|U.S.]]-funded [[al-Hurra]] TV station and his 3-year-old son as they leave their home.
** A roadside bomb in [[Samarra]] leaves four policemen dead. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/4253005.stm (BBC)] [http://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory?id=486736 (ABC)]
* [[Israeli-Palestinian Conflict]]: Israel has postponed security talks with [[Palestinians]] following a [[mortar (weapon)|mortar]] attack by [[Hamas]] on the [[Gush Katif]] [[Jewish]] settlements in the [[Gaza Strip]].  [[Hamas]] claim the attack was in retaliation for an attack by [[Jew]]ish settlers which left one man dead. [[President of the Palestinian Authority|Palestinian leader]] [[Mahmoud Abbas]] responds by firing the top security commanders in the [[Gaza Strip]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/4254725.stm (BBC)] 
* [[Clarence House]] announces that [[Charles, Prince of Wales]] is to marry [[Camilla Parker Bowles]] in a civil ceremony on 8 April. When Charles becomes a [[List of British Monarchs|king]], it is intended that she be styled as a [[princess consort]], rather than a [[queen consort]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/4252795.stm (BBC)]

* [[Saudi Arabia]] starts its [[Saudi Arabia municipal elections, 2005|first nationwide municipal elections]]. Voting is limited to men in [[Riyadh]] and voters elect only half of the municipal councils; the other half is appointed by the [[monarchy]]. Other regions are scheduled to hold elections next month.
