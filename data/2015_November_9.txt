*[[2015 North Indian Ocean cyclone season]]
**[[2015 North Indian Ocean cyclone season#Extremely Severe Cyclonic Storm Megh|Cyclone Megh]]
***Cyclone Megh had sustained winds of 204 km/h (127 mph), the equivalent of a [[Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale|Category 3 hurricane]], when it hit the island of [[Socotra]]. According to [[Agence France-Presse|AFP]], Megh killed six, injured dozens with three reported missing and 5,000 people forced to leave their homes. [http://www.reuters.com/article/2015/11/08/us-yemen-cyclone-idUSKCN0SX0TD20151108#x3ZfgHQ8zQJ5rsA8.97 (Reuters)] [http://www.usatoday.com/story/weather/2015/11/08/cyclone-megh-yemen-socotra/75411872/ (USA Today)] [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/2015/11/panic-cyclone-hits-yemen-week-deadly-weather-151108081019982.html (Al Jazeera)] [http://news.yahoo.com/second-cyclone-kills-6-yemen-island-203101338.html (AFP via Yahoo)]
***Megh is expected to cross the [[Yemen]] coast Tuesday at 9:00 a.m. [[UTC+03:00#Arabia Standard Time|Arabia Standard Time]]  (6:00 a.m. UTC). In the [[Gulf of Aden]], the storm has maximum sustained winds of 111 km/h (69 mph), gusting to 130 km/h (81 mph). Its strength will significantly erode once it makes landfall and continue to decay after it moves inland into the dry and rugged terrain of Western Yemen. It is not expected to track back over water.
