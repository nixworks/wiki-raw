*[[Pollution in China]]
**The [[Government of China|Chinese government]] warns residents in [[North China|Northern China]] to prepare for a wave of choking [[smog]] arriving over the weekend and lasting through Tuesday, with [[Beijing]] affected hardest. The capital city issues its second-ever "red alert" -- the first was announced on December 7, 2015. This alert triggers the odd-even license plate system to halve cars on the road, and recommends schools close. China's [[National Meteorological Center of CMA|National Meteorological Center]] reports the pollution will stretch from [[Xi'an]], across part of [[Central China]], through Beijing and up into [[Shenyang]] and [[Harbin]] in China's frigid northeast. [http://in.reuters.com/article/us-china-pollution-idINKBN0U102Z20151218 (Reuters)] [http://www.scmp.com/news/china/policies-politics/article/1892504/airpocalypse-iii-worst-smog-year-headed-northern-china (''South China Morning Post'')]
