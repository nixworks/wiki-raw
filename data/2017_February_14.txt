;Business and economics
*[[Cigna]] announces that it has terminated its merger with [[Anthem Inc.|Anthem]], a deal that was blocked by a U.S. federal judge last week. In addition, Cigna sues  Anthem for the $1.85 billion reverse termination fee,  and for more than $13 billion in additional damages.  Anthem says Cigna has no right to terminate the merger, a deal which in January was extended to April 30, 2017. [https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-02-14/cigna-sues-anthem-after-ending-their-merger-agreement (Bloomberg)] [http://www.cnbc.com/2017/02/14/cigna-terminates-merger-with-anthem-sues-for-fee-damages.html (CNBC)]
*[[Aetna]] and [[Humana]] terminate their proposed merger that had been blocked last month by a U.S. federal judge. Aetna announces it will pay Humana  the $1 billion fee for calling off the agreement. [http://www.forbes.com/sites/brucejapsen/2017/02/14/aetna-gives-up-on-merger-will-pay-humana-1b-breakup-fee/#4b72d60b150a (''Forbes'')] [http://www.npr.org/sections/thetwo-way/2017/02/14/515167491/aetna-and-humana-call-off-merger-after-court-decision (NPR)]

*[[United States–Venezuela relations]]
**The [[United States]] sanctions [[Vice President of Venezuela]] [[Tareck El Aissami]] for allegedly "playing a significant role in international narcotics trafficking". The sanctions freeze his assets in the U.S. and bar him from entering the country. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-latin-america-38964575 (BBC)]
*[[Foreign relations of the Gambia]]
**[[United Kingdom|British]] [[Foreign Secretary of the United Kingdom|Foreign Secretary]] [[Boris Johnson]], during a visit to [[The Gambia|the Gambia]], announces that the [[West Africa]]n nation is to rejoin the [[Commonwealth of Nations]] following talks with [[President of the Gambia|Gambian President]] [[Adama Barrow]]. The Gambia withdrew from the Commonwealth in 2013 under [[Yahya Jammeh]], who considered it a "neo-colonial institution". [http://news.sky.com/story/gambia-to-rejoin-the-commonwealth-after-four-years-10767332 (Sky News)]
