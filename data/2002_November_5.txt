* Embattled [[U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission]]er [[Harvey Pitt]] tenders his resignation.
* [[Election Day (United States)|Election Day]]: In the [[United States elections, 2002|2002 midterm elections]], [[Republican Party (United States)|Republicans]] receive a net gain of two seats of the closely divided [[United States Senate|U.S. Senate]], retaking control of the body. The Republicans also retain control of the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. House of Representatives]], with a net gain of eight House seats. [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrats]] make some gains in the [[Governor (United States)|state governors']] races, with a net gain of three governorships.
* [[Prime Minister of Israel|Israeli Prime Minister]] [[Ariel Sharon]] dissolves the [[Knesset]] and calls for [[Israeli legislative election, 2003|elections early the next year]].
* [[Conservative Party (UK)|UK Conservative Party]] leader [[Iain Duncan Smith]] made what was widely considered to be a disastrous speech where he demanded that his party "unite or die"; observers believe that this marks the start of a new leadership struggle within the Conservative Party.
