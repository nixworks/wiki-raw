;International relations
*[[Positions on Jerusalem]]
**[[U.S. President]] [[Donald Trump]] recognizes Jerusalem as the capital of Israel and says the U.S. starts the process of moving its embassy from Tel Aviv to Jerusalem. The U.S. State Department issues an internal travel warning to Israel, Jerusalem and the West Bank until December 20. The U.S. embassy in [[Amman]] temporarily suspends routine public services and stops embassy travel around [[Jordan]]. Embassies in Berlin and London issue vigilance warnings to U.S. citizens. [http://edition.cnn.com/2017/12/06/politics/president-donald-trump-jerusalem/index.html (CNN)] [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-trump-israel-cable/u-s-asks-officials-to-defer-travel-to-israel-west-bank-to-december-20-idUSKBN1E02HU (Reuters)] [http://www.bbc.com/news/live/world-middle-east-42255592 (BBC)]
***[[President of the State of Palestine]] [[Mahmoud Abbas]] says the move undermines the U.S. position as a peace mediator. He repeats that Jerusalem is the "eternal capital of the state of Palestine". [[Hamas]] says that the decision would "open the gates of hell on US interests in the region." [http://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-42250340 (BBC)]
***The [[Arab League]] calls it "a dangerous measure that would have repercussions" and also questions the future role of the US as a "trusted mediator" in peace talks. [http://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-42250340 (BBC)]
***Protests against the decision break out in Jordan, Palestine and Turkey. [http://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-42259443 (BBC)], [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-trump-israel/trump-recognizes-jerusalem-as-israels-capital-defying-allies-and-foes-idUSKBN1E01PS (Reuters)]
***Israeli Prime Minister [[Benjamin Netanyahu]] praises the announcement as a “historic landmark.” This backs an [[Land of Israel|Israel claim]] of controlling the entire city as its indivisible capital. The move of the [[United States]] embassy is the first among nations in history. [http://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-42246564 (BBC)][http://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-42250340 (BBC)]
***The decision draws critical reaction from key US allies that include the EU, France, Germany and the United Kingdom. The [[United Kingdom]] had warned it was time for the U.S. to present their new [[List of Middle East peace proposals|peace plan for the region]]. [http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/nationworld/ct-jerusalem-israel-capital-declaration-20171206-story.html (''Chicago Tribune'')][http://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-42250340 (BBC)]
***Already before the announcement, [[Pope Francis]] urged respect for the [[status quo]] for Jerusalem and called for "[[wisdom]] and [[prudence]]" to avoid further conflict. [http://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/pope-francis-urges-status-quo-jerusalem-calls-wisdom-51608418 (ABC News)]
***[[Secretary-General of the United Nations]] [[António Guterres]] criticizes the announcement, saying that it "would jeopardise the prospect of peace for Israelis and Palestinians".[http://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-42259443 (BBC)]
***8 of the 15 current [[United Nations  Security Council]] members call for the body to hold an urgent meeting on the US decision. The [[Sweden|Swedish]] mission wants a meeting by the end of the week. [[Japan]], which holds the current presidency, did not yet set out a time. [http://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-42259443 (BBC)] [https://www.enca.com/world/eight-nations-seek-security-council-meeting-on-jerusalem (eNCA)]
