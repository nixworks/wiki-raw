*In the [[United States]], the [[California]] Labor Commission ruled that a San Francisco [[Uber (company)|Uber]] driver is a company employee, not a contractor. Uber is appealing this ruling, that would increase the company's costs and liabilities, to the state's [[California courts of appeal|court]] system. A hearing for a class certification of a similar lawsuit against Uber is scheduled for August 2015. [http://www.npr.org/sections/thetwo-way/2015/06/17/415262801/california-labor-commission-rules-uber-driver-is-an-employee-not-a-contractor (NPR)], [http://time.com/3924941/uber-california-labor-commission-ruling/ (Time Magazine)] [http://www.usnews.com/news/articles/2015/06/18/employee-or-contractor-uber-ruling-could-affect-other-companies?google_editors_picks=true (U.S. News & World Report)]
