* A powerful [[car bomb]] [[explosion|explodes]] outside the Marriott Hotel,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marriott.com/dpp/PropertyPage.asp?MarshaCode=AMMJR |title=Hotels Amman - Hotels in Amman &#124; Amman Marriott Hotel |publisher=Marriott.com |date= |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref> killing at least fourteen people and injuring about 150 in downtown [[Jakarta]], a popular district for foreigners. It is believed to be a [[suicide bombing]]. The blast comes two days before a [[Bali]] court was due to deliver the verdict of the first suspect of the [[2002 Bali terrorist bombing]] and four days after President [[Megawati Sukarnoputri]] vowed to wipe out [[terrorism|terrorist]] networks in [[Indonesia]]. [[Jemaah Islamiyah]] claimed responsibility for the attack through a [[Singapore]] newspaper.<ref>[http://straitstimes.asia1.com.sg/topstories/story/0,4386,203327,00.html ]{{dead link|date=September 2015}}</ref><ref>[http://apnews.myway.com/article/20030806/D7SOB9NG0.html] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20030826131012/http://apnews.myway.com/article/20030806/D7SOB9NG0.html |date=August 26, 2003 }}</ref>
* A planned meeting between [[Israel]]i prime minister [[Ariel Sharon]] and [[Palestinian people|Palestinian]] prime minister [[Mahmoud Abbas]] is cancelled by Abbas. He accuses Israel of not doing enough in a US-backed [[road map for peace]]. Israel had said that 540 [[Palestinian prisoners]] would be freed but only released 342 names in a prisoner list. Israel accuses the Palestinians of not curbing terrorist attacks on Israel.
* A further twist to the British [[David Kelly (weapons expert)|David Kelly]] scandal occurs, as [[Tony Blair]]'s official spokesman, Tom Kelly, apologizes to David Kelly's family for having compared the late and still un-buried Dr. Kelly to a "[[The Secret Life of Walter Mitty|Walter Mitty]]" character in a "private" conversation with a journalist.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/3124677.stm |title=UK &#124; Politics &#124; Blair under pressure over Mitty remark |publisher=BBC News |date=2003-08-05 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Matthew Tempest and Julia Day |url=http://politics.guardian.co.uk/kelly/story/0,13747,1012664,00.html |title=No 10 apology for Kelly blunder &#124; Politics &#124; The Guardian |publisher=Politics.guardian.co.uk |date= |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
* [[Nova Scotia general election, 2003|Election in Nova Scotia, Canada]]: the [[Progressive Conservative Party of Nova Scotia|Progressive Conservative Party]] of Premier [[John Hamm]] is reelected with a minority government. They receive 25 seats, the [[New Democratic Party of Nova Scotia|New Democratic Party]] 15, and the [[Liberal Party of Nova Scotia|Liberals]] 12.
* [[Episcopal Church in the United States of America|The Episcopal Church]], the U.S. branch of the [[Anglican Communion]], approves its first openly [[gay]] [[bishop]] as the final vote was cast to confirm [[Gene Robinson]] as the [[Diocese]] of [[New Hampshire]]. Robinson was cleared of allegations of misconduct before the vote. The action incites protests, a declaration of a "pastoral emergency", and calls for intervention by the Anglican Communion chief bishops.<ref>[http://apnews.myway.com/article/20030806/D7SOAJ9G0.html]{{dead link|date=September 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/0,2933,93903,00.html |title=Gay Bishop Says Confirmation Shouldn't Split Church |publisher=Fox News |date=2003-08-06 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>[http://www.voanews.com/article.cfm?objectID=7281F6FF-B2B9-49B3-86EE432F76D63123 ]{{dead link|date=September 2015}}</ref>
