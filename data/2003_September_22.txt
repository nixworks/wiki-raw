* A [[suicide attack]]er detonates a [[car bomb]] near [[United Nations]] headquarters in [[Baghdad]], killing himself and an Iraqi guard and injuring at least 11 others. The attack came a month after a massive truck bomb devastated the complex and as the UN considers expanding its role in Iraq. [[Secretary-General of the United Nations|UN Secretary-General]] [[Kofi Annan]] states that personnel are assessing the situation following the attack.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2003/sep/22/world/fg-iraq22|title=Bomb Near U.N. Base in Baghdad Kills Iraqi|work=latimes|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.un.org/apps/news/story.asp?NewsID=8316&Cr=iraq&Cr1=|title=United Nations News Centre|date=22 September 2003|work=UN News Service Section|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* 90% of the delegates elected to the November 15 convention of the [[Liberal Party of Canada]] support [[Paul Martin]], thereby assuring that he will win the leadership of the party and thereby become [[Prime Minister of Canada]] after [[Jean Chrétien]] retires in February 2004.
* The [[Scientific journal|journal]]'' [[Science (journal)|Science]]'' publishes an article on a [[Human jawbone|jawbone]] of an [[Anatomically modern humans|early modern human]] found in 2002 near the entrance of the Pestera cu Oase (cave with bones) in the southwestern [[Carpathian Mountains]] in [[Romania]]. [[Radiocarbon dating]] places its age at between 34,000 and 36,000 years, making it the oldest [[fossil]] from an early modern human. The jawbone suggests interbreeding between modern humans and [[Neanderthal]]s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/science/articles/2003/09/24/952446.htm|title=Euro jawbone rattles Neanderthal debate|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
