*[[2017 Qatar diplomatic crisis]]
**[[Bahrain]], [[Egypt]], the [[Maldives]], [[Saudi Arabia]], the [[United Arab Emirates]], and the internationally recognized government of [[Yemen]] sever diplomatic ties with [[Qatar]], ordering Qatari citizens in those countries to be expelled as well as cutting all land, air and sea connections. "National security," "media incitement" and Qatar's support of [[Iran]] are variously cited. [https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/jun/05/saudi-arabia-and-bahrain-break-diplomatic-ties-with-qatar-over-terrorism (''The Guardian'')] [https://apnews.com/8257ce650e224188a1884e34eabb5e90/4-Arab-nations-cut-diplomatic-ties-to-Qatar-as-rift-deepens (AP)]

*[[2016–17 Turkish purges]]
**The [[Ministry of the Interior (Turkey)|Turkish interior ministry]] announces that 130 people, living outside the country and suspected of militant links, will lose their  [[Turkish nationality law|citizenship]] unless they return to Turkey within three months and meet government standards. Named suspects include  U.S.-based cleric [[Fethullah Gülen]], and [[Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)|Peoples' Democratic Party]] leaders Faysal Sariyildiz, Tuğba Hezer, and  Özdal Üçer. [http://www.reuters.com/article/us-turkey-security-citizenship-idUSKBN18W17D (Reuters)]
