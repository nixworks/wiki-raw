* Two [[explosion]]s, apparently caused by [[car bomb]]s placed in taxis, kills at least 44 and injures a further 150 in [[Mumbai]], India. It is the sixth bombing in Mumbai in a year.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/south_asia/3178893.stm |title=South Asia &#124; Twin blasts cause Bombay carnage |publisher=BBC News |date=2003-08-25 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
* In Australia's [[One Nation Party]] case, it has been revealed that [[Federation|Federal]] [[Cabinet (government)|Cabinet]] [[Political minister|Minister]] [[Tony Abbott]] controlled "[[slush fund]]s" which were used to lay the groundwork for party leader [[Pauline Hanson]]'s prosecution, and to guarantee a private [[lawsuit]] against the party.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/articles/2003/08/25/1061663736939.html |title=Abbott set up slush fund to ruin Hanson - National |publisher=smh.com.au |date=2003-08-26 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref> [[Liberal Party of Australia|Liberal]] ranks split as Abbot's colleague [[Bronwyn Bishop]] joined many [[Australian Labor Party|Labor]] [[Member of Parliament|MPs]] in calling for disclosure of his role in the case, and described Hanson as a "[[political prisoner]]". Remarkably, the nearly defunct One Nation Party's support surged to 21% on news of Hanson's imprisonment.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/articles/2003/08/25/1061663736886.html |title=Viva free speech as Bishop joins battler's bandwagon - National |publisher=smh.com.au |date=2003-08-26 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
