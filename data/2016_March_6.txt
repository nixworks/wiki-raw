*[[Slovak parliamentary election, 2016]]
**[[Prime Minister of Slovakia|Prime Minister]] [[Robert Fico|Robert Fico's]]  [[Direction – Social Democracy|Smer party]] won 28.3 percent of the vote, but with just 49 seats, lost its majority  in the 150-member parliament. The [[Freedom and Solidarity]] party  won 21 seats (12.1%), and the [[Ordinary People (Slovakia)|Ordinary People]] party took 19 seats (11.0%). [http://www.dailysabah.com/europe/2016/03/07/anti-migrant-fico-wins-slovakias-election-neo-nazis-gain-seats  (AP via ''Daily Sabah'')]

[https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/slovakia-in-shock-over-far-right-partys-parliament-election-success/2016/03/06/658b1a72-e3d5-11e5-bc08-3e03a5b41910_story.html (''The Washington Post'')] [http://www.independent.ie/world-news/europe/surprise-wins-for-far-right-in-slovakia-34516970.html (''Irish Independent'')]

Fico's rhetoric strengthened the right in Slovakia."
