;International relations
*[[2014 pro-Russian unrest in Ukraine]]:
**[[Russia]] and [[Ukraine]] together with the [[United States]] and the [[European Union]] sit down for emergency talks in [[Geneva]]. All four agree that "illegal military formations in Ukraine" must be dissolved, and that everyone occupying buildings must be disarmed and leave them. There would be an amnesty for all anti-government protesters under the agreement. [https://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/apr/17/ukraine-crisis-agreement-us-russia-eu (''The Guardian'')] [http://www.skynews.com.au/topstories/article.aspx?id=968581&vId= (Sky News Australia)]
**A skirmish on the Ukrainian military base in [[Mariupol]] by pro-Russian militants results in the deaths of 3 of the militants, the wounding of another 13, and the capture of 63 others. [http://www.cnn.com/2014/04/17/world/europe/ukraine-crisis/index.html?hpt=wo_c1 (BBC)]
**The [[Ukrainian Army]] unit whose armoured vehicles were seized by pro-Russian forces is disbanded and its members charged with crimes. [http://un.ua/eng/article/504866.html (Ukrainian News Agency)]
**[[President of Russia|Russian President]] [[Vladimir Putin]] admits having sent Russian forces to [[Crimea]] last month to protect its military equipment there. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/putin-changes-course-admits-russian-troops-were-in-crimea-before-vote/2014/04/17/b3300a54-c617-11e3-bf7a-be01a9b69cf1_story.html (''Washington Post'')] 
**Jews in Donetsk are ordered to register with the government of the self-proclaimed [[Donetsk People's Republic]] via leaflets [[Donetsk People's Republic#Anti-semitic leaflets|dropped]] by an unknown party. The Jewish community in Donetsk dismissed it as a "provocation". [https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/world/2014/04/17/jews-ordered-to-register-in-east-ukraine/7816951/ (''USA Today'')][http://www.cnn.com/2014/04/17/world/ukraine-religious-threats/ (CNN)]
