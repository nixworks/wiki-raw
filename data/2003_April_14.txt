* [[Boutros Boutros-Ghali]], former [[United Nations Secretary General]], reports that the [[United Nations]] is increasingly "being marginalised" and that preparation is needed for a new organisation to succeed the UN.
* U.S. President [[George W. Bush]] says that U.S.-led coalition victory in the [[2003 invasion of Iraq|2003 Iraq war]] is "certain, but not complete."<ref>[http://customwire.ap.org/dynamic/stories/W/WAR_US_MILITARY?SITE=DCTMS&SECTION=HOME&TEMPLATE=DEFAULT]{{dead link|date=September 2015}}</ref>
* In [[Quebec]], Canada, the governing [[Quebec sovereignty movement|sovereigntist]] ''[[Parti Québécois]]'' is defeated in the [[Quebec general election, 2003|2003 general election]]. The [[Parti libéral du Québec|Liberals]] are returned to power after nine years, and [[Jean Charest]] becomes the new [[List of Quebec premiers|premier]].
* The [[Congregation for the Causes of Saints]] in the [[Vatican City|Vatican]], in the presence of [[Pope John Paul&nbsp;II]], promulgates a decree declaring that Emperor-King [[Karl of Austria]]-Hungary ([[Reign|r]]: 1916–1918) possessed "heroic virtues". This decree marks a significant step towards canonisation in the Roman Catholic Church for the last Austrian emperor and king of Hungary.
* The bodies of a headless woman and a newborn [[fetus]] with the [[umbilical cord]] still attached washed up separately on the shore of [[San Francisco Bay]] near [[Richmond, California|Richmond]], California.  [[DNA]] testing determined the body is that of [[Laci Peterson]], who had been missing from her home in [[Modesto, California|Modesto]] since December 24, 2002.
