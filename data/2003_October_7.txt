* [[2003 California recall|California recall]]:  The state of California held a special election to decide whether to recall [[Governor]] [[Gray Davis]], and, if so, who to replace him with.  Also on the ballot: [[California Proposition 53 (2003)|Proposition 53]], the "California Twenty-First Century Infrastructure Investment Fund," and [[California Proposition 54 (2003)|Proposition 54]], the "Racial Privacy Initiative."
* The [[Nobel Prize in Physics]] is awarded jointly to [[Alexei Alexeyevich Abrikosov|Alexei Abrikosov]], [[Vitaly Ginzburg]] and [[Anthony Leggett]] for their work on the theory of [[superconductivity|superconductors]] and [[superfluid]]s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nobel.se/physics/laureates/2003/index.html|title=The Nobel Prize in Physics 2003|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[United Nations]] envoy and Special Coordinator for the [[Middle East peace process]], [[Terje Roed-Larsen]], condemns attack from [[Lebanese territory]] that killed an [[Israel]]i soldier across the southern withdrawal line and urges [[Beirut]] to control the use of force everywhere in its jurisdiction. Roed-Larsen, states the attack "constitutes a clear violation of the Blue Line and Security Council resolutions and could escalate tension between Israel and its northern neighbours" and he calls on all sides to use [[diplomacy]] and take no action that "could increase the already high level of tension in the region".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.un.org/apps/news/story.asp?NewsID=8474&Cr=middle&Cr1=east|title=United Nations News Centre|date=7 October 2003|work=UN News Service Section|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* UN spokesman states that a [[peacekeeping operation]] in the [[Democratic Republic of the Congo]] (DRC) has discovered 65 bodies, mostly children, apparently massacred.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.un.org/apps/news/story.asp?NewsID=8472&Cr=DR&Cr1=Congo|title=United Nations News Centre|date=7 October 2003|work=UN News Service Section|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[American occupation of Iraq|Occupation of Iraq]]: The [[Parliament of Turkey|Turkish Parliament]] votes (358-to-183) to approve the dispatch of [[peacekeeping|peacekeepers]] to [[Iraq]], in a major victory for United States efforts to broaden foreign involvement in Iraq. In [[Baghdad]], [[Iraqi Governing Council]] officials state that they would oppose any new foreign troop deployment to Iraq. No formal decision had been made by the Council and leaders of the council have stated they would support this if the United States requested this.<ref>[http://www.iht.com/articles/112829.html] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050312185850/http://www.iht.com/articles/112829.html |date=March 12, 2005 }}</ref>
* The [[South Africa]]n government announce they would not prosecute the five policemen accused of killing [[Steve Biko]] in 1977, citing insufficient evidence to support a murder charge.<ref>[http://www.news24.com/News24/South_Africa/News/0,,2-7-1442_1426929,00.html] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080105160806/http://www.news24.com/News24/South_Africa/News/0,,2-7-1442_1426929,00.html |date=January 5, 2008 }}</ref>
* The [[Association of Southeast Asian Nations]] (ASEAN) announces its intention to form a single-market "Asian Community" by 2020.
