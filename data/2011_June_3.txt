*[[2011 Yemeni uprising|Yemeni uprising]]:
**A rocket attack on the presidential palace in [[Sana'a]] during the [[Battle of Sana'a (2011)|Battle of Sana'a]] leaves president [[Ali Abdullah Saleh]] lightly injured and seven other top government officials wounded, including Prime Minister [[Ali Muhammad Mujawar]]. Four presidential guards and Sheikh Ali Mohsen al-Matari, an imam at the mosque, were killed. [http://www.cnn.com/2011/WORLD/meast/06/03/yemen.unrest/index.html?section=cnn_latest (CNN)] [http://www.deseretnews.com/mobile/article/700141205/Yemen-president-wounded-as-tribesmen-strike-palace.html (Deseret News)]
*[[2011 Syrian uprising|Syrian uprising]]:
**At least 34 more protesters are killed by [[Syrian army|Syrian security forces]] in protest marches dedicated to children who have died during the demonstrations. [http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/middleeast/syria/8555576/Syria-bloody-protests-over-the-slaying-of-30-children.html (The Telegraph)] [https://www.nytimes.com/2011/06/04/world/middleeast/04syria.html?_r=1 (''The New York Times'')]

*[[2011 Libyan Civil War|Libyan Civil War]]:
**At least 150 people drown when an overcrowded boat carrying about 850 refugees fleeing [[Libya]] capsized off the [[Tunisia]]n coast; scores more are still missing as a search for survivors by Tunisian authorities continues. [http://www.cnn.com/2011/WORLD/africa/06/03/libya.migrants/index.html (CNN)]
*[[Sudanese army|Sudanese government troops]] and [[Sudan Liberation Army]] rebels [[War in Darfur|clash]] in the western [[Darfur]] region; two SLA rebels are reported killed. [http://af.reuters.com/article/topNews/idAFJOE75203A20110603 (Reuters)] 
*More than 200 [[Afghanistan|Afghan]] militants cross over into [[Pakistan]] and attack a [[security checkpoint]] in [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa|northwest Pakistan]], sparking a clash in which 28 Pakistani security forces and six civilians die, along with 45 Afghans. [http://www.cnn.com/2011/WORLD/asiapcf/06/02/pakistan.militant.attack/index.html (CNN)]
*[[Somalia|Somali]] government forces and [[African Union]] troops expel [[al Qaeda]]-linked militants from their last outpost in the western part of [[Mogadishu]], opening up a main road in the city. [http://www.cnn.com/2011/WORLD/africa/06/03/somalia.fighting/index.html (CNN)]
*In two bombing attacks in [[Iraq]], 17 people are killed and 50 others wounded in a blast outside of the Presidential Palaces Mosque in central Tikrit, Iraq; and later by a [[suicide bomber]] in a [[Tikrit]] hospital treating the wounded from the first attack, killing six people and injuring ten at the hospital. [http://www.cnn.com/2011/WORLD/meast/06/03/iraq.violence/index.html (CNN)]
*[[2011 Egyptian revolution]]: A female journalist is attacked in [[Cairo]]'s [[Tahrir Square]] while covering a demonstration demanding an immediate trial for former [[President of Egypt]] [[Hosni Mubarak]]. [http://www.en.rian.ru/world/20110604/164432001.html (RIA Novosti)]
