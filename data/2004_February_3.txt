* [[Israeli Army]] Chief of Personnel [[Aluf|Major-General]] Gil Regev tells a [[Knesset]] committee that the number of soldiers [[Refusal to serve in the Israeli military|refusing to serve]] in the territories had dramatically decreased in 2003.  He said that 26 persons had been imprisoned for refusal in 2003 compared to 129 in 2002, a decrease of 80%. The refusers' organization [[Yesh G'vul]] claimed that Regev's figures were "ridiculous" since 76 persons had been imprisoned for refusal in 2003.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.haaretzdaily.com/hasen/pages/ShArt.jhtml?itemNo=390329|title=The Truth about Income Inequality|work=haaretzdaily.com|accessdate=6 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.haaretzdaily.com/hasen/pages/ShArt.jhtml?itemNo=390419|title=The Truth about Income Inequality|work=haaretzdaily.com|accessdate=6 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.haaretzdaily.com/hasen/pages/ShArt.jhtml?itemNo=390681|title=The Truth about Income Inequality|work=haaretzdaily.com|accessdate=6 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs|British Foreign Secretary]] [[Jack Straw]] announces an independent inquiry, to be chaired by [[Robin Butler, Baron Butler of Brockwell|Lord Butler of Brockwell]], to examine the reliability of intelligence on [[Iraq and weapons of mass destruction|weapons of mass destruction in Iraq]]. His report, the [[Butler Review]], is published on July 14.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/3453305.stm | work=BBC News | title=Iraq WMD inquiry details unveiled | date=February 3, 2004}}</ref>

* [[Political status of Taiwan]]: [[President of the Republic of China|President]] [[Chen Shui-bian]] proposes to set up a [[demilitarized zone]] between [[Taiwan]] and [[mainland China]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/3453631.stm | work=BBC News | title=Taiwan seeks demilitarised zone | date=February 3, 2004}}</ref>
* [[Mini-Tuesday]]: Five [[U.S. state|states]] hold [[United States presidential primary|primaries]] and two states hold [[caucus]]es for the [[Democratic Party (United States) presidential primaries, 2004|Democratic presidential nomination]]. [[United States Senate|U.S. Senator]] [[John Kerry]] of [[Massachusetts]] wins primary elections in [[Arizona]], [[Delaware]], [[New Mexico]], [[Missouri]] and [[North Dakota]], which a best result is 51 percent in Missouri. Senator [[John Edwards]] of [[North Carolina]] wins in [[South Carolina]]. Retired [[United States Army|U.S. Army]] [[General (United States)|General]] [[Wesley Clark]] narrowly wins in [[Oklahoma]]. Former [[Governor of Vermont|Vermont Governor]] [[Howard Dean]], who opted to focus on the [[Michigan]] and [[Washington (state)|Washington]] primaries the following Saturday, polls poorly in all these primaries, with a best result is third with 18% in New Mexico. For the first time, Kerry obtains a majority of elected delegates to the [[2004 Democratic National Convention]], with 244 delegates to Dean's 121, Edwards' 102 and Clark's 79. Senator [[Joseph Lieberman]] of [[Connecticut]], having earlier announced "[[Joementum]]", comes in second in [[Delaware]] and announces his withdrawal from the race.
* Nine [[Cuba]]n [[immigrant|migrant]]s attempt to reach [[Florida]] in a modified 1959 [[Buick]] that was converted to a boat.  They are intercepted by the [[United States Coast Guard]].  The same group had made a similar attempt the summer before in a modified 1951 [[Chevrolet]] [[truck]].
