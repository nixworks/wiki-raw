* [[North Korea]] announces re-election of dictator [[Kim Jong-il]] as chairman of the National Defense Commission by a unanimous vote of the [[Supreme People's Assembly]], a move dismissed as a [[propaganda]] stunt by [[Western countries|Western]] observers, who nearly all regard the Supreme People's Assembly as a [[rubber stamp]] body.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.twincities.com/mld/twincities/news/world/6678039.htm|title=Twin Cities News, Sports, Weather, Traffic|work=TwinCities.com|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[California]] legislature passes expanded [[domestic partner]]ship bill. The state assembly approved a measure to extend nearly all the legal rights of married couples to people in same-sex partnerships. If signed by the governor, the bill will become law in 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nbc11.com/politics/2453726/detail.html|title=Bay Area News, Local News, Weather, Traffic, Entertainment, Breaking News|work=NBC Bay Area|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* The [[right wing]] [[British National Party]] (BNP) candidate Nicholas Geri, who is of Italian descent, wins a surprise victory in a local government [[by-election]] to [[Thurrock]] Borough Council in [[Essex, England|Essex]]. The [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]], which has a 21-seat majority on the Council, sees its candidate pushed into third place, behind the BNP and the [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]]. Turnout in the by-election was 22%.<ref>[http://www.sky.com/skynews/article/0,,30100-12761138,00.html ]{{dead link|date=October 2015}}</ref>
* [[Singapore]] drops its 21-year ban on ''[[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]]'' magazine and slightly relaxes its film [[censorship]] policy. Despite this move, the censorship board's surveyors found the Singaporean public largely does not want the country's tough censorship rules liberalized.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=638&ncid=638&e=1&u=/nm/20030904/en_nm/singapore_censorship_dc|title=Yahoo News - Latest News & Headlines|work=Yahoo News|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[Natural disaster]]: The Booth and Bear Butte [[forest fire]]s in the [[Cascade Range|Cascade Mountains]], which had been 45% contained, explodes to burn an additional 20,000 acres (80&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>). Estimates of the size of this fire vary between 62,000 and 80,000 acres (250 and 320&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>). The resort community of [[Camp Sherman, Oregon|Camp Sherman]], where authorities allowed residents to return, is once again evacuated.<ref>[http://www.bendbulletin.com/news/story.cfm?story_no=10649 ]{{dead link|date=October 2015}}</ref>
