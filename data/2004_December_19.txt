* [[Conflict in Iraq]]: Bomb blasts in the Iraqi [[Shia]] cities of [[Karbala]] and [[Najaf]] kill up to 60 people and injure a further 120. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/middle_east/4109227.stm (BBC)]
* Russia auctions off the main production unit of oil giant [[YUKOS]] to the small [[Baikalfinansgroup]] for 260.75bn [[Russian ruble|rubles]] ([[USD|US$]]9.37bn). Before the sale, YUKOS was said to owe US$27bn in unpaid taxes, specifically an average of 90% of its revenue. Former CEO jailed [[Mikhail Khodorkovsky]] and other YUKOS officials maintain it is politically motivated. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/4108509.stm (BBC)]
* The people of [[Turkmenistan]] go to the polls to elect a new [[Parliament of Turkmenistan|parliament]]. Voters will choose between candidates, all of whom have pledged support to President [[Saparmurat Niyazov|Niyazov]], the "Turkmenbashi" or "leader of Turkmens". [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/asia-pacific/4108659.stm (BBC)]
