* An Italian intelligence report states that Italy is a departure point, as well as focus of logistic and financial support, for [[suicide bombers]] linked to [[al-Qaida]] and active against United States-led forces in [[Iraq]]. * The oil cartel [[OPEC]] announces further limits on the output of [[crude oil|crude]] by one million barrels a day beginning April 1, 2004. * Recent violence in [[Haiti]] has spread as anti-government forces take control of eight towns in Western Haiti. 46 people are dead thus far. Government forces in [[Cap-Haïtien]] (second largest city in Haiti) built flaming barricades to keep the rebel forces out of the city. The [[United Nations]] urges Haitians on both sides to stop the violence.<ref>[http://abcnews.go.com/wire/World/ap20040210_619.html] {{dead link|date=October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2004/02/10/world/main599197.shtml | work=CBS News | title=Violence Sweeps Haiti | date=February 10, 2004}}</ref>

* 2004 [[Philippines|Philippine]] elections: The 90-day campaigning period for the [[President of the Philippines|president]], vice-president, and [[Senate of the Philippines|senators]] starts this day with no less than six qualified candidates, half of which have no previous political experience. The current president, [[Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo]] is seeking a full six-year term. The elections will be held on May 10.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/asia-pacific/3472269.stm | work=BBC News | first=Sarah | last=Toms | title=Philippine presidency race begins | date=February 9, 2004}}</ref>
