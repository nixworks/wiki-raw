* [[American occupation of Iraq|Occupation of Iraq]]: US Congress allocates {{Nowrap|$87 billion}} for occupation and reconstruction of Iraq and Afghanistan. The funding bill omits a provision included in the [[United States Senate|Senate]] version of the bill, demanding that Iraq repay some of the {{Nowrap|$20 billion}} of the funds dedicated for rebuilding. U.S. President Bush had been strongly opposed to this provision.
* At a campaign fundraiser in [[Birmingham, Alabama|Birmingham]], Alabama, President [[George W. Bush]] states that the [[tax cut]]s are working to help the economy. Bush also vows that the coalition forces will stay in [[Iraq]]. The president states the deaths of 15 soldiers in an attack on a [[helicopter]] will not deter the United States. Bush states, "The enemy in Iraq believes America will run. That's why they're willing to kill innocent [[civilian]]s, [[relief worker]]s, coalition troops. America will never run."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.al.com/news/huntsvilletimes/index.ssf?/base/news/1067885186126782.xml |title=Huntsville, Alabama Real-Time News |publisher=al.com |date= |accessdate=2015-10-24}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/americas/3238459.stm |title=Americas &#124; Bush undeterred by Iraq violence |publisher=BBC News |date=2003-11-03 |accessdate=2015-10-24}}</ref>
* [[American occupation of Iraq|Occupation of Iraq]]: Attacks consisting of six explosions, reportedly coordinated, occur (one in [[Kirkuk]], five in [[Baghdad]]). The series of explosions in Baghdad, which may have come from [[Mortar (weapon)|mortar]] shells, is in an area that is home to several coalition headquarters buildings. The Kirkuk [[bomb]] blast northeast of Baghdad kills one Iraqi and wounds 15. The target of this explosion was the [[deputy governor]] of the northern [[Diyala Governorate|Diyala]] province [[Aqil al-Hamid]], who was in a [[convoy]] driving near the city of [[Baquba]]. He escapes uninjured. Also, another blast occurs near a holy [[Shiite]] Muslim [[shrine]] in the city of [[Karbala]] kills three people and injured 12.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/0,2933,102051,00.html |title=Series of Blasts Heard in Baghdad |publisher=Fox News |date=2003-11-03 |accessdate=2015-10-24}}</ref><ref>[http://english.aljazeera.net/NR/exeres/AD3C25C9-F2C0-4214-82F8-16CC4EB10439.htm ]{{dead link|date=October 2015}}</ref>
* The [[European Commission]] comes out with another [[Eurobarometer]], a survey of [[European Union|EU]] citizens. According to the survey, most Europeans think that the war in [[Iraq]] is not justified, that [[UN]] should supervise Iraq and provide security, and that U.S. should pay for the rebuilding of Iraq. As to which countries pose a threat to world peace, 59% think it is [[Israel]], and 53% that it is the United States.<ref>http://gandalf.ics.uci.edu/blog/2003/11/eurobarometer_iraq_and_peace_in_the_world.html</ref>
