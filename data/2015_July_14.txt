*[[global warming|Recent climate change]] would still cause a 6-meter (20-feet) [[sea-level rise]] even if [[greenhouse gas]] emissions were reduced today per a scientific paper in ''[[Science (magazine)|Science]]''.[http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20150713/environment/rising-oceans-impact-enormous.576417 (''Times of Malta'')], [http://www.sciencemag.org/content/349/6244/aaa4019 (''Science'')]
*[[NASA]]'s ''[[New Horizons]]'' spacecraft completes a historic flyby of the [[dwarf planet]] [[Pluto]] and its [[Moons of Pluto|five moons]]. Measurements, along with many high-quality photos and reams of data, were taken, including conclusive information regarding the diameter of Pluto and its moons.[http://www.gizmag.com/new-horizons-flyby-coverage/38363/ (Gizmodo)] [http://www.cnn.com/2015/07/14/us/nasa-new-horizons-pluto-flyby/index.html (CNN)] [http://solarsystem.nasa.gov/missions/profile.cfm?Sort=Alpha&Letter=N&Alias=New%20Horizons (official NASA page)]
*Scientists at [[Northumbria University]] in northeast [[England]] develop a computerized statistical model of the [[Sun]]'s [[solar cycle]] (a measurement of the number of sunspots and solar flares, in this case between 1976 and 2008, and their intensity and activity). While preliminary, and not yet peer-reviewed, the data predicts irregularities in the cycle: the Northern Hemisphere could experience a slight lowering in temperatures in the 2030s. [http://www.cnn.com/2015/07/13/world/sun-irregular-heartbeat-ice/index.html (CNN)] [https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/energy-environment/wp/2015/07/14/no-earth-is-not-heading-toward-a-mini-ice-age/ (Washington Post)]
