*[[Monetary policy of the United States]]
**Financial analysts interpret remarks by [[United States Secretary of the Treasury]] [[Steven Mnuchin]] as an indication that the [[United States]] may change the [[strong dollar policy]] that it has held since the 1990s. This is evidenced by the fact that he did not deliver the usual message that a strong dollar is good for America. [https://www.cnbc.com/2018/01/25/us-dollar-slammed-as-mnuchin-abandons-strong-dollar-mantra.html (CNBC)]
**[[Mario Draghi]], the [[President of the European Central Bank]], says that Mnuchin's comments are contrary to a decades-old agreement not to "target" each other's exchange rate. The [[European Central Bank]] keeps its policy unchanged for now despite citing the euro's surge as a source of uncertainty with respect to its [[Monetary hegemony|strong currency]] policy. [https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-ecb-policy/ecb-hits-out-at-washington-for-talking-down-the-dollar-idUKKBN1FD38G (Reuters)]

*[[Time in Florida]]
**Two bills are unanimously approved by the [[Florida House of Representatives|Florida House]] and [[Florida Senate|Senate]] Committees that propose moving [[Florida|the state]] permanently to [[Daylight saving time|Daylight Saving Time]] ([[UTC−04:00]]), and shifting most of the [[Florida Panhandle|Panhandle]] west of a line from eastern [[Jackson County, Florida|Jackson County]] to northern [[Gulf County, Florida|Gulf County]] – which currently lies within the southern boundary of the [[Central Time Zone]] ([[UTC−05:00]]) – to the [[Eastern Time Zone]], in alignment with the remainder of Florida's 67 counties. If passed by the [[Florida Legislature|state legislature]], any changes would need to be approved by the [[United States Department of Transportation|Department of Transportation]] and [[United States Congress|Congress]]. *[[Doomsday Clock]]
**The [[Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists]] moves the [[End time|Doomsday]] Clock thirty seconds forward, to two minutes before midnight, the closest setting since 1953. The journal cites the closer approach to the point of hypothetical [[global catastrophe]] to the failure of world leaders to deal with looming threats of [[nuclear warfare|nuclear war]] (particularly, [[2017–18 North Korea crisis|tensions]] involving the [[North Korea]]n nuclear program) and [[climate change]]. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/speaking-of-science/wp/2018/01/25/after-a-missile-scare-and-insult-war-with-north-korea-its-time-to-check-the-doomsday-clock/ (''The Washington Post'')] [https://motherboard.vice.com/en_us/article/9kn4ja/the-doomsday-clock-people-are-freaking-me-out (''Vice Motherboard'')]
