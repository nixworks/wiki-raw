;Law and crime
*A [[London]]-based trader who traded on the [[Chicago Mercantile Exchange]], [[2010 Flash Crash#Evidence of market manipulation and arrest|Navinder Singh Sarao]], accused of contributing to the [[2010 Flash Crash|2010 Wall Street "flash crash"]] by placing bogus orders to spoof the market, fails in his legal bid to stop extradition and will now be sent to the [[United States]] to face trial where he is wanted by U.S. authorities on 22 criminal counts of [[wire fraud]], [[commodities]] fraud, and [[market manipulation]]. A judge initially approved his extradition in March, and today his bid to launch an appeal against that decision was rejected, ending his 18-month legal fight. He will now be extradited within 28 days. [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-flashcrash-trader-extradition-idUSKBN12E196 (Reuters)]
