*[[2015 G-20 Antalya summit]] 
**The leaders of nations with the top 20 economies vow to use all their policy resources to bolster a sagging global economic environment. While the [[European migrant crisis]] and [[terrorism]] were high on the agenda of this economic summit the [[November 2015 Paris attacks|terror attacks in Paris]] dominate conversations and meetings. In the wake of the attacks and during ongoing investigation, French President [[François Hollande]] remains in [[Paris]]. [http://uk.reuters.com/article/2015/11/15/uk-g20-turkey-communique-idUKKCN0T40N420151115 (Reuters)] [http://money.cnn.com/2015/11/15/news/economy/paris-attack-g20-summit/index.html (CNN Money)] [https://www.nytimes.com/2015/11/15/us/politics/paris-attacks-to-dominate-agenda-at-g-20-conference-in-turkey.html (New York Times)]
**[[President of the United States|United States President]] [[Barack Obama]] and [[President of Russia|Russian President]] [[Vladimir Putin]], in a G20 working lunch meeting, traded views on [[Syria|Syria's]] civil war: where they agreed, i.e. UN negotiations, cease-fire, and Syrian-led political transition; and where they disagreed, i.e. [[President of Syria|President]] [[Bashar al-Assad|Bashar al-Assad's]] future role. Obama reiterated his support for the full implementation of the [[Minsk II|Minsk agreements]] on [[Ukraine]], including Ukraine's control of its borders.
