* [[South Africa]]'s [[Constitutional Court of South Africa|Constitutional Court]] declares that current marriage laws restricting marriage to opposite-sex couples are [[Constitutionality|unconstitutional]] and [[Same-sex marriage in South Africa|must be changed within a year]]. Once the change is made, South Africa will be the fifth country in the world where [[same-sex marriages]] are recognized, after [[Canada]], [[Spain]], the [[Netherlands]], and [[Belgium]]. [http://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20051201/ap_on_re_af/south_africa_gay_marriage (AP via Yahoo)]
* The [[European Central Bank]] raises interest rates for the first time in five years, from 2.0% to 2.25%. This will affect the cost of money in the twelve [[Eurozone]] countries. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/4488550.stm (BBC)]
* A [[Buddhist]] [[manuscript]] written on [[Birch bark document|birch bark]] in the 1st century or 2nd century passes from a private collection to the [[University of Washington]] [[library]], becoming part of the [[Early Buddhist Manuscripts Project]]. [http://www.uwnews.org/article.asp?articleID=3487 (uwnews.org)]
* [[Muriel Degauque]] is identified as the [[Belgium|Belgian]] [[suicide bomber]] who killed herself in [[Iraq]] on November 9, 2005. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4488642.stm (BBC)]
* [[Ray Hanna]], who died on this day in Switzerland, was an air-display pilot, regarded by many as the best of the best, and was well known for flying [[Supermarine Spitfire | Spitfire]] Mk IX MH-434. He was with the [[Red Arrows]] from 1965 to 1971, and in that time was their longest serving - and some say their most influential - leader. He and his son, Mark Hanna, started the [http://www.ofmc.co.uk/mah/mah.htm Old Flying Machine Company] The Red Arrows paid [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/suffolk/4532334.stm tribute] to him with a [[flypast]] at his funeral.
