*[[Myanma general election, 2015|Myanmar general election, 2015]]
**With 45 percent of the [[House of Representatives (Myanmar)|Pyithu Hluttaw (House of Representatives)]] results announced from Sunday's election, [[Aung San Suu Kyi|Aung San Suu Kyi's]] [[National League for Democracy]] (NLD) moves closer to an absolute majority in the country's [[Assembly of the Union|parliament]], winning  over 90 percent  (135 of 149 seats) in the country's [[House of Representatives (Myanmar)|lower house]]. The NLD also has taken over 85 percent in  the [[House of Nationalities|upper house]] and [[State and Region Hluttaws|regional assemblies]]. The NLD needs to win more than two-thirds of seats that were contested in parliament to form [[Myanmar|Myanmar's]] first democratically elected government since the early 1960s. [http://www.reuters.com/article/2015/11/11/us-myanmar-election-idUSKCN0SW0TP20151111#TZdzAMkJp5jOPYS2.97 (Reuters)]
**Twenty-five percent (25%) of the seats in the legislature are not elected; they are reserved for military appointees, taken from [[Tatmadaw|Myanmar Armed Forces (Tatmadaw)]] personnel, and officially known as "Army Representatives." [http://www.channel3000.com/news/myanmar-vote-suu-kyi-predicts-majority/36358008 (Channel 3000)]
