;Business and economy
*[[Procter and Gamble]] announces that it will close its plant in [[Kansas City, Kansas]] and reduce operations at its [[Iowa City, Iowa]] plant, as the company plans to shift production to factories in [[Tablers Station, West Virginia]] and [[Cincinnati|Cincinnati, Ohio]] by 2020. The reductions will affect 280 full-time workers with the Kansas City factory and 500 workers at its two Iowa City factories (with the workforce at the latter plant being reduced to about 700 employees.) [https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/nation-now/2018/02/07/procter-gamble-plant-cuts/316907002/ (''USA Today'')], [http://www.kansascity.com/news/business/article198918024.html (''Kansas City Star'')]

*[[Macedonia naming dispute]]
**[[Prime Minister of Macedonia]] [[Zoran Zaev]] states that his country is ready to add a geographical qualifier to its name in order to end the dispute. He also says that his government renamed the country's [[International Airport Skopje|main airport]] and a [[M-1 motorway (Republic of Macedonia)|key highway]]. [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-macedonia-greece/macedonia-says-ready-to-change-its-name-and-end-row-with-greece-idUSKBN1FQ2OY (Reuters)]

;Politics and elections
*[[Politics of the European Union]]
**The [[European Parliament]] votes 447–196 to dismiss [[Ryszard Czarnecki]] ([[Law and Justice|PiS]]) as one of its 14 vice-presidents, for calling fellow [[Poland|Polish]] parliamentarian [[Róża Thun]] ([[Civic Platform]]) a "[[szmalcownik]]" (meaning [[Nazi]] [[Collaboration with the Axis Powers during World War II|collaborator]]). It is the first time that the parliament invokes the powers of [[w:s:Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union#Article 21 – Non-discrimination|Article 21]] of the [[Treaty on European Union]] to dismiss a senior office holder. Czarnecki remains a [[Member of the European Parliament]]. [http://www.dw.com/en/european-parliament-dismisses-polish-vice-president-ryszard-czarnecki-over-nazi-slur/a-42485239 (Deutsche Welle)]

;Science and technology
*[[Prehistoric Britain]]
**Researchers at [[Natural History Museum, London|London's Natural History Museum]] state that the DNA extracted from "[[Cheddar Man]]" reveals that early inhabitants of [[Great Britain]] had blue eyes and dark skin. The name "Cheddar Man" was given to a fossil of a human man that lived thousands of years ago, which was discovered in 1903. [http://www.bbc.com/news/science-environment-42939192 (BBC)], [https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/07/world/europe/uk-cheddar-man-skeleton-skin.html (''The New York Times'')]
