;Disasters and accidents
*Debris from a [[Buk missile system|Buk]] surface-to-air missile is found at an unspecified location in eastern [[Ukraine]]. According to the Dutch Safety Board and the Dutch prosecutor overseeing the criminal investigation, it may have come from a Buk (possibly of Russian origin) fired by rebel fighters, accidentally or purposefully, in the downing of [[Malaysia Airlines Flight 17]]. The investigation remains classified. [http://www.msn.com/en-us/news/world/mh17-investigators-find-possible-buk-missile-fragments/ar-BBlDRrd?ocid=spartandhp (AFP via MSN)] 
*A [[heatwave]] in [[Egypt]] has killed at least 42 people. [http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/stories/M/ML_MIDEAST_WEATHER?SITE=AP&SECTION=HOME&TEMPLATE=DEFAULT&CTIME=2015-08-11-12-46-54 (AP)]
*Flooding in [[Argentina]] kills at least three people and 11,000 are made homeless. [http://www.abc.net.au/news/2015-08-12/argentina-flooding-sparks-evacuation-of-thousands/6692118 (AFP via ABC News)]
*[[Japan|Japan's]] [[Sendai Nuclear Power Plant]] in [[Satsumasendai]] in [[Kagoshima Prefecture]] on [[Kyushu]] restarts reactor no. 1. This is the first of [[Japanese reaction to Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster#Japanese nuclear reactors taken offline|50-plus shut-down nuclear reactors to go back online since the 2011]] [[Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster]]. The problems discovered in the reactor model after the disaster have not been fixed. [http://www.upi.com/Business_News/Energy-Resources/2015/08/11/Japan-restarts-nuclear-reactor-first-since-Fukushima/4611439285816/ (UPI)], [http://www.cnn.com/2015/08/11/asia/japan-nuclear-reactor/ (CNN)]
