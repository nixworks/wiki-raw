* [[War on Terror]]: [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Jane Harman]], the [[ranking member]] of the [[U.S. House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence]], announces she is planning to introduce a bill to ban [[torture]] by U.S. interrogators and bar transfers of detainees to countries that engage in torture. [http://www.latimes.com/news/nationworld/nation/la-na-briefs8.5feb08,0,6788474.story?coll=la-home-nation (LA Times)]
* [[Conflict in Iraq]]: A suicide bomber kills at least 21 people outside a police recruiting center in [[Baghdad]]. In a separate incident also in Baghdad, three policemen die from a firefight in the notoriously dangerous neighborhood of [[Ghazaliyah]]. [http://www.newsday.com/ny-woiraq0208,0,2619531.story?coll=ny-homepage-big-pix (Newsday/AP)]
* [[Carly Fiorina]], widely considered one of the most powerful women in [[United States of America|American]] business, resigns her post as [[Chief executive officer|CEO]] of [[Hewlett-Packard]], having been asked to do so by the company's board of directors after she failed to cut costs and increase revenue as quickly as the board had hoped. She had served as CEO of the company since [[1999]] and [[Chair (official)|Chairman of the Board]] since 2000. [http://abcnews.go.com/Business/wireStory?id=484825 (ABC News)]
* British survey ship HMS ''Scott'' produces the first sonar survey of the seabed site of the [[2004 Indian Ocean earthquake]]. Some images appear to show a landslide 100 metres high and 2 kilometres long. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/science/nature/4247409.stm (BBC)].
* [[Switzerland]]'s Federal Court rules that the money that former Nigerian [[List of Presidents of Nigeria|dictator]] [[Sani Abacha]] took from Nigeria may be returned to the country.  Abacha took hundreds of millions of dollars from Nigeria during his rule as dictator.  [http://www.swissinfo.org/sen/swissinfo.html?siteSect=106&sid=5524038 (Swissinfo)]
* The [[Economic Community of West African States]] intends to hold an emergency summit in [[Niger]] to discuss the political developments about the appointment of [[Faure Gnassingbé]] as the president of [[Togo]]. [http://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/NewsArchive/artikel.php?ID=74979 (GhanaWeb)] [http://allafrica.com/stories/200502080545.html (AllAfrica)] [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/4248887.stm (BBC)]
* A car bomb, apparently set up by [[Basque people|Basque]] [[paramilitary]] group [[ETA (separatist group)|ETA]], explodes in [[Madrid]], injuring 31 people outside a conference center where the [[List of Spanish monarchs|King and Queen]] of Spain and [[President of Mexico]] were to open an exhibition later today. [http://www.reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml?type=worldNews&storyID=7577444 (Reuters)] [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/europe/4249159.stm (BBC)].
* In [[Canada]], former [[Prime Minister of Canada|prime minister]] [[Jean Chrétien]] defends his government's actions in an inquiry into the misuse of government advertising and sponsorship funds.  [http://www.canoe.ca/NewsStand/WinnipegSun/News/2005/02/09/925056-sun.html (Winnipeg Sun)] [http://www.canada.com/news/national/story.html?id=27c45868-c3be-46f2-842e-e9dbb5cee69c (Canada.Com)]
