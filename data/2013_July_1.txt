*[[2012–2013 Egyptian protests]]:
**Demonstrations across [[Egypt]] gather about 15 million people nationwide calling for the resignation of [[President of Egypt|Egyptian President]] [[Mohammed Morsi]]. **At least 16 people, including an American, have been killed, and 800 injured, in the [[Egyptian protests (June 2013–present)|anti-government demonstrations]] that began last week. [http://www.jpost.com/Middle-East/Egypt-braces-for-storm-of-protest-318205 (''The Jerusalem Post'')] [http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/01/world/meast/egypt-protests/index.html?hpt=hp_t1 (CNN)]
**[[Egyptian Army|Egyptian army officials]] issue a 48-hour ultimatum to the [[Muslim Brotherhood in Egypt|Muslim Brotherhood]]-linked government of [[President of Egypt|President]] [[Mohamed Morsi]] to respond to the concerns of millions of [[Egyptian protests (June 2013–present)|protesters who have taken to the streets]] calling for Morsi’s resignation, or it will “step in to restore order”. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-23133174 (BBC)] [https://www.nytimes.com/2013/07/02/world/middleeast/egypt-protests.html?pagewanted=all&_r=0 (''The New York Times'')] [http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/01/world/meast/egypt-protests/index.html?hpt=hp_t1  (''CNN'')]
*At least 47 people are killed and more than 90 wounded in two separate bomb blasts in [[Pakistan]], one in a [[Shiite]] suburb of [[Quetta]] and the other in an attack on a paramilitary convoy in the [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa|northwest of the country]]; a [[Sunni]] extremist group reportedly claims responsibility. [http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/01/world/asia/pakistan-violence/index.html?hpt=wo_c2 (''CNN'')]
