* The northern [[Iraq]]i city of [[Mosul]] falls to coalition forces as the Iraqi army's fifth Corps offers a letter of surrender. The only remaining major city left to fall is [[Saddam Hussein]]'s hometown of [[Tikrit]], where some expect the remaining regime loyalists to make their final stand.<ref>[http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/news/archive/2003/04/11/international0620EDT0536.DTL ]{{dead link|date=September 2015}}</ref>
* Europe's largest [[civil engineering]] project, and the world's largest single [[rapid transit|metro]] expansion project, is officially opened in [[Madrid]]. ''[[Line 12 (Madrid Metro)|MetroSur]]'', a 40-kilometre loop of the [[Madrid Metro]] in the southern suburbs of the city, took under three years to complete.
* [[Cuba]] executes three men charged with [[terrorism]] for [[Maritime hijacking|hijacking]] a passenger [[ferry]] on April 2. Another four men receive life sentences.<ref>[http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story2&cid=589&ncid=589&amp;e=1&u=/ap/20030411/ap_on_re_la_am_ca/cuba_hijackers_executed_6]{{dead link|date=August 2015}}</ref>
* In response to [[Baseball Hall of Fame]] president [[Dale Petroskey]]'s April 8 decision to cancel a 15th anniversary celebration of "''[[Bull Durham]]'', [[sportswriter]] [[Roger Kahn]] cancels a planned appearance at the [[baseball]] museum.<ref>[http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&u=/nm/20030411/people_nm/people_robbins_writer_dc_4]{{dead link|date=August 2015}}</ref>
* [[Tom Hurndall]], a British [[photojournalist]] is shot in the head by an Israeli [[sniper]] in [[Rafah]], [[Gaza Strip]].
