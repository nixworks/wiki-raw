<!-- All news items below this line -->{{Current events header|2004|02|06}}

* Tens of thousands demonstrate in [[Caracas]], Venezuela, against what they see as the government's fraud committed by the [[National Electoral Council (Venezuela)|National Electoral Council]] related to the realization of a presidential referendum in mid-2004.
* The United States puts forth a [[UN Security Council]] resolution seeking to freeze the assets of [[Charles G. Taylor|Charles Taylor]], the exiled former president of [[Liberia]]. The U.S. also announces that it is pledging {{Nowrap|$35 million}} to help rebuild Liberia's armed forces and that it supports the cancellation of Liberia's international debt, providing that economic reforms are implemented. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/3538553.stm (BBC)]
* Singer [[David Crosby]] is arrested in [[New York City]] after [[marijuana]] and a [[handgun]] were found in a suitcase Crosby accidentally left behind at his hotel. [http://www.cnn.com/2004/SHOWBIZ/Music/03/06/david.crosby.arrested/ (CNN)]
* [[Palestinians]] are killed and wounded in attack on the main crossing point between the [[Gaza Strip]] and [[Israel]]. [[Hamas]], [[Islamic Jihad Movement in Palestine|Islamic Jihad]], and the [[al-Aqsa Martyrs Brigades]] claim responsibility. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/3538607.stm (BBC)]
* [[Teenagers]] released from [[Guantanamo Bay detainment camp|Guantanamo Bay]]'s [[Camp X-Ray]] recall the place fondly. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/guantanamo/story/0,13743,1163435,00.html (Guardian)]
* [[Turkey|Turkish]] [[politics|politicians]] claim that there is political consensus within Turkey to join the [[European Union]], despite the EU's demands for changes before that can occur. [http://www.iht.com/articles/509118.html (IHT)]
* Up to 80,000 people march through the [[Turkey|Turkish]] capital [[Ankara]] against plans to reform the country's [[civil service]]. The marchers fear that the reforms could lead to the civil service becoming politicised or losing its secular status. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/3539881.stm (BBC)]
* [[Canadian federal election, 2004|Canadian federal election]]: Former deputy prime minister [[Sheila Copps]] loses the [[Liberal Party of Canada|Liberal]] constituency nomination to current Transport Minister [[Tony Valeri]] by 311 votes. She will likely appeal; there is some speculation that if the loss remains, she will either run as an independent or for the [[New Democratic Party of Canada|New Democratic Party]]. [http://www.thestar.com/NASApp/cs/ContentServer?pagename=thestar/Layout/Article_Type1&c=Article&cid=1078614610252&call_pageid=968332188492&col=968793972154 (Toronto Star)]
* The scientists of the Russian polar research station near the [[North Pole]] are evacuated from their shifting and cracking ice floe by two Russian helicopters via Norway's [[Spitzbergen]] island. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/3538937.stm (BBC)]
