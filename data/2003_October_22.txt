* India launches a peace initiative to normalise relations with [[Pakistan]].  Formal talks are conditional on [[Islamabad]] ending [[Kashmir]]i [[cross-border terrorism]] initiatives.<ref>[http://jang.com.pk/thenews/oct2003-daily/23-10-2003/main/main1.htm] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040301034842/http://jang.com.pk/thenews/oct2003-daily/23-10-2003/main/main1.htm |date=March 1, 2004 }}</ref>
* [[Mahathir bin Mohamad]], outgoing [[prime minister]] of [[Malaysia]], accuses leading democratic nations of terrorising the world.  He seemed to be referring to the US, [[Israel]], and Australia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/3204001.stm|title=BBC NEWS - Asia-Pacific - Mahathir attacks 'state terrorism'|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[American occupation of Iraq|Occupation of Iraq]]: The commander of US ground forces in [[Iraq]] says that [[Al-Qaeda]] is now operating in Iraq as witnessed by increasingly sophisticated attacks on US troops.<ref>[http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=578&ncid=578&e=7&u=/nm/20031022/ts_nm/iraq_dc] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050315232051/http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=578&ncid=578&e=7&u=/nm/20031022/ts_nm/iraq_dc |date=March 15, 2005 }}</ref>
* [[European Union]] and [[Guantanamo Bay detainment camp|Guantanamo Bay]]: Leaders of the European Union parliament urge the EU to take action over 26 Europeans being held indefinitely by the US without charges, without trial, without legal representation at Guantanamo Bay. The detainees are experiencing increasing psychological problems.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/3205055.stm|title=BBC NEWS - Americas - Europe urges Guantanamo action|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[Human Rights Watch]] (HRW) releases a report about [[mental illness|mentally ill]] inmates of United States [[prison]]s. It concludes that mentally ill offenders are frequently physically abused, punished by staff for self-destructive behavior and not given the treatment they need.<ref>[http://www.hrw.org/press/2003/10/us102203.htm] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141017221103/http://www.hrw.org/press/2003/10/us102203.htm |date=October 17, 2014 }}</ref>
* Top British runner [[Dwain Chambers]] tests positive for the drug [[tetrahydrogestrinone]] (THG). The [[steroid]] was previously believed to be undetectable but an anonymous source provided a used syringe containing traces last week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/athletics/3203005.stm|title=BBC SPORT - Athletics - Chambers fails drugs test|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
