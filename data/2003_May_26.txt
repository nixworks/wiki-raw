* A Ukrainian YAk-42 plane crashes in northeast Turkey, near the city of Trabzon, killing all aboard. The plane carried 12 crew-members and 62 [[Spain|Spanish]] soldiers returning from a six-month peacekeeping mission in [[Afghanistan]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.cnn.com/2003/WORLD/europe/05/26/turkey.crash/index.html | work=CNN | title=Spain minister to visit crash site | date=May 26, 2003 | accessdate=May 12, 2010}}</ref>
* A draft of the proposed [[European constitution]] is unveiled.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/2938272.stm | work=BBC News | title=Excerpts: Europe's draft constitution | date=June 21, 2004 | accessdate=May 12, 2010}}</ref>
* In the city of [[Munich]] (Germany), the [[Social Democratic Party of Germany|SPD]] and [[Alliance '90/The Greens|Greens]], who have a city hall majority, decide in separate meetings to migrate 14,000 city-owned [[Windows NT]] PC workstations to the [[SUSE Linux distributions|SUSE]] [[Linux]] [[operating system]]. In spite of personal meetings with Microsoft [[CEO]] [[Steve Ballmer]] and substantial rebates offered on a Microsoft solution, Munich now becomes the largest German city to migrate all of its client PCs to [[Linux]]. As an office suite, [[OpenOffice.org]] will be used.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heise.de/newsticker/data/anw-26.05.03-004/|title=Münchener Rathaus-SPD entscheidet sich für Linux [Update]|author=Andreas Wilkens|date=26 May 2003|work=heise online|accessdate=24 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[Peter Hollingworth]] resigns as [[Governor-General of Australia]].
