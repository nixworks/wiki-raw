;Business and economy
*[[Economy of the European Union]]
**The [[European Commission]] scraps the draft legislation that would have permitted the [[European Banking Authority|EBA regulator]] to order "[[too big to fail]]" banks to split off their [[Trader (finance)|trading activities]], citing "no foreseeable agreement" in sight on criteria. The draft was supposed to be the [[European Union|EU]]'s answer to the [[United States]]' [[Volcker Rule]]. [http://www.reuters.com/article/us-eu-banks-regulations/eu-scraps-its-answer-to-u-s-volcker-rule-for-banks-idUSKBN1CT285?il=0 (Reuters)]
*[[Banking in the United States]]
**[[Vice President]] [[Mike Pence]] casts the tie-breaking vote late Tuesday night to repeal a rule that would have made it easier for Americans to sue their banks and credit card companies. [http://www.cnn.com/2017/10/24/politics/senate-cfpb-arbitration-repeal/index.html (CNN)]

*[[Immigration policy of Donald Trump]]
**U.S. President [[Donald Trump]] signs an [[List of executive actions by Donald Trump|executive order]] which allows U.S. to resume its refugee program, with a 90-day review period for 11 countries identified as "high risk". [https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2017/oct/24/trump-refugee-ban-end-immigration-executive-order (''The Guardian'')]
