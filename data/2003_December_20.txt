* The [[Holy See]] announces plans to [[beatification|beatify]] the last [[Austria-Hungary|Austro-Hungarian]] [[emperor]] [[Karl of Austria|Karl]]. Karl, who was overthrown in [[1918]] and died in exile in [[1922]], is expected to be beatified, a step to [[saint]]hood, in the next year. Karl's widow, [[Zita of Bourbon-Parma]] died in 1989. His son, former [[Crown Prince]] [[Otto von Habsburg]] was until recently a [[Germany|German]] [[Member of the European Parliament|MEP]]. * [[Closed-circuit television|CCTV]] footage at [[Hampton Court]] Palace near London, once home of King [[Henry VIII of England]], is released, and claimed to show a "[[ghost]]". The footage, taken in October 2003, shows a man in [[16th century]] clothes closing a firedoor that had blown open. The palace markets itself as one of [[United Kingdom|Britain]]'s most haunted locations. [http://www.itv.com/news/1720815.html] [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/3336299.stm]
*Celebrations marking the Bicentennial of the [[Louisiana Purchase]] culminate in [[New Orleans, Louisiana]]. [http://www.ajc.com/news/content/news/1203/20louisiana.html]
*[[Ireland|Irish]] charity fundraiser [[John O'Shea]] attacks [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] football manager Sir [[Alex Ferguson]] as "greedy" for demanding £90,000 to attend a [[cancer]] charity function in Ireland in 1999. According to O'Shea, a sports celebrity demanding 'appearance money' from a charity is unheard of in his experience. Ferguson's appearance fee amounted to half the money raised. The fundraisers, until now unaware that Ferguson had taken half the proceeds, denounce his behaviour and say if they had known about it at the time they would have cancelled the event.
