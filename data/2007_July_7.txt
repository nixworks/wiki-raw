* Pope [[Benedict XVI]] removes restrictions on celebrating the old [[Tridentine Mass|Latin Mass]], reviving an ancient [[Roman Rite]] [[Mass (liturgy)|Mass]] liturgy that was essentially abolished after the [[Second Vatican Council]]. [http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/07/07/AR2007070700376.html?hpid=sec-religion (Washington Post via ''AP'')]
* The [[New Seven Wonders of the World]] are announced.  These are [[The Great Wall of China]], [[Petra]] in [[Jordan]], the [[Christ the Redeemer (statue)|Christ the Redeemer statue]] in [[Brazil]], [[Machu Picchu]] in [[Peru]], [[Mexico]]'s [[Chichen Itza]] [[Maya civilization|Mayan]] site, the [[Colosseum]] in [[Rome]] and the [[Taj Mahal]] in [[India]]. [http://www.abc.net.au/news/stories/2007/07/08/1972797.htm (Reuters via ABC News Australia)]
* A [[bus]] crash in [[Java]] kills at least 14 people. 48 people were injured, many seriously. [http://www.asknedemek.com (AP via the Guardian)]
* [[2007 Amirli bombing]]: At least 105 people are killed when a [[suicide bomber|suicide truck bomber]] attacks a market in [[Amirli]] in northern [[Iraq]] with a majority [[Shiite]] [[Iraqi Turkmen|Turkmen]] population. [http://www.birakma.com (Reuters via ABC News Australia)]
* The Government of [[Afghanistan]] states that it will investigate claims that [[United States]] and [[NATO]] air strikes caused heavy civilian casualties in [[Farah Province]] and [[Kunar Province]]. [http://www.reuters.com/article/worldNews/idUSISL26807920070707 (Reuters)]
* [[Live Earth]] gets underway with concerts in [[Australia]], the [[United States]], [[Germany]], [[South Africa]], the [[United Kingdom]], [[Brazil]], [[Japan]] and [[China]]. [http://www.smh.com.au/news/music/global-extravaganza-underway/2007/07/07/1183351498368.html?s_cid=rss_smh (Sydney Morning Herald)]
* [[King of Nepal|King]] [[Gyanendra of Nepal]] celebrates his 60th birthday amid protests by students and youth wings of eight ruling parties.
