*[[2016 Fort McMurray wildfire]]
**The [[Canada|western Canadian]] [[Provinces and territories of Canada|province]] of [[Alberta]] declares a [[state of emergency]]  as devastating wildfires,  fed by unseasonably hot temperatures and dry conditions, grow to five times their initial size. The mandatory evacuation order expands to include the communities of [[Anzac, Alberta|Anzac]], [[Gregoire Lake Estates]], and [[Fort McMurray First Nation]],  forcing thousands  who had just resettled  to  move again. [http://www.ctvnews.ca/canada/fort-mcmurray-fire-state-of-emergency-declared-1.2888796 (CTV)] [https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/2016/05/05/raging-wildfire-drives-out-newly-resettled-evacuees/83960660/ (''USA Today'')] [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-canada-wildfire-fortmcmurray-idUSKCN0XU2D8 (Reuters)]
**Since the fire has intermittently blocked the only route south toward [[Alberta|Alberta's]] major cities,  evacuees have had to go north toward oil camps and a few small settlements.  The province has airlifted 4,000 people to [[Edmonton]] and [[Calgary]] as of late Thursday. Roughly 20 percent of [[Canada|Canada's]] [[Petroleum|crude oil production]], around 680,000 barrels per day, is offline and is expected to increase. There are no known casualties from the fire, which today has grown to 210,000 acres (85,000 hectares), but fatalities are reported in at least one vehicle crash along the evacuation route. [http://www.dailymail.co.uk/wires/reuters/article-3575125/New-evacuations-fire-struck-Canadian-city-energy-heartland.html (''Daily Mail'')]
**[[Reuters]] reports that [[Alberta|Alberta Province]], which projects a C$10 billion deficit this fiscal year as the prolonged slump in [[Price of oil|oil prices]] has reduced [[tax revenue]], last month cut funding for [[Wildfire#Prevention|wildfire prevention]], including millions for programs created in response to the province's [[2011 Slave Lake wildfire|Slave Lake wildfire]] in 2011, which destroyed more than 300 homes.  [http://www.voanews.com/content/canada-fire-hit-as-government-cut-spending-on-prevention-planes/3317951.html (Reuters via Voice of America)]
