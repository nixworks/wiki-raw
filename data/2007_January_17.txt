* [[United States]] [[President of the United States|President]] [[George W. Bush]] announces that the [[National Security Agency|NSA]] has ended its practice of [[NSA warrantless surveillance controversy|warrantless wiretapping]] for domestic surveillance, and will go to the courts for [[warrant (law)|warrants]] in the future. [http://www.cnn.com/2007/POLITICS/01/17/domestic.spying/index.html (CNN)]
* Lieutenant-General [[Dan Halutz]], the Chief of Staff of the [[Israel]]i Army, resigns while inquiries into the performance of the [[Israel Defense Forces]] in the action against [[Hezbollah]] continue. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/6269353.stm (BBC)]
* Ali al-Sadig, spokesman for the Sudanese Foreign Ministry, accuses U.S. troops of raiding Sudan's defunct embassy in Iraq. The U.S. government denies that any raid took place. [http://www.cnn.com/2007/WORLD/africa/01/17/Sudan.embassy.reut/index.html?section=cnn_latest (CNN)] 
* U.S. television network [[NBC]] officially pulls out of the [[soap opera]] market by canceling ''[[Passions]]'', which is aired both domestically and internationally. [[NBC Universal Television]] president [[Jeff Zucker]] remarks that the network's other daytime drama, ''[[Days of Our Lives]]'', is "unlikely to continue" when its contract expires in 2009. [https://www.usatoday.com/life/television/news/2007-01-17-nbc-presstour_x.htm (USA Today)]
* The [[Philippine Army]] kills [[Jainal Antel Sali, Jr.|Abu Sulaiman]], the leader of [[Abu Sayyaf]], a terrorist organization affiliated with [[Al Qaeda]] that operates in the [[Philippines]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/6269701.stm (BBC)]
* Members of the [[Senate of the United States]] agree on a draft resolution opposing the [[Iraq War troop surge of 2007|proposed increase in the number of troops]] in [[Iraq]]. [https://www.usatoday.com/news/washington/2007-01-17-dems-iraq_x.htm?csp=34 (USA Today)]
* Four employees for the [[National Democratic Institute]], a U.S. [[think tank]], are killed when their convoy is ambushed in Baghdad. [http://www.nasdaq.com/aspxcontent/NewsStory.aspx?cpath=20070117%5cACQDJON200701171500DOWJONESDJONLINE000893.htm& (Dow Jones Online)]
* The minute hand on the [[Doomsday clock]] of the [[Bulletin of Atomic Scientists]] is moved to five minutes to midnight. [https://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=20601102&sid=aTa7clkaxKCU&refer=uk (Bloomberg)]
* [[Walter Forbes]], the former chair of [[Cendant]], is sentenced to twelve and a half years in jail and ordered to pay $3.28 billion in restitution for his role in the biggest accounting fraud in the 1990s.  [https://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=20601103&sid=a9SsnDTz.6Ao (Bloomberg)]
* The [[India]]n Government has waded into the alleged [[bullying]] and racial abuse of contestant [[Shilpa Shetty]] on [[Celebrity Big Brother 2007 (UK)|Celebrity Big Brother UK]], which is said to have sparked more viewer complaints to media regulator [[Ofcom]] than any other show in the history of [[United Kingdom|British]] television.
