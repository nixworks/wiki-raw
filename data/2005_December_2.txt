* The "Thermopolis" specimen, recently donated to the [[Wyoming Dinosaur Center]] in [[Thermopolis, Wyoming]] and described in the ''Science'' article "A well-preserved [[Archaeopteryx]] specimen with theropod features", shows that the Archaeopteryx lacked a reversed toe&mdash;a universal feature of birds&mdash;limiting its ability to perch in trees and implying a terrestrial lifestyle. This has been interpreted as evidence of [[Theropoda|theropod]] ancestry. The specimen also has a hyperextendible second toe. "Until now, the feature was thought to belong only to the species' close relatives, the deinonychosaurs."
