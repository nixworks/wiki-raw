* Construction workers of [[Qiqihar]], [[Heilongjiang Province]], People's Republic of China accidentally dig out five Japanese [[mustard gas]] bombs from the [[Sino-Japanese War (1937-1945)]]. Two of the bombs are damaged, and the gas [[poison]]s 43 (one died 19 days later). Japan a week later accepts responsibility and sends doctors and compensation to China.<ref>{{cite web|author=Willy Wo-Lap Lam |url=http://edition.cnn.com/2003/WORLD/asiapcf/east/08/17/china.bombs/ |title=CNN.com - WWII chemical bombs add to Japan damages call - Aug. 18, 2003 |publisher=Edition.cnn.com |date=2003-08-18 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
* The clergy and lay people of the [[Episcopal Church in the United States of America]], by a comfortable margin, vote in favor of the appointment of an openly [[gay]] [[bishop]]. The vote is thought likely to get confirmation from the bishops' collegium, which however is delayed due to last minute independent allegations of misconduct and intense conservative opposition.<ref>{{cite web|author=Gene-Robinson |url=https://news.google.com/news?q=Gene-Robinson+&num=20&hl=en&safe=off&sa=G&scoring=d |title=Gene-Robinson |publisher=News.google.com |date= |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/3120171.stm |title=Americas &#124; US Church approves gay bishop |publisher=BBC News |date=2003-08-04 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
* ''[[SCO v. IBM Linux lawsuit]]:'' Reuters have reported that [[Red Hat]] intends to start legal action against [[SCO Group|SCO]] to establish that SCO's claims against the Linux operating system are invalid.
