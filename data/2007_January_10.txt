<!-- All news items below this line -->{{Current events header|2007|01|10}}

* U.S. President [[George W. Bush]] announces, in a televised address, [[The New Way Forward|his strategy change in response to Iraq Study Group criticisms]]. This will involve a surge of 21,500 more troops to fight in the [[Iraq War|War in Iraq]]. [http://www.cnn.com/2007/POLITICS/01/10/iraq.bush/index.html (CNN)]
* The [[Supreme Assembly of Tajikistan|Tajik Parliament]] approves a Memorandum of Understanding between [[Tajikistan]] and [[Iran]] agreeing to begin a nuclear program with assistance from the Iranian government in building a power plant. Tajikistan is the second nation in [[Central Asia]] to pursue a nuclear program, the other being [[Kazakhstan]]. [http://www.irna.ir/en/news/view/menu-237/0701107277190106.htm (IRNA)]
* [[Kazakhstan political shakeup of 2007]]: [[Karim Masimov]] is confirmed by the [[Parliament of Kazakhstan]] as [[Prime Minister of Kazakhstan|Prime Minister]]. Massimov appoints his former superior and political rival [[Daniyal Akhmetov]] to Defense Minister and Economy Minister [[Aslan Musin]] to Deputy Prime Minister. [http://www.iht.com/articles/ap/2007/01/10/asia/AS-POL-Kazakhstan-Cabinet.php (IHT)]
* [[Sudan]] adopts a new currency; the [[Sudanese pound]] replaces the [[Sudanese dinar]]. The change is part of reconciliation measures after the end of the [[Second Sudanese Civil War]]. [http://www.ecanadanow.com/world/2007/01/10/sudan-adopts-new-currency/ (E Canada Now)]
* [[Mirsad Bektašević]], a 19-year-old [[Sweden|Swedish]] citizen of [[Bosniaks|Bosnian]] descent, is convicted of [[terrorism]]-related charges by a court in [[Sarajevo]] and sentenced to 15 years' imprisonment. [http://today.reuters.com/news/articlenews.aspx?type=worldNews&storyID=2007-01-10T175835Z_01_L10385465_RTRUKOC_0_US-BOSNIA-COURT.xml (Reuters)] [http://www.thelocal.se/6051/20070110/ (The Local)]
* [[War in Somalia (2006–present)|War in Somalia]]: [[Somalia|Somali]] official says that a senior [[al-Qaeda]] suspect responsible for [[1998 United States embassy bombings|bombing]] [[United States|U.S.]] embassies in [[East Africa]] eight years ago is possibly killed in a U.S. airstrike in the [[Battle of Ras Kamboni]]. [http://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory?id=2783895&CMP=OTC-RSSFeeds0312 (AP via ABC News)] [http://edition.cnn.com/2007/WORLD/africa/01/10/somalia.strike.ap/index.html (CNN)] [http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2007/01/10/world/main2344598.shtml (CBS)]
* Two [[Eurocopter AS350|Squirrel]] helicopters collide at [[RAF Ternhill]] in [[Shropshire]], [[United Kingdom|UK]], killing one and injuring two others. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/shropshire/6249259.stm (BBC News)]
* Iranian news agency reports a [[UFO]] has crashed in the [[Kerman]] province. [http://english.farsnews.com/newstext.php?nn=8510200361 (Fars News Agency)]
* [[India]] [[Rocket launch|launch]]es four [[satellite]]s at a time with their [[PSLV]]-C7 rocket, including the [[Space Capsule Recovery Experiment|SRE-1]] test article, which will return to Earth in a test for a future Indian [[manned spaceflight]] program. [http://today.reuters.com/news/articlenews.aspx?type=scienceNews&storyID=2007-01-10T084639Z_01_DEL159433_RTRUKOC_0_US-INDIA-SPACE-SATELLITES.xml (Reuters)]
* The [[Energy policy of the European Union|European Commission]] announces proposals for the [[European Union|EU]] to cut [[carbon dioxide emissions]] by 20% by 2020 and 60% by 2050 in order to [[Mitigation of global warming|mitigate global warming]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/sci/tech/6247199.stm (BBC News)]
* Following the [[Nicaraguan general election of 2006|2006 general election]], [[Daniel Ortega]] becomes the new [[President of Nicaragua]], replacing [[Enrique Bolaños]]. [http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2007/01/10/ap/world/mainD8MIAAHG0.shtml (CBS)]
* The [[Social Democratic Party of Austria]], the future majority partner in the [[grand coalition]] [[Gusenbauer cabinet|government]] that will be sworn in in [[Austria]] on January 11, 2007, announces its future ministers. [http://www.gulf-times.com/site/topics/article.asp?cu_no=2&item_no=126422&version=1&template_id=39&parent_id=21 (Gulf Times)]
* Start of the 12th [[ASEAN Summit]] and 2nd [[East Asia Summit]] in Metro Cebu, Philippines. Meetings involve heads of the 10 member states and 6 dialogue partners with major discussions on relevance, diplomacy, security, economy and free trade and other important global issues. [http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/nation/view_article.php?article_id=42629 (Philippine Daily Inquirer)]
* A [[general strike]] starts in [[Guinea]], with [[trade union]]s calling for pay rises, the return to jail of [[Mamadou Sylla]] and the resignation of [[President of Guinea|President]] [[Lansana Conté]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/6247965.stm (BBC)]
