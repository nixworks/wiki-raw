* [[Italy|Italian]] [[Minister of the Environment]] [[Altero Matteoli]] announces interest in switching to [[nuclear power]] as the main source of energy within 10-15 years. Nuclear power has been banned in Italy since the [[Chernobyl accident]] in [[1986]]. [http://news.yahoo.com/s/nm/20051030/sc_nm/energy_italy_nuclear_dc;_ylt=Apb9oSiP.vGOIAUFZ7FojFGs0NUE;_ylu=X3oDMTA3MzV0MTdmBHNlYwM3NTM- (Reuters)]
* [[Hurricane Beta]], the first [[hurricane]] named with the [[Greek alphabet|Greek letter]] Beta, approaches [[Nicaragua]] and [[Honduras]] as a [[Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale|Category 3 storm]],  [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/4383940.stm (BBC)], and makes landfall on the [[Mosquito Coast]] at Category 2 intensity. Thousands of residents of [[Puerto Cabezas]], [[Nicaragua]] have been evacuated to shelters. [http://www.voanews.com/english/2005-10-30-voa7.cfm (VOA)], [http://news.scotsman.com/latest.cfm?id=2169692005 (Scotsman)] 
* Further [[rioting]] occurs overnight in the [[Paris]]ian [[suburb]] of [[Clichy-sous-Bois]] following the electrocutions of two teenagers, aged 15 and 17, and a third teenager suffering severe burns when they entered a [[Electrical substation|substation]] whilst fleeing police. Rioters attacked police and journalists. Critics allege that the teenagers were targeted because they were [[Muslim]], though this claim has been denied by [[France|French]] [[Minister of the Interior (France)|Interior Minister]] [[Nicolas Sarkozy]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4389920.stm (BBC)] [http://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory?id=1262800 (ABC News)]
* After an official 3-day visit to [[Pyongyang]], [[North Korea]], [[President of the People's Republic of China|President]] [[Hu Jintao]] of the [[People's Republic of China]] returns to [[Beijing]], having reached new directions in Sino-DPRK relations. This comes after North Korean [[Chairman of the National Defence Commission of North Korea|Leader]] [[Kim Jong-il]] called Hu the "most respected guest". [http://www.xxcb.com.cn/Articles/05/10/31/732973.HTM (Xiaoxiang Morning News)] [http://news.qq.com/a/20051031/001247.htm (QQ News)]
* The [[Dresden Frauenkirche]] (Church of Our Lady), a [[Protestantism|Protestant]] [[Church (building)|Church]] in [[Dresden]], [[Germany]] is [[Consecration|re-consecrated]]. The church was destroyed during the [[bombing of Dresden in World War II]]. The remains of the church had been left untouched by the government of [[East Germany]] as an anti-war monument. Some 60,000 people celebrated the reopening. Both [[Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom|Queen Elizabeth II]] of the [[United Kingdom]] and [[President of Germany|German President]] [[Horst Köhler]] said that the rebuilding was a 'symbol of reconciliation'.
