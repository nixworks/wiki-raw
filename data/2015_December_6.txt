*Scientists warn a new strain of the [[Panama disease|Panama Disease]] [[fungus]], which in the 1950s nearly wiped out the most popular banana in the world, the [[Gros Michel banana|Gros Michel]],  is threatening today's favorite, the [[Cavendish bananas|Cavendish]], according to a new study published in  ''[[PLOS Pathogens]]''. This  untreatable fungal disease, called [[Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. cubense|Tropical Race 4]], has been active for decades in [[Southeast Asia]]. It  recently has spread to other parts of [[Asia]], [[Africa]], [[Middle East|the Middle East]], and [[Australia]], and is expected to make its way to [[Latin America]], where the vast majority of the world's exported bananas are grown.
