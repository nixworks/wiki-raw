*[[Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals]]
**[[Presidency of Donald Trump|The Trump Administration]] announces it is ending the [[Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals|DACA program]] that has protected from deportation those who illegally entered the U.S. before their  16th birthday and before June 2007.  Congress is called upon to pass legislation to correct the situation. The so-called  [[DREAM Act|Dreamers]]  have been living in the country under “lawful status.” The Department of Homeland Security says it will no longer accept new applications but those enrolled in DACA will be able to continue working until their permits expire. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-politics/wp/2017/09/05/trump-administration-announces-end-of-immigration-protection-program-for-dreamers/?tid=pm_politics_pop&utm_term=.c40bc4175856 (''The Washington Post'')]  [http://www.npr.org/2017/09/05/546423550/trump-signals-end-to-daca-calls-on-congress-to-act (NPR)][https://www.theatlantic.com/politics/archive/2017/09/trump-ends-daca/538248/ (''The Atlantic'')] [http://www.cnn.com/2017/09/04/politics/daca-dreamers-immigration-program/index.html (CNN)][http://abcnews.go.com/Politics/trump-expected-announce-daca-decision-today/story?id=49611829 (ABC News)]
