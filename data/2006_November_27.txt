* The [[Her Majesty's Government|British government]] rejects proposals to increase the length of sound recording's [[copyright]] protection from 50 years to 95 years. This means that [[Cliff Richard|Sir Cliff Richard's]] earliest songs will come out of copyright in [[2008]], and the earliest songs recorded by the [[The Beatles|Beatles]] in [[2013]]. Songs composed and/or written will still have copyright for 70 years after the author's death. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/6186436.stm (BBC)]
* An airplane crashes in [[Tehran]] killing 28 members of the [[Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps]] including high-ranking officers. [http://www.iht.com/articles/ap/2006/11/27/africa/ME_GEN_Iran_Plane_Crash.php (AP via International Herald Tribune)]
* Australian [[Attorney-General]] [[Philip Ruddock]] tables the multi-volume [[Cole Inquiry]] report. It finds that the [[Prime Minister of Australia]] [[John Howard]], his ministers or Australian government departments were not aware of [[AWB Limited]] paying kickbacks to the regime of [[Saddam Hussein]] to buy Australian wheat under the [[Oil for Food]] program. [http://www.news.com.au/story/0,10117,20828605-2,00.html?from=public_rss (AAP via News Limited)]
* [[John Key]] is elected leader of the opposition [[New Zealand National Party]], with [[Bill English]] as his deputy. Both were unopposed. [http://www.nzherald.co.nz/section/story.cfm?c_id=1&ObjectID=10412658 (NZ Herald)]
* [[Prime Minister of Canada|Canadian Prime Minister]] [[Stephen Harper]] and the [[Canadian House of Commons]] endorse a motion to declare that the [[French-speaking Quebecer|Québécois]] form a nation within a unified [[Canada]].
