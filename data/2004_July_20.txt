* [[People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals]] releases a video of gross [[cruelty to animals|cruelty]] to chickens taken at [[Pilgrim's Pride]], one of [[KFC]]'s suppliers in [[West Virginia]]. The supplier will investigate the claims. [http://news.independent.co.uk/world/americas/story.jsp?story=543026 (The Independent)] [http://www.foxnews.com/story/0,2933,126359,00.html (FOX News)]
* [[Sandy Berger]] resigns as a foreign affairs advisor to [[John Kerry]]'s [[John Kerry presidential campaign, 2004|presidential campaign]] after it is reported that Berger was under investigation for allegedly illegally taking classified documents belonging to the [[National Archives and Records Administration|U.S. National Archives]], intended for review by the [[National Commission on Terrorist Attacks Upon the United States|9/11 Commission]], related to the [[Bill Clinton|Clinton administration's]] handling of millennium terror threats. [https://www.reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml?type=politicsNews&storyID=5723828 (Reuters)]
* Canadian [[Prime Minister of Canada|Prime minister]] [[Paul Martin]] announces his new [[cabinet of Canada|cabinet]], which includes new faces such as [[ice hockey]] great [[Ken Dryden]] and former [[British Columbia|BC]] premier [[Ujjal Dosanjh]]. [http://www.cbc.ca/canada/story/2004/07/20/cabinet_new040720.html (CBC)]
* [[Gloria Arroyo]], [[President of the Philippines|President]] of the [[Philippines]], confirms that hostage [[Angelo de la Cruz]] has been freed by his captors after their demands for a one-month-early withdrawal of all 51 Filipino troops from [[Iraq]] were met.  [https://www.reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml?type=topNews&storyID=5714443 (Reuters)]
* [[Ahmed Qurei]], Prime Minister of the [[Palestinian Authority]], agrees to withdraw his resignation, three days after tendering it.  Qurei is maintaining a threat to quit "because he has no powers". [https://www.reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml;jsessionid=SOX5QFE02GZRECRBAEZSFFA?type=worldNews&storyID=5716917 (Reuters)]
* [[Human Rights Watch]] releases a [http://hrw.org/english/docs/2004/07/19/darfur9096.htm report] stating that [[Sudan]]ese government documents confirm support for the [[Arab]] [[Janjaweed]] militia in their campaign of [[ethnic cleansing]] against [[Ethnic groups of Africa|African]] Muslims in [[Darfur]].
* The [[United States House of Representatives]] votes to override a proposal by the [[Financial Accounting Standards Board]] that would require publicly traded companies to record all forms of share-based payments to employees, including [[stock option]]s, as expenses. Similar legislation remains stalled in the [[United States Senate]].
* The [[European Union]] approves a 50-50 merger between [[Bertelsmann Music Group|BMG]] and [[Sony]]. [http://edition.cnn.com/2004/BUSINESS/07/20/sony.bmg.merger.ap/index.html (CNN)]
