;Armed conflicts and attacks
*[[Turkish military intervention in the Syrian Civil War]]
**[[Turkey|Turkish]] jets hit [[Kurds|Kurdish]]-led [[Syrian Democratic Forces]] (SDF) militia with 26 airstrikes in three recently-captured-from-[[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|ISIL]] villages west of [[al-Bab]] and northeast of [[Aleppo]]. The [[General Staff of the Republic of Turkey|Turkish General Staff]] says between 160 and 200 have been killed, while the [[Syrian Observatory for Human Rights]] reports 11 dead and dozens of people wounded. [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-mideast-crisis-syria-kurds-idUSKCN12K0ER (Reuters)], [https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/world/2016/10/20/turkish-media-strikes-kill-up-200-kurdish-militants-syria/92450020/ (USA Today)]

*[[Canada–European Union relations]]
**The [[European Union]] calls an emergency two-day meeting about the planned [[Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement|EU-Canada free trade agreement]], approved by all 28 EU member governments but held up by the "non" vote in [[Walloonia]], one of [[Belgium]]'s five sub-federal administrations. [http://www.nasdaq.com/article/eu-calls-emergency-meeting-to-save-canadian-free-trade-deal-cm696011 (NASDAQ)], [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-eu-canada-trade-idUSKCN12K1Z3 (Reuters)], [https://www.nytimes.com/2016/10/21/world/europe/brexit-migration-walloons-eus-list-of-crises-keeps-growing.html?_r=0 (''The New York Times'')]
*[[South Africa]] will notify the [[United Nations]] that the country is withdrawing from the [[International Criminal Court]] (ICC). This separation would take effect one year from when notice is formally received by the  [[Secretary-General of the United Nations|U.N. Secretary-General]]. Last week, [[Burundi]]'s parliament voted to leave the ICC, but the required paperwork has not yet reached the U.N. [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-safrica-icc-idUSKCN12L025 (Reuters)]

;Law and crime
*[[Brazil]]ian prosecutors file homicide charges against 21 people employed by the companies [[Samarco]], [[Vale (mining company)|Vale]], and [[BHP Billiton]] for the November 2015 iron ore mine dam burst in the state of [[Minas Gerais]] that killed 19 people and polluted waterways. [http://www.bbc.com/news/business-37723057 (BBC)]
*Following his October 5 arrest, [[Prosecutor#United States|U.S. federal prosecutors]] in [[Baltimore]] charge Harold T. Martin III, a former [[National Security Agency]] contractor, for violating the [[Espionage Act of 1917|Espionage Act]], specifically, with felony theft of government property. Prosecutors state that, over a 20-year time period, Martin stole at least 50 [[terabyte]]s of data and "six full [[Fellowes, Inc.|bankers boxes]]" of classified and other documents. The prosecutors state that Martin had an “arsenal” of weapons in his home. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/government-alleges-massive-theft-by-nsa-contractor/2016/10/20/e021c380-96cc-11e6-bb29-bf2701dbe0a3_story.html (''The Washington Post'')]
