* [[War on Terrorism]] – [[Canal Hotel bombing|Canal Hotel]]: A [[Canal Hotel truck bomb|truck bomb explosion at the Baghdad Canal Hotel]] that houses the [[United Nations]] mission kills at least 17 people and injures over 100. Among the casualties is [[Sérgio Vieira de Mello]], U.N. special representative in Iraq. The bomb damages a hospital nearby, and the [[shock wave]] is felt a mile away.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/middle_east/3163877.stm |title=Middle East &#124; Blast hits UN building in Baghdad |publisher=BBC News |date=2003-08-19 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref name="cbsnews2003">{{cite web|last=Roberts |first=Joel |url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2003/02/24/iraq/main541815.shtml |title=Iraqi PM Appeals For Reconciliation |publisher=CBS News |date=2003-02-24 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/news/world/iraq/2003-08-19-iraq-unblast_x.htm |title=Huge explosion rocks U.N. headquarters in Iraq |publisher=Usatoday.Com |date=2003-08-20 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
* 20 killed, 136 wounded by an explosion on board an [[Israel]]i bus in [[Jerusalem]]. Among the victims are several children. The explosion was caused by a [[Palestinian people|Palestinian]] [[suicide bomber]]. The [[Palestinian Islamic Jihad]] in [[Hebron]] claims responsibility for the attack. There are conflicting reports that [[Hamas]] is responsible for the bombing. Israeli government reportedly freezes [[road map for peace]] negotiations. In the following days, two additional victims died of their wounds, raising the death toll to 22.,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/0,2933,95150,00.html |title=Breaking News &#124; Latest News &#124; Current News |publisher=FoxNews.com |date= |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml?type=topNews&storyID=3302933 |title=Breaking News, Top News & Latest News Headlines |publisher=Reuters.com |date=2009-02-09 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml?type=topNews&storyID=3303310 |title=Breaking News, Top News & Latest News Headlines |publisher=Reuters.com |date=2009-02-09 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>[http://www.ctv.ca/servlet/ArticleNews/story/CTVNews/1061318328538_25///?hub=TopStories ]{{dead link|date=September 2015}}</ref> see also: [[Jerusalem bus 2 massacre]]
* [[War on Terrorism]]: A [[Morocco|Moroccan]] court sentences four men to death and jails 83 others for their involvement in a wave of terror attacks in [[Casablanca]] that killed 33 bystanders and a dozen suicide bombers in May 2003.   The trial involved dozens of defendants accused of belonging to a clandestine Moroccan group, the [[Salafia Jihadia]]. Moroccan authorities have linked the group to [[Osama bin Laden]]'s [[al-Qaida]] network.
* [[2003 occupation of Iraq timeline|Occupation of Iraq]]: Iraqi [[Vice President]] [[Taha Yassin Ramadan]], disguised as an Arab [[Bedouin]], has been captured. Ramadan served as Saddam's thug and a member of Iraq's [[Iraqi Revolutionary Command Council|Revolutionary Command Council]]. [[Kurd]]ish group claims responsibility for the capture of Ramadan. He was handed over to Coalition forces in [[Mosul]].<ref>[http://abcnews.go.com/wire/World/ap20030819_261.html ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20030821010349/http://abcnews.go.com/wire/World/ap20030819_261.html |date=August 21, 2003 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2003/WORLD/meast/08/19/sprj.irq.main/index.html |title=Truck bomb kills chief U.N. envoy to Iraq - Aug. 20, 2003 |publisher=CNN.com |date=2003-08-20 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/middle_east/3163219.stm |title=Middle East &#124; Saddam deputy captured |publisher=BBC News |date=2003-08-19 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
* [[Afghanistan]] celebrates its Independence Day amid one of the bloodiest weeks in a year, with heavily armed [[guerrilla warfare|guerrilla]]s killing at least nine police officers in the latest in a string of ambushes.  In the last week, the country has been battered by an onslaught from insurgents, who are believed to be a mix of guerrillas from the ousted [[Taliban]] regime, [[al-Qaida]] fighters and supporters of renegade warlord [[Gulbuddin Hekmatyar]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.deepikaglobal.com/ENG4_sub.asp?ccode=ENG4&newscode=19401 |title=World News Detail |publisher=deepikaglobal.com |date= |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
* The [[International Survivors Action Committee]] releases a 152-page [http://www.isaccorp.com/tbj/tranquilityreport.doc report] on the controversial [[Tranquility Bay]] behavior correction facility.
* [[Natural disaster]]: Start of the Booth and Bear Butte [[forest fire]]s in the [[Cascade Range|Cascade Mountains]], the worst fire in [[Oregon]] of this year. Within three days the resort community of [[Camp Sherman, Oregon|Camp Sherman]] is evacuated, affecting 1,500 residents and campers, closure of US highway 20 over [[Santiam Pass]], and burning at least 41,000 acres (170&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>).
