* [[2006 United Nations Security Council election|UN Security Council election]]: The [[United Nations]] [[UNGA|General Assembly]] elects [[Belgium]], [[Indonesia]], [[Italy]] and [[South Africa]] to two-year terms on the [[UNSC|Security Council]], commencing 1 January 2007. The fifth seat remains deadlocked after ten rounds of voting between [[Guatemala]] and [[Venezuela]] and may be thrown open to other candidates from [[Latin America and the Caribbean]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/6053984.stm (BBC)]
* [[United Nations Secretary-General]] [[Kofi Annan]] condemns movement by [[Eritrea]] of 1,500 troops and 14 tanks into the [[United Nations Mission in Ethiopia and Eritrea|UNMEE]]-monitored [[Temporary Security Zone]] with [[Ethiopia]] as a "major breach" of the ceasefire agreement that ended the [[Eritrean-Ethiopian War]]. [http://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory?id=2573493&CMP=OTC-RSSFeeds0312 (ABC News)]
* [[2006 North Korean nuclear test]]: The [[United States]] confirms that [[North Korea]] conducted a [[nuclear test]] on October 9, 2006. [http://www.foxnews.com/story/0,2933,221182,00.html (FOX News)]
* The government of [[Sudan]] and the [[Eastern Front (Sudan)|Eastern Front]] rebels sign a [[peace treaty]] in [[Asmara]], [[Eritrea]]. [http://irinnews.org/report.asp?ReportID=55962 (IRIN)]
* The [[Industrial and Commercial Bank of China]] starts its dual [[initial public offering]] (IPO) in [[Hong Kong Stock Exchange]] and [[Shanghai Stock Exchange]], in what would be the world's largest ever IPO. [http://www.iht.com/articles/ap/2006/10/16/business/AS_FIN_China_ICBC.php (AP via International Herald Tribune)]
* Suspected [[LTTE|Tamil Tiger]] rebels detonate a truck packed with explosives amongst a convoy of buses carrying [[Sri Lankan Navy]] personnel in the country's northeast. Approximately 102 people are killed, and 150 people are wounded. [http://news.yahoo.com/s/afp/20061016/ts_afp/srilankaunrest_061016140027 (AFP via Yahoo! News)]
* [[Sweden|Swedish]] Minister for Culture [[Cecilia Stegö Chilò]] resigns after 11 days in office, the second resignation within the [[Cabinet of Fredrik Reinfeldt]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/6054814.stm (BBC)]
* The government of [[Hong Kong]] will not appeal a court ruling striking down the territory's [[sodomy law]]. [http://www.365gay.com/Newscon06/10/101606hongKong.htm (365gay.com)]
* American and Russian scientists announce the discovery of a new [[chemical element]] with the [[atomic number]] 118, [[Systematic element name|temporarily designated]] as [[Ununoctium]]. [http://abcnews.go.com/US/wireStory?id=2574354 (ABC News)]
* [[Uzbek President]] [[Islam Karimov]] fires Saidullo Begaliev, Governor of [[Andijan Province|Andijan]], for "short-sighted policies" and "lack of attention to the people's needs" that led to the [[Andijan massacre]] in 2005. Karimov appoints Ahmad Usmonov as Begaliev's replacement. [http://www.rferl.org/featuresarticle/2006/10/c1119594-cdda-4dfc-a1db-a4c6d3c161f1.html (RadioFreeEurope/RadioLiberty)]
