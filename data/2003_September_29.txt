* Abdalla Yones, who was convicted of murder for killing his daughter, [[Heshu Yones]], for dating a Christian, is sentenced to life in prison after becoming the first person in Britain to admit an "[[honour killing]]".<ref>http://news.independent.co.uk/uk/crime/story.jsp?story=448384</ref>
* [[Terrorism]]: Pakistan dismisses and condemns [[al-Qaida]] [[terrorist network]] threat against [[President]] [[Pervez Musharraf]], saying the [[War on Terrorism|war against terrorism]] will continue. [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Pakistan)|Pakistan Foreign Ministry]] spokesman [[Masood Khan]] states (in [[Islamabad]]) Pakistan will not be deterred by such threats. The identity of the speaker on the [[Compact Cassette|audio tape]] or the authenticity of the tape has not been verified. The message was attributed to al-Qaida's second-ranking leader, [[Ayman al-Zawahri]], and was aired on [[Al Jazeera]] and [[Al-Arabiya]].<ref>[http://www.voanews.com/article.cfm?objectID=E6D30B0D-AFC3-4A1B-8688F94380F80B7A ]{{dead link|date=December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/middle_east/3150050.stm|title=BBC NEWS - Middle East - Pakistan condemns 'al-Qaeda tape'|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/storydisplay.cfm?storyID=3526177&thesection=news&thesubsection=world|title=NZ Herald: New Zealand's Latest News, Business, Sport, Weather, Travel, Technology, Entertainment, Politics, Finance, Health, Environment and Science|work=The New Zealand Herald|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[Outer space|Space]] – [[Technology]]: [[NASA]] outlines plans for the [[Space Shuttle]]'s Replacement, a "''Space Taxi''". The next-generation space vehicle is on the drawing boards now and NASA has just issued newly defined requirements.<ref>[http://abcnews.go.com/sections/scitech/US/nextgeneration030929.html ]{{dead link|date=December 2015}}</ref>
* [[2003 occupation of Iraq timeline|Occupation of Iraq]]: [[Iraq]]i [[Iraqi Security Forces|security force]]s and United States [[military police]] in [[Tikrit]] launch a hunt for [[guerrilla warfare|guerrillas]] behind a series of deadly attacks on [[coalition]] troops&mdash;the largest-ever joint military operation to date. During the raids, dozens of soldiers from the [[United States Army]]'s [[720th Military Police Battalion]] backed up over 200 Iraqi police. The raids netted 92 people and [[weapon]]s that included [[Kalashnikov rifle]]s, [[Mortar (weapon)|mortar]]s, firing tubes, 155&nbsp;mm [[artillery]] [[shell (projectile)|shell]]s and [[Shoulder-launched missile weapon|rocket launchers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/0,2933,98543,00.html|title=One Soldier Dead, 3 Wounded in Roadside Attacks|work=Fox News|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[Nuclear weapon]]s: [[Iran]]ian official confirm traces of highly enriched [[uranium]] found in the country at the [[Kalaye Electric Company]] near [[Tehran]] (this was the second time such a discovery was made by [[United Nations]] inspectors). Iran's ambassador to the [[International Atomic Energy Agency]], [[Ali Akbar Salehi]], attributes the find to the contamination of imported equipment on state television. Iranians have allegedly used Kalaye Electric Company to test centrifuges used to make highly enriched uranium that can be used to make atomic bombs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spacewar.com/2003/030929103544.xaohm76w.html|title=Iran says enriched uranium found by inspectors was on imported equipment|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[International relations]]: European Union increases pressure on [[Tehran]] to cooperate with international [[nuclear weapons]] inspectors. Britain states [[Iran]] must declare "''unequivocally''" that it harbours no ambitions to develop nuclear arms. European Union, in a draft joint statement, warns of economic fallout if Tehran does not make progress on key areas including [[Nuclear proliferation|non-proliferation]], fighting [[terrorism]], [[human rights]] and the [[Middle East peace process]].<ref>[http://www.eubusiness.com/afp/030929125811.76c6zl5l ]{{dead link|date=December 2015}}</ref>
* [[International relations]]: Former [[US Secretary of State]], [[Madeleine Albright]] (commenting on [[European Union]] relations to the United States), says that current transatlantic relations are in a dangerous "vicious circle". She states that the European Union is not a counterweight to American power in the world. She also states that there is an American "''[[Catch-22 (logic)|catch-22]]''", and that America is criticized no matter which foreign policy it adopts.<ref>[http://www.euobserver.com/index.phtml?sid=9&aid=12837 ]{{dead link|date=December 2015}}</ref>
* United Kingdom: The British [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] is holding its annual conference in [[Bournemouth]] on the English south coast.  For the party and especially [[Tony Blair]] it is a tough conference as his policies (especially over Iraq) are under heavy attack.
* [[European Union]]: Member states clash over the issue of how many MEPs should represent the European Parliament on the approaching [[Intergovernmental Conference]] on October 4. The developing consensus seems to be that at least one representative from the two major parties in the [[European Parliament]] will attend the conference, but this procedure is highly controversial—normally parliamentarians do not attend high level meetings among EU leaders.<ref>[http://www.euobserver.com/index.phtml?sid=9&aid=12853 ]{{dead link|date=December 2015}}</ref>
* [[Election]]s: Despite the [[Power outage|blackout]] and other damage caused by [[Hurricane Juan]], [[Prince Edward Island]]ers go to the polls during the [[Prince Edward Island general election, 2003]], re-electing the [[Progressive Conservative Party of Prince Edward Island|Tories]] under Premier [[Pat Binns]]. The [[Liberal Party of Prince Edward Island|Liberals]] receive four seats.<ref name="cbc.ca"/>
* [[Election]]s: [[Newfoundland and Labrador]] premier [[Roger Grimes]] calls [[Newfoundland and Labrador general election, 2003|a general election]] for [[October 2003|October 21]]. Grimes' [[Liberal Party of Newfoundland and Labrador|Liberals]] are seeking reelection against the [[Progressive Conservative Party of Newfoundland and Labrador|Tories]], [[Newfoundland and Labrador New Democratic Party|NDP]], and the [[Labrador Party]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cbc.ca/nlvotes2003/|title=CBC - Newfoundland and Labrador Votes 2003|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
