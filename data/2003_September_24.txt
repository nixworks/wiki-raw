* [[Sweden|Swedish]] police arrests a new suspect in the murder of [[Anna Lindh]]. [[Per-Olof Svensson]] is no longer a suspect and has been released.
* Belgium's highest [[court]], the [[Court of Cassation (Belgium)|Court of Cassation]], throws out case against [[Prime Minister]] [[Ariel Sharon]] and [[Israel]]i [[General]] [[Amos Yaron]]. Bush]] (for [[war crime]]s in [[Iraq]]) and [[Secretary of State]] [[Colin Powell]] is dismissed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml?type=topNews&storyID=3498968|title=Breaking News, Top News & Latest News Headlines - Reuters.com|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.haaretz.com/hasen/spages/343928.html|title=Belgium's Highest Court Throws Out Case Against Sharon|work=Haaretz.com|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[Israeli-Palestinian conflict]]: A protest letter by a group of 27 Israeli pilots to  the [[Israeli air force]] is publicized. In the letter, the pilots announce their refusal to fly further missions to bomb leaders of Palestinian terrorist groups in civilian areas. The pilots' letter calls the attacks "illegal and immoral". It draws quick condemnation from commentators, from politicians and from military leaders, with calls for severe punishment including jail, although a dismissal is considered the most likely result. The pilots' protest is a reaction to attacks like the one on [[Hamas]] leader [[Salah Shehade]] in July 2002, which killed Shehade, his bodyguard and 15 civilians, among them nine children.,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/articles/2003/09/25/1064083125257.html|title=Israel vows to punish rebel pilots for act of conscience|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://asia.reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml?type=topNews&storyID=3509703|title=Business & Financial News, Breaking US & International News - Reuters.com|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.msnbc.com/news/971397.asp?cp1=1|title=MSNBC|work=MSNBC|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[Computer and Communications Industry Association]] report, written by a handful of security experts, [[Microsoft]]'s dominance in key [[technology|technologies]] poses security risk and threatens the [[national infrastructure]]. Computer and Communications Industry Association states reliance on a single technology, such as the [[Microsoft Windows|Windows]] [[operating system]], threatens economic security and critical infrastructure. The paper warns that many security improvements planned by Microsoft are likely designed to deter customers from switching to another operating system.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://archive.is/20130120022654/http://news.com.com/2100-1029_3-5081214.html|title=Report: Microsoft dominance poses security risk|work=archive.is|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* After several postponements the [[European Parliament]] finally passes a [[European Union directive|directive]] concerning the "[[patent]]ability of [[computer]]-implemented [[invention]]s". The final text differs substantially from the original proposal and is seen as going a long way in addressing the concerns that it would legalize [[software patent|patents on software]] and business methods. The directive should now be under review by the [[Council of the European Union]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.zdnet.co.uk/business/legal/0,39020651,39116642,00.htm|title=Latest Topics|work=ZDNet|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theregister.co.uk/content/4/33016.html|title=European Parliament castrates software patent regs|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* Judge [[Lee Roy West]] of the [[United States District Court for the Western District of Oklahoma]] rules that the [[Federal Trade Commission]] lacks the authority to create the [[National Do Not Call Registry]]. The ruling came in response to a [[Lawsuit|suit]] filed by the [[telemarketing]] industry.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/2003/09/25/cx_da_0925topnews.html|title=Do-Not-Call List Likely To Call Back|author=Dan Ackman|date=25 September 2003|work=Forbes|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
