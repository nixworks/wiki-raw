* The [[United Nations]] sides with the United States on voting in [[Iraq]]. [[Secretary-General of the United Nations|Secretary-General]] [[Kofi Annan]] and other UN officials call direct elections in advance of July 2004 impractical, due to continuing disorder in Iraq and other factors.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.washtimes.com/world/20040115-112502-3479r.htm|title=U.N. sides with U.S. on voting in Iraq|work=The Washingtion Times|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>

* South Korea's foreign minister [[Yoon Young-kwan]] resigns after a controversy in which his ministry was accused of diverging from the government's policy of increased independence from the United States.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/worldlatest/story/0,1280,-3627495,00.html|title=World news and comment from the Guardian - The Guardian|work=the Guardian|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* The [[European Union]] asks the [[World Trade Organization]] for authorization to impose [[trade sanctions]] against the United States in response to the U.S.'s anti-[[dumping (pricing policy)|dumping]] scheme, which has been ruled illegal by the WTO.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/wto/article/0,2763,1123880,00.html|title=EU seeks sanctions against US|author=Mark Tran|work=the Guardian|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[Canadian federal election, 2004]]: Former [[Ontario]] Health Minister [[Tony Clement]] declares his candidacy for the leadership of the [[Conservative Party of Canada]].  Also confirmed is the candidacy of auto parts magnate [[Belinda Stronach]], who will announce her entry into the leadership race next week.<ref>[http://www.cbc.ca/stories/2004/01/15/conservatives_clement040115 ]{{dead link|date=December 2015}}</ref>
* Italy has indicted three Germans, all former members of an [[Waffen-SS|SS]] [[Waffen-SS Order of Battle|Panzergrenadier Division]], on charges of massacring 560 people in 1944 in the Italian village of [[Sant'Anna di Stazzema]].  The three Germans, [[Gerhard Sommer]], 83; [[Alfred Schonenberg]], 83; and [[Ludwig Sonntag]], 80, are currently living in Germany. It is not clear whether Italy will request the three men's [[extradition]] from Germany.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.israelnationalnews.com/news.php3?id=56139|title=Three SS Officers Indicted In Italy|work=Arutz Sheva|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>

* [[Exploration of Mars]]: The [[MER-A|Spirit Rover]] has rolled off its lander to start its exploration. The first journey was only three metres. It took the rover about 78 seconds, ending with the back of the rover being 0.7 metres from the lander.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://spaceflightnow.com/mars/mera/040115drive.html|title=Spaceflight Now - Destination Mars - Six-wheeling on Mars: Spirit rover drives off lander|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* The [[World Wide Web Consortium|World Wide Web Consortium (W3C)]] announces the release of the [[Composite Capability/Preference Profiles]] (CC/PP 1.0). W3C's new standard profiling language CC/PP provides a [[Internet standard|standard]]ized format of the description of information that will allow [[WWW|Web]]-enabled devices to effectively communicate their capabilities to the desired [[Server (computing)|server]] and aids in delivering Web content to broad range of devices.
