;Science and technology
*[[Human impact on the environment]]
**In a ''[[Scientific Reports]]'' publication, researchers propose 1965 as the start of the [[Anthropocene]] era. In that year, human [[nuclear weapons testing]] caused a noticeable spike in [[radiocarbon]] in the [[heartwood]] of the world's [[Campbell Island, New Zealand#Remotest tree|remotest tree]], a [[Picea sitchensis|Sitka spruce]] on [[Campbell Island, New Zealand|Campbell Island]], [[New Zealand]]. The general scientific community has already been using [[1950]] as the year "[[Before Present|Before Physics]]", when nuclear weapons began to significantly affect the reliability of radiocarbon dating of events that happen after that epoch. [http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sciencetech/article-5401139/amp/The-year-humans-began-causing-irreversible-damage-Earth.html (''Daily Mail'')]
