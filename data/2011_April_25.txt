*[[Guantanamo Bay files leak|Guantanamo Files]]: [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/guantanamo-files (''The Guardian'')] [http://wikileaks.ch/gitmo/ (WikiLeaks)] [http://english.aljazeera.net/news/americas/2011/04/201142561524783918.html (Al Jazeera)]
**[[WikiLeaks]] releases [[Classified information|classified]] cables detailing the interrogations carried out by the United States at the [[Guantanamo Bay detention camp]], as well as the imprisonment in the camp of [[Demography of Afghanistan|Afghans]] and [[Demographics of Pakistan|Pakistanis]], children, elderly and mentally ill, before later being released without charge. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2011/apr/25/guantanamo-files-lift-lid-prison (''The Guardian'')] [http://www.smh.com.au/world/wikileaks-releases-new-guantanamo-files-20110425-1dtne.html (''The Sydney Morning Herald'')]
**The cables show the United States relied on the internationally widely available [[Casio F91W]] digital watch as "the sign of al-Qaida" and as "evidence" to imprison its captives in the [[Guantanamo Bay detention camp]]. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2011/apr/25/guantanamo-files-casio-wristwatch-alqaida (''The Guardian'')]
**Staff at Guantánamo Bay were instructed that any Muslim traveling to Afghanistan after 11 September 2001 was likely to have gone there "to support [[Osama bin Laden]] through direct hostilities against the US forces", with any other reasons being dismissed as "total fabrications", making it difficult for the interrogated to plead their innocence. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2011/apr/25/guantanamo-files-interrogation-muslim-travel (''The Guardian'')]
**Details of U.S. collaboration with at least 10 foreign [[Intelligence agency|intelligence agencies]] emerge, with [[China|Chin]]ese, Tunisian, Moroccan, Russian, Saudi, [[Tajikistan|Tajik]], Jordanian, Algerian, Yemeni and Kuwaiti delegations assisting the U.S. with interrogations at the [[Guantanamo Bay detention camp]], and China and Russia vowing to prosecute and punish any repatriated [[Uyghur people|Uighur]]s or [[Uzbeks]]. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2011/apr/25/guantanamo-files-china-interrogated-prisoners (''The Guardian'')]
**A British resident, an organiser of [[hunger strike]]s imprisoned for nine years without trial and whose release has been repeatedly requested by [[William Hague]], remains locked up in the [[Guantanamo Bay detention camp]]. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2011/apr/25/guantanamo-files-shaker-aamer-british (''The Guardian'')]
**Details of how an al-Qaeda-linked militant duped Canadian intelligence agents also emerge. [https://www.theglobeandmail.com/news/world/wikileaks-unearths-case-of-al-qaeda-double-agent-who-duped-canadians/article1997237/ (''The Globe and Mail'')]
**It is disclosed that an [[Al Jazeera]] journalist imprisoned by the United States at Guantánamo for six years was interrogated about the news network. He claims to have been beaten and sexually assaulted. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2011/apr/25/guantanamo-files-sami-al-hajj?intcmp=239 (''The Guardian'')]
**The controversial detention in the [[Guantanamo Bay detention camp]] of anti-extremist author [[Abdul Badr Mannan]] emerges. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2011/apr/25/guantanamo-files-framed-author-mannan (''The Guardian'')]
**It emerges that the [[Federal government of the United States|U.S. government]] released dozens of Guantánamo inmates it regarded as "high risk" and that one of the rebels it is backing in the ongoing 2011 [[2011 Libyan Civil War|Libyan Civil War]] fought for the [[Taliban]] against the [[Soviet Union]] and served as [[Osama bin Laden]]'s driver in [[Sudan]].  [http://www.straitstimes.com/BreakingNews/World/Story/STIStory_660921.html (''The Straits Times'')] 
**The U.S. government "strongly condemns" international [[Media (communication)|media]] outlets, specifically ''[[The New York Times]]'', for publishing the files it had wanted to keep secret. [http://www.jpost.com/International/Article.aspx?id=217764 (''The Jerusalem Post'')]
