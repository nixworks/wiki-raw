;Armed conflicts and attacks
*[[Iraq War (2014–present)]]
**An [[Iraqi Air Force]] [[Sukhoi Su-25]] jet, returning from a bombing raid against [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|ISIL]] militants in the north, accidentally drops a bomb on [[Baghdad]] destroying several homes and killing at least 7 civilians. American officials claimed that as many as 65% of Iraqi air force bombers do not drop their bombs due to "lack of targets". [http://news.sky.com/story/1514106/iraqi-warplane-accidentally-bombs-baghdad (Sky News)]
*[[Yemeni Civil War (2015)]]
**An air-strike by the [[Saudi Arabian-led intervention in Yemen|Saudi-led coalition]] on the southern port city of [[Aden, Yemen|Aden]] kills at least 30 people. It is unclear how many of the dead are civilians or [[Houthis|Houthi]] rebels. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-33408473 (BBC)]

*[[Greek bailout referendum, 2015]]
**[[Yanis Varoufakis]] resigns as the [[Ministry of Finance (Greece)|Greek Minister of Finance]] despite the success of the "no" vote which supported his views. [http://www.afr.com/markets/equity-markets/greece-debt-crisis-yanis-varoufakis-stands-down-as-finance-minister-20150706-gi66ui (AFR)]
**[[Prime Minister of Greece|Greek Prime Minister]] [[Alexis Tsipras]] calls [[President of Russia|Russian President]] [[Vladimir Putin]] in the wake of the referendum; Putin expresses his support for the [[Greek people]] in the call. Many [[Western world|western]] officials express concerns that, like Egypt, Greece may increase its ties to Russia at the expense of the west. Greece is a member of the [[NATO]] military alliance. [http://finance.yahoo.com/news/latest-tsipras-calls-putin-wake-121828444.html (Yahoo)]
;Sports
*In [[auto racing]], [[Dale Earnhardt Jr.]] wins the [[NASCAR]] [[2015 Coke Zero 400]] at the [[Daytona International Speedway]] after a four-hour rain delay. On the final lap, a major crash sent [[Austin Dillon]] into the catch fence in front of the grandstands, causing minor injuries to five fans. Dillon himself walked away unhurt. [http://espn.go.com/racing/nascar/cup/story/_/id/13204963/daytona-wreck-damages-catch-fence-mars-finish-coke-zero-400 (ESPN)]
*[[2015 Tour de France]]
**In cycling, the [[2015 Tour de France]] race is neutralized 100km into the third stage due to a major crash involving about 30 riders. [[General classification in the Tour de France|Yellow jersey]] leader [[Fabian Cancellara]] is injured in the crash and is forced to withdraw from the Tour. The race is eventually stopped 50km from the finish with Briton [[Chris Froome]] taking the yellow jersey. [http://www.news24.com/sport24/OtherSport/TourdeFrance/Horror-crash-mars-Tour-de-France-Stage-3-20150706 (News24)] [https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/cycling/33416098 (BBC)]
*In [[boxing]], the [[World Boxing Organization]] strips [[Floyd Mayweather Jr.]] of the [[welterweight]] title he won in his fight with [[Manny Pacquiao]] for failing to pay a US$200,000 fee in time and failing to vacate a [[Light middleweight]] title he also held. [http://www.msn.com/en-au/sport/more-sports/mayweather-stripped-of-title-he-won-in-pacquiao-fight/ar-AAcEqOk?ocid=UP94DHP (Reuters via MSN)]
