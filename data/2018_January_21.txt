*[[2018 Inter-Continental Hotel Kabul attack]]
**Six insurgents kill at least 18 people—4 [[Afghanistan|Afghans]] and 14 foreigners—in a 13-hour gun battle. The [[Taliban]] claim that they sent five suicide bombers armed with hand grenades and AK-47 assault rifles. *[[Democratic Republic of the Congo general election, 2018|Democratic Republic of the Congo general election]]
**The [[Joseph Kabila|Kabila government]] begins [[Internet#Access|blocking internet access]] in the capital [[Kinshasa]] at midnight, after a [[Catholic lay organisations|Catholic lay group]]'s call to march today, in defiance of a government ban on public demonstrations. A joint statement was released by the [[List of diplomatic missions of the United States#Africa|American]] and [[List of diplomatic missions of the United Kingdom#Africa|British]] embassies on [[Twitter]] the day prior, applauding "Congolese citizens exercising their constitutional right to assemble peacefully in support of the full implementation" of the [[December 2016 Congolese protests#Events|Sylvester agreements]] of December 2016. The statement also said that those who do not protect these fundamental human rights must be held accountable "at the highest level". [https://www.hln.be/nieuws/buitenland/congolese-regering-legt-internet-in-kinshasa-plat~af69ccdd (''Het Laatste Nieuws'')], [https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2018-01-21/congo-police-fire-tear-gas-to-disperse-demonstrators (AP via ''U.S. News & World Report'')], [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-congo-protests/congolese-security-forces-kill-six-while-dispersing-anti-kabila-protest-idUSKBN1FA0KJ (Reuters)] 
**Congolese security forces kill at least six protesters and fire tear gas in Kinshasa. 57 people are wounded and dozens of arrests are made in protests across the country, notably in [[Mbuji-Mayi]], [[Goma]], [[Lubumbashi]] and several other places. [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-congo-protests/congolese-security-forces-kill-three-while-dispersing-anti-kabila-protest-idUSKBN1FA0KJ (Reuters)]
*[[United States federal government shutdown of 2018]]
**[[President of the United States|U.S. President]] [[Donald Trump]] says on Twitter that if the shutdown stalemate continues, Republicans should consider the "[[nuclear option]]" [[parliamentary procedure]] in the Senate. Majority leader [[Mitch McConnell]] is opposed to taking this step,  a spokesperson said. [https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-01-21/key-day-could-yield-fast-deal-or-long-impasse-shutdown-update (Bloomberg)] [https://www.cnbc.com/2018/01/21/mcconnell-opposes-shutdown-nuclear-option-trump-urged-in-tweet.html (CNBC)]
**The [[United States Senate]] schedules a  vote for noon  Monday to  fund the government through February 8. Since the Senate did not reach a budget compromise late Sunday evening, hundreds of thousands of federal workers will face [[furlough]]s on Monday and many federal government offices will remain closed though some facilities, such as the [[List of Smithsonian museums|Smithsonian Museums]] and the [[National Zoological Park (United States)|National Zoo]], are scheduled to be open.
