*[[Arab Spring]]:
**[[Tunisian revolution]]: Thousands of people demonstrate in [[Tunis]] to demand both the resignation of interim prime minister [[Beji Caid el Sebsi]], a link with the old guard brought down in January's popular revolution, and the prosecution of [[Zine El Abidine Ben Ali]], who fled to [[Saudi Arabia]] after being ousted, on charges such as murder and drug-trafficking. [http://www.presstv.ir/detail/176537.html (Press TV)]
**[[2011 Syrian protests]]: Police and soldiers open fire from rooftops in [[Jabla]], killing and injuring nearby people; no protest was taking place at the time. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-13180847 (BBC)] [http://www.cnn.com/2011/WORLD/meast/04/24/syria.unrest/index.html (CNN)] [http://english.aljazeera.net/news/middleeast/2011/04/2011424181530901869.html (Al Jazeera)]
**[[2011 Moroccan protests]]: Thousands of people participate in massive peaceful demonstrations against the government on streets across Morocco, calling for an end to corruption and torture. [http://www.presstv.ir/detail/176480.html (Press TV)] [http://english.aljazeera.net/video/africa/2011/04/201142512013759717.html (Al Jazeera)]
**[[2011 Saudi Arabian protests]]: Amid demonstrations by the unemployed, women protesters gather to demand a vote but are defeated and rounded up by authorities. [http://www.presstv.ir/detail/176474.html (Press TV)]
**[[2011 Yemeni protests]]: Mass protests continue nationwide in defiance of the Saleh regime's claims the country's leader is to soon resign from his 32-year rule; protesters demand his immediate removal from power.
