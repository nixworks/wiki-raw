*[[National Basketball Association criticisms and controversies|National Basketball Association controversies]]
**The office of the [[United States Attorney for the Southern District of New York]] announces that 10 individuals, including assistant coaches at four [[National Collegiate Athletic Association|NCAA]] [[NCAA Division I|Division I]] men's [[basketball]] programs—[[Arizona Wildcats men's basketball|Arizona]], [[Auburn Tigers men's basketball|Auburn]], [[Oklahoma State Cowboys basketball|Oklahoma State]], and [[USC Trojans men's basketball|USC]]—have been arrested on federal corruption charges. The accused are alleged to be part of a scheme by which coaches accepted bribes to steer [[National Basketball Association|NBA]]-bound college players toward certain [[Sports agent|agents]] and financial advisers. Court documents also allege that an apparel company paid $100,000 to the family of an unnamed player to ensure his signing with an unnamed Division I school, which was later confirmed as the [[Louisville Cardinals men's basketball|University of Louisville]]. [http://www.espn.com/mens-college-basketball/story/_/id/20824193/ncaa-basketball-coaches-10-charged-fraud-corruption (ESPN)]
