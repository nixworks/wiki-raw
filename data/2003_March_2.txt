* Four [[Korean People's Air Force|North Korean fighter jets]] intercept a United States [[reconnaissance plane]] over [[international waters]] in the [[Sea of Japan]]
* [[Iraq disarmament crisis]]: Iraq destroys six more [[Al-Samoud 2]] missiles, bringing the total destroyed to 10 out of an estimated 100 missiles ordered eliminated by the U.N. The U.S. continues to dismiss Iraq's actions as "part of its game of deception." Iraq indicates that it may halt destruction of the missiles if the U.S. indicates it will go to war anyway.
* The British newspaper ''[[The Observer|Observer]]'' publishes what it claims to be a leaked memo<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.observer.co.uk/iraq/story/0,12239,905954,00.html |title=US plan to bug Security Council: the text &#124; World news &#124; The Guardian |publisher=Observer.co.uk |date= |accessdate=2015-10-24}}</ref> from a high-ranking [[NSA]] official dated January 31, 2003. In it are orders to spy on the domestic and official communications of the [[United Nations Security Council]] members other than the U.S. and the United Kingdom. The memo names "... members [[Angola]], [[Cameroon]], [[Chile]], [[Bulgaria]] and [[Guinea]],&nbsp;..." as candidates for special attention.<ref>{{cite web|author=Martin Bright, Ed Vulliamy in New York and Peter Beaumont |url=http://www.observer.co.uk/iraq/story/0,12239,905936,00.html |title=Revealed: US dirty tricks to win vote on Iraq war &#124; World news &#124; The Guardian |publisher=Observer.co.uk |date= |accessdate=2015-10-24}}</ref>
* French president [[Jacques Chirac]] starts a three-day visit to the former French colony [[Algeria]]. It is the first visit of a French president to Algeria at the highest ceremonial level.
