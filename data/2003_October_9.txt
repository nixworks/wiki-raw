* ''Time'' magazine reports that [[Yassir Arafat]], whose health has led to confused reporting over the past days, with him variously reported as having had flu and having had a heart attack, in actuality has [[stomach cancer]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.time.com/time/world/article/0,8599,516827,00.html|title=Arafat's Illness|date=9 October 2003|work=TIME.com|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[Pakistan]] successfully [[test fire]]s a medium-range, [[nuclear weapon|nuclear]]-capable [[missile]], the second such [[Quality control|test]] in less than a [[week]], the [[Pakistan army]] states. The [[Hatf-4 missile]], also known as the [[Shaheen 1 missile|Shaheen 1]], was fired off, according to the [[army]]. The missile has a range of 435 miles, meaning it can hit most major targets in India. The test followed a similar launching on Friday of the short-range [[Hatf-2 Ghaznavi]] after which Pakistan said it was in the middle of a series of such tests. Pakistani army [[spokesman]] [[Major General]] [[Shaukat Sultan]] said India had been told of each of the launches beforehand and he states the tests should not affect the [[international relations]] between the two neighbors.<ref>[http://www.lasvegassun.com/sunbin/stories/news/2003/oct/08/100807434.html] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080101233755/http://www.lasvegassun.com/sunbin/stories/news/2003/oct/08/100807434.html |date=January 1, 2008 }}</ref>
* [[Israeli-Palestinian conflict]]: Defense Minister [[Shaul Mofaz]] ordered dispatches of [[Israel]]i [[troop]] reinforcements to the [[Palestinian Area]]s, [[West Bank]] and [[Gaza Strip]], and weighed a call-up of reserves, citing new warnings about planned attacks by Palestinian militants. The Israeli military also extended a two-[[week]] [[embargo|lockdown]] on Palestinians' travel within the West Bank and Gaza in what it states as a bid to prevent further attacks.<ref>[http://www.lasvegassun.com/sunbin/stories/news/2003/oct/08/100807921.html] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080101233800/http://www.lasvegassun.com/sunbin/stories/news/2003/oct/08/100807921.html |date=January 1, 2008 }}</ref> Meanwhile, prime minister Ahmed Qurei is reported to have declined to form a government and told President Yasser Arafat he wants to quit his post.<ref>[http://www.rte.ie/aertel/p113.htm] {{dead link|date=August 2016|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref>
