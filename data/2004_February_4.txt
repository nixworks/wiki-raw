* A three-judge panel of the [[United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit]] in San Francisco upholds an August 2003 decision by [[United States magistrate judge|U.S. Magistrate Judge]] John Jelderks in [[Portland, Oregon|Portland]] that the remains of the [[Kennewick Man]] can be studied. In a decision was written by Judge [[Ronald M. Gould]], the court denies the request of the [[Nez Perce tribe|Nez Perce]], [[Confederated Tribes of the Umatilla Indian Reservation|Umatilla]], [[Colville Indian Reservation|Colville]], and [[Yakama]] [[Indian tribe]]s, which wanted the bones to be turned over to them for burial, holding that the remains do not fall under the [[Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act]] and can be studied under the [[Archaeological Resources Protection Act]].<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=https://web.archive.org/20040207030219/http://seattletimes.nwsource.com:80/html/localnews/2001851129_kennewick05m.html |title=The Seattle Times: Local News: Scientists can study Kennewick remains, U.S. appeals court rules |publisher=Web.archive.org |date=2004-02-05 |accessdate=2015-10-25}}</ref>

* The [[U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission]] held an open meeting in what may be the longest-running SEC fraud case. At issue are the trades that resulted in the firing of Orlando (Joseph) Jett from [[Kidder, Peabody & Co.]] in 1994. An [[administrative law judge]] held that Jett was responsible for record keeping violations, but rejected the claim of SEC lawyers that he was guilty of securities fraud. Both the staff and Jett appealed to the full commission.
