* [[British Airways]] and [[Air France]] simultaneously announce that they will retire the supersonic [[Concorde]] aircraft. Passenger numbers had never recovered following a crash that killed 113 in 2000.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/2934257.stm |title=UK &#124; Concorde grounded for good |publisher=BBC News |date=2003-04-10 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref> In response, Sir [[Richard Branson]] offers to buy British Airways' Concordes for £1 for the use of his [[Virgin Atlantic Airways|Virgin Atlantic]] Airlines. BA dismisses the offer as a stunt and indicates that the planes will go to air museums.<ref>[http://www.news24.com/News24/Finance/Companies/0,,2-8-24_1346143,00.html]{{dead link|date=September 2015}}</ref>
* A fire destroys a [[boarding school]] for the deaf in [[Makhachkala]], Russia, killing 28 children, aged eight to 14. About 100 other children suffer burns and smoke inhalation, 39 of which are in serious condition.<ref>[http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&u=/ap/20030410/ap_on_re_eu/russia_school_fire&cid=518&ncid=716]{{dead link|date=August 2015}}</ref>
