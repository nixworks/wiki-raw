;Armed conflicts and attacks
*[[In Aménas hostage crisis]]:
**An [[Algeria]]n official reports that a group of hostages has escaped from the [[natural gas]] complex that was taken over by [[Islamist]] militants. It is reported as many as 20 escaped, including [[Americans]]. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-21054521 (BBC)] [http://www.latimes.com/news/nationworld/world/la-20-foreign-hostages-escape-islamist-captors-in-algeria-20130117,0,7004240.story (''Los Angeles Times'')] [http://www.foxnews.com/world/2013/01/17/us-france-reportedly-in-talks-with-algeria-over-hostage-standoff/ (Fox News)]
**The [[Algerian Army]] assaults the complex with helicopter gunships, reportedly killing 15 kidnappers and as many as 34 hostages. According to local sources, at least 7 hostages remain alive and in captivity. [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/01/201311713160194432.html (Al Jazeera)]
**Algerian news agency ANP reports that during the [[Algerian Army]] assault 600 hostages were freed. Terrorists claim that they killed 35 hostages during the battle and that 15 terrorists were killed. [https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/world/2013/01/17/algeria-hostages/1841343/ (USA Today)]
*[[Syrian civil war]]:
**106 people are massacred in the Basatin al-Hasawiya district of [[Homs]], [[Syria]], including children. People were shot, stabbed, and burned to death by forces loyal to [[Bashar al-Assad]] known as the [[Shabiha]]. [https://www.reuters.com/article/2013/01/17/us-syria-crisis-idUSBRE90G09R20130117 (Reuters)] [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-21073447 (BBC)] [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/middleeast/2013/01/201311716944605506.html (Al Jazeera)] [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2013/jan/17/homs-massacre-syria-basatin-hasawiya (''The Guardian'')]
*[[Northern Mali conflict (2012–present)]]:
**The number of French forces in the country rises to 1,400, more than half of the planned 2,500 strong force, as its troops continue to fight insurgents in [[Battle of Konna|Konna]] and [[Battle of Diabaly|Diabaly]]. [[Chad]] prepares to send the first 200 troops of a 2000-strong force to assist in the military operations as part of a regional mission. The [[European Union]] votes to send 450 to 500 "non-combat" troops to [[Mali]], half of them are trainers. [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/01/2013117114015728807.html (Al Jazeera)]
**The first [[West Africa]]n troops enter [[Mali]]. 100 soldiers from the [[Military of Togo|Togolese military]] enter the country with [[Military of Nigeria|Nigerian military]] due to enter shortly afterward. [https://www.reuters.com/article/2013/01/17/us-mali-rebels-idUSBRE90D0FX20130117 (Reuters)]
*[[Iraqi insurgency (post-U.S. withdrawal)]]:
**Two car bombs explode in the city of [[Dujail]], [[Iraq]], leaving at least 7 dead and 25 wounded. [http://news.xinhuanet.com/english/world/2013-01/17/c_132109650.htm (Xinhua)]
**A bomb detonated on a bus carrying pilgrims in [[Iraq]]. In a separate incident a roadside bomb detonated and injures two pilgrims walking to [[Hussein ibn Ali]]. Another bomb detonated at a bus stop killing 7 people and wounding 28. Also a civilian vehicle was hit by a roadside bomb leaving 2 dead. In total 33 people were killed in one of the most violent days this month. [https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/world/2013/01/17/iraq-car-bomb/1841467/ (AP via USA Today)]
*[[War in Somalia (2009–present)]]:
**Militants belonging to [[Somalia|Somali]] group [[Al-Shabaab (militant group)|al-Shabaab]] announce they have executed the [[France|French]] [[intelligence agent]] who was the target of the unsuccessful French [[Bulo Marer hostage rescue attempt|rescue attempt]] on January 11. [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/01/20131179238178979.html (Al Jazeera)]
