[[April 29]] [[2005]] was a Friday
* The next launch of the [[Space Shuttle Discovery|Space Shuttle ''Discovery'']], [[STS-114]],  is delayed until at least [[July 13]]. This is to be the first Space Shuttle launch since the [[Columbia disaster|''Columbia'' disaster]] in [[February 2003]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/sci/tech/4496955.stm (BBC)] [http://www.cnn.com/2005/TECH/space/04/29/shuttle.delay/index.html (CNN)]
* [[United States|U.S.]] Congressman [[Rob Portman]] was confirmed by the [[United States Senate]] to be [[United States Trade Representative]]. [http://www.marketwatch.com/news/story.asp?guid=%7BD8143D14-B626-4FD8-8D82-1568451035C3%7D&siteid=google (CBS Marketwatch)]
* The death toll in the [[Amagasaki rail crash]] in [[Japan]] totals 106. Rescue efforts are over and police begin the crash investigation. [http://www.japantoday.com/e/?content=news&cat=1&id=335311 (Japan Today)]
* [[Kuomintang]] Chairman [[Lien Chan]] meets with [[Communist Party of China Secretary-General]] [[Hu Jintao]] in the highest level contact between leaders of the two parties since the meeting of [[Chiang Kai-shek]] and [[Mao Zedong]] in August [[1945]] at the end of the [[Second Sino-Japanese War]]. [http://news.ft.com/cms/s/85702204-b864-11d9-bc7c-00000e2511c8.html]
* A prominent article in the state-owned [[China Securities Journal]] says conditions are ripe for changes in the country's policy of keeping the [[Renminbi|yuan]] pegged to the [[U.S. dollar]]. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2005/04/29/AR2005042901705.html (Washington Post)]
* Three currencies join the [[European Exchange Rate Mechanism|ERM]] II: The [[Cyprus pound]], the [[Lat|Latvian lat]], and the [[Maltese Lira]]. The ERM II is one of the steps needed for a currency to become part of the Eurozone. [http://www.eubusiness.com/afp/050429215029.w0uofrf7 EUbusiness]
