* A new biography, reported in ''[[The Sunday Times (UK)|The Sunday Times]]'', claims that the prosecution of [[Oscar Wilde]] for [[sodomy]] was ordered by the then [[Liberal Party (UK)|Liberal]] government, in a deal with the [[John Sholto Douglas, 9th Marquess of Queensberry|Marquess of Queensberry]], the father of [[Lord Alfred Douglas]], to prevent his exposure of a homosexual relationship between another of his sons, Viscount Drumlanrig, and [[British Prime Minister]] [[Archibald Philip Primrose, 5th Earl of Rosebery|Lord Rosebery]]. According to reports, future prime minister [[H. H. Asquith]] played a key role in negotiating the deal, which led to the imprisonment of the Irish writer.{{Citation needed|date=February 2010}}
