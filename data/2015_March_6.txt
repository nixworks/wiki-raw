;Health
*The U.S. [[Food and Drug Administration]] (FDA) approves the country's first [[biosimilar]] drug, [[Filgrastim|Zarxio]] (Europe: 2009, as [[Filgrastim|Zarzio]]), made by [[Novartis]]. Biosimilars are a relatively new class of drugs designed to pave the way for less expensive versions of the complex [[biologics]] drug class, and they are not synthesized from chemicals like other drugs, but are made from living cells. Some well known biologics include [[Remicade]] and [[Enbrel]] for [[autoimmune diseases]], and [[Herceptin]] and [[Avastin]] for cancer. Zarxio, used to prevent infection in cancer patients receiving chemotherapy, is a close copy of [[Neupogen]], made by [[Amgen]]. [https://www.nytimes.com/2015/03/07/health/fda-approves-zarxio-first-biosimilar-drug.html?ref=health (''The New York Times'')]
