* At the International Science Festival at [[Edinburgh]]'s Royal Museum, the [[taxidermy|stuffed]] remains of [[Dolly the sheep]] are for the first time displayed.<ref>[http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story2&cid=573&ncid=757&e=6&;u=/nm/20030409/od_nm/odd_britain_dolly_dc]{{dead link|date=August 2015}}</ref>
* [[Baghdad]] falls to coalition forces. American infantrymen seize deserted [[Ba'ath Party]] ministries and pull down a huge iron statue of [[Saddam Hussein]] at the [[Firdos Square]] in front of the [[Palestine Hotel]], as a symbolic ending his autocratic rule of [[Iraq]]. Baghdad citizens then dragged the severed head of the statue through the streets of the city. Dozens of people there cheer U.S. soldiers, according to BBC. Much looting of cars and buildings is seen in Baghdad and other cities as the government and police lost control.<ref>[http://www.washtimes.com/national/20030410-71572160.htm ]{{dead link|date=September 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/c/a/2003/04/09/MN249161.DTL |title=Baghdad closer to collapse / Army, Marine tanks and troops lay waste to ministries, capture air base, repel Iraqi counterattack / BAGHDAD: The city wonders - when will Americans take over? |publisher=SFGate |date=2003-04-09 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>[http://www.asahi.com/international/update/0411/005.html ]{{dead link|date=September 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/middle_east/2933707.stm |title=Middle East &#124; Baghdad falls to US forces |publisher=BBC News |date=2003-04-09 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.informationclearinghouse.info/article2838.htm |title=A tale of two photos |publisher=Informationclearinghouse.info |date= |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
* The fate of [[Saddam Hussein]] remains unknown after a U.S. [[B-1 Lancer|B-1B bomber]] dropped four 2,000-pound bunker-busting bombs on a building where Hussein was thought to be meeting with his sons and senior aides on April 7. The bombs blew a 60-foot-deep crater in a residential neighborhood that is not under coalition control, refueling speculation about the [[possible death of Saddam Hussein]].  British intelligence officials said that they believed Hussein left the targeted building just minutes before it was destroyed, and that he probably survived the attack.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/article/0,,5944-639777,00.html |title=The Times &#124; UK News, World News and Opinion |publisher=Timesonline.co.uk |date=2013-07-10 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Johnston |first=David |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2003/04/09/international/worldspecial/09INTE.html |title=Hussein Fate After Attack Still Unclear |location=Iraq |publisher=NYTimes.com |date=2003-04-09 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/Iraq/Story/0,2763,932750,00.html |title=Saddam survived attack on building say British intelligence sources &#124; World news |publisher=The Guardian |date= |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/news/nationworld/iraq/inside/la-war-id9apr09,1,3264432.story |title=If Hussein Is Really Dead, Where's the Proof? |publisher=latimes |date=2003-04-09 |accessdate=2015-09-26}}</ref>
