;Science and technology
*A study published in the ''[[Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America|Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences]]'' on [[gender identity]]  reports [[brain]]s do not really fit into "male" or "female" categories. [[Tel Aviv University|Tel Aviv University's]] Daphna Joel's research team, in analyzing the [[Magnetic resonance imaging|MRI scans]] of some 1,400 individuals, found only a very small number of the brains studied had features that were entirely male, female, or intermediate between the two. The vast majority had a [[Mosaic (genetics)|mosaic]]. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/speaking-of-science/wp/2015/12/01/brains-arent-actually-male-or-female-new-study-suggests/?wpmm=1&wpisrc=nl_evening (''The Washington Post'')], [http://www.pnas.org/content/early/2015/11/24/1509654112.abstract (The Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences)], [http://people.socsci.tau.ac.il/mu/daphnajoel/ (Daphna Joel)]
