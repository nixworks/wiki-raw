* In [[Norfolk, Virginia|Norfolk]], Virginia, the [[USS Cole (DDG-67)|USS ''Cole'']] leaves port on the destroyer's first overseas deployment since it was [[USS Cole bombing|bombed in 2000]] in [[Yemen]]'s port at [[Aden]].<ref>http://www.cnn.com/2003/US/11/29/cole.deploys.ap/index.html</ref>
* Police in [[Turkey]] announce the arrest of a yet-unnamed man they state has admitted giving the order to [[suicide bomber]]s to attack [[Bet Israel Synagogue (Istanbul, Turkey)|Beth Israel]] synagogue in [[Istanbul]] on November 15.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cnn.com/2003/WORLD/europe/11/29/turkey.arrest/index.html|title=CNN.com - Arrest over Turkey synagogue blast - Nov. 29, 2003|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[Luan Enjie]], director of the National Aerospace Bureau of the People's Republic of China states that "By 2020, we will achieve visiting the moon."<ref>http://www.cnn.com/2003/TECH/space/11/29/china.moon.ap/index.html</ref>
* [[American occupation of Iraq|Occupation of Iraq]]: A team of eight [[Spain|Spanish]] intelligence agents is attacked south of [[Baghdad]]; seven are killed and one wounded.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newsday.com/news/nationworld/wire/sns-ap-iraq-attack,0,878878.story?coll=sns-ap-nationworld-headlines|title=National & World News on Newsday.com|work=Newsday|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref> Two Japanese diplomats are killed near [[Tikrit]]. Two U.S. soldiers and a Colombian civilian contractor are killed in Baghdad.
* In Australia, the opposition [[Australian Labor Party|Labor Party]]'s finance spokesperson, [[Mark Latham]], announces that he will contest the party leadership ballot on {{Nowrap|2 December}} against the former leader [[Kim Beazley]]. Press reports place the two candidates at about 40 votes each, with about ten undecided.<ref>http://www.abc.net.au/news/newsitems/s999859.htm</ref>
* French and German [[university]] students continue to hold protests, including [[strike action|strikes]], over controversial policies such as [[tuition|tuition fees]]. German students also occupied the central offices of the [[Party of Democratic Socialism (Germany)|PDS]] in [[Berlin]], following a similar protest earlier in the week in which 30 to 40 students occupied the office of [[Thomas Flierl]] for more than 24 hours.
