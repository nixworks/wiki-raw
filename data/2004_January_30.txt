* [[Hutton Inquiry]]: Reporter [[Andrew Gilligan]] resigns from the [[BBC]] in the continuing fallout of the publication of [[James Brian Edward Hutton|Lord Hutton's]] report into the circumstances of the death of [[Dr David Kelly]]. This follows the earlier resignation of the [[Director-General of the BBC|Director-General]] [[Greg Dyke]] and chairman of the Board of Governors [[Gavyn Davies]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/3446391.stm|title=BBC NEWS - UK - Politics - Gilligan quits BBC over Hutton row|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/3446443.stm|title=BBC NEWS - UK - Politics - Gilligan statement in full|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* Former French Prime Minister and current Mayor of [[Bordeaux]] [[Alain Juppé]] is convicted of a party funding scam in the 1980s and early 1990s, and is given an 18-month suspended jail sentence and disqualified from elected office for 10 years, although he retains his [[mayor]]alty pending his appeal. He had been viewed as [[Jacques Chirac]]'s likely successor in the [[French presidential election, 2007|2007 Presidential election]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/europe/3444239.stm|title=BBC NEWS - Europe - Ex-French PM guilty of corruption|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* Muslims begin the annual [[Hajj]] today amid fears of a possible attack by Saudi-born [[Osama bin Laden]]'s [[al Qaeda]] network.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml?type=worldNews&storyID=4249017|title=World News|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* Sheik [[Ahmed Yassin]], leader of [[Hamas]], announces that his group is making an all-out effort to kidnap [[Israel]]i soldiers to use as bargaining chips for [[Palestinians]] in Israeli prisons, following the prisoner exchange between [[Israel]] and [[Hezbollah]] in which the remains of three Israeli soldiers and a businessman were exchanged for over 400 prisoners on January 29, 2004.<ref>http://www.nytimes.com/aponline/international/AP-Israel-Palestinians.html</ref>
* Self-confessed German [[cannibalism|cannibal]] [[Armin Meiwes]] is sentenced to eight years and six months' imprisonment for [[manslaughter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/europe/3443293.stm|title=BBC NEWS - Europe - Manslaughter verdict for cannibal|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref> The prosecution had sought a life sentence for murder.
* [[David Bradley (engineer)|David Bradley]], creator of the famous "[[Control-Alt-Delete|Ctrl-Alt-Del]]" keystroke combination, retires from [[IBM]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theregister.co.uk/content/28/35226.html|title=Ctrl-Alt-Del inventor makes final reboot|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
