;Politics and elections
*Following [[Tunisian Constituent Assembly election, 2011|Tunisia's first election]] since the [[Tunisian Revolution|ousting]] of President [[Zine El Abidine Ben Ali]] in January, the moderate [[Islamist]] [[Renaissance Party (Tunisia)|Renaissance Party]] declares victory, with their centre-left rivals, the [[Progressive Democratic Party (Tunisia)|Progressive Democratic Party]], conceding defeat. The official results of the election are due to be released next week. [http://www.reuters.com/article/2011/10/24/us-tunisia-election-idUSTRE79L28820111024 (Reuters)]
*The [[United Kingdom|British]] [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons]] votes to reject a proposed [[referendum]] on Britain's membership of the [[European Union]], with 483 MPs voting against the proposal and 111 voting in favour. Among those who supported the proposal were 81 [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] members, who rebelled against the anti-referendum stance of [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] [[David Cameron]], the leader of the Conservative Party. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-15425256 (BBC)]

;Science
*[[Ghulam Nabi Azad]], [[India]]'s [[Minister of Health and Family Welfare (India)|Minister of Health and Family Welfare]], reports that the country has almost entirely eradicated [[polio]] through a [[vaccination]] program which immunises over 170 million children every year. No new polio cases have been reported in India for over nine months. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-south-asia-15425852 (BBC)]
