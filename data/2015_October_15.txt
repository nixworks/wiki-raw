;Science
*[[Astronomy|Astronomers]] say they have observed bizarre light patterns using the [[Kepler (spacecraft)|Kepler Space Telescope]] from a star that appears old, but is shrouded in debris like a much younger star, roughly 1,500 [[Light-year|light-years]] away. This has led to speculation that these are an "artificial [[Extraterrestrial life|extraterrestrial]] mega-structure", orbiting the star known as [[KIC 8462852]] in the [[Cygnus (constellation)|Cygnus]] constellation. KIC 8462852 lies just above the [[Milky Way]] between the constellations Cygnus and Lyra. It first attracted the attention of astronomers in 2009 when the Kepler Space Telescope identified it as a candidate for having orbiting [[Earth]]-like planets. [https://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/forget-water-on-mars-astronomers-may-have-just-found-giant-alien-megastructures-orbiting-a-star-near-a6693886.html (Independent)] [http://news.discovery.com/space/alien-life-exoplanets/has-kepler-discovered-an-alien-megastructure-151014.htm (Discovery News)]
