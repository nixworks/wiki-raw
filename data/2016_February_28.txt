*[[South Korea]]n opposition lawmakers'  anti-terrorism bill filibuster, which stops all other legislative business, extends its world record to  115 hours Sunday afternoon,  easily besting [[Parliament of Canada|Canada's]] 58-hour session in 2011. The major issue is giving [[National Intelligence Service (South Korea)|South Korea's intelligence agency]] powers to monitor private communications. This [[National Assembly (South Korea)|parliamentary session]] is set to end March 11, with elections on [[South Korean legislative election, 2016|April 13]]. [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-southkorea-politics-filibuster-idUSKCN0W103V (Reuters)]
