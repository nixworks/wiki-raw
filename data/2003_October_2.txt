* [[North Korea]] claims to "have already [[Nuclear fuel cycle|processed]]" 8,000 [[fuel rod]]s from its [[Yongbyon nuclear reactor]] north of [[Pyongyang]] and is using the [[plutonium]] extracted during the process to make [[atomic bomb]]s to boost its nuclear capabilities for [[nuclear deterrence|nuclear deterrent]] force. Citing a "hostile policy" by the United States, Vice Foreign Minister [[Choe Su Hon]] states this is a means to safeguard the country's territory.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cnn.com/2003/WORLD/asiapcf/east/10/01/nkorea.nukes/index.html|title=CNN.com - U.S. doubts North Korean claims - Oct.  2, 2003|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[American occupation of Iraq|Occupation of Iraq]]: The [[United Nations Security Council]] discusses a new United States draft resolution on [[Iraq]], which would enhance the [[United Nations]]' role in [[Iraq]]. [[Secretary General of the United Nations|Secretary General]] [[Kofi Annan]] states draft did not go in the direction he recommended.  A quicker transfer of [[sovereignty]] "''may change the dynamics on the ground, in terms of the security situation, and send a message''".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.un.org/apps/news/story.asp?NewsID=8436&Cr=iraq&Cr1=|title=United Nations News Centre|date=2 October 2003|work=UN News Service Section|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[Technology]] – [[Genome]]: The genome chip arrived with several companies rushing to sell the known human genes.  The products will allow scientists to scan all genes in a human tissue sample at once to determine which are active, with lower cost and increased speed.<ref>[http://www.iht.com/articles/112288.html] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050307155052/http://www.iht.com/articles/112288.html |date=March 7, 2005 }}</ref>
* [[Sino-American relations]]: The [[United States Navy]] blames China for the 2001 [[Hainan Island incident]].  A report released to ''[[Jane's Defense Weekly]]'' under the [[Freedom of Information Act (United States)|Freedom of Information Act]] said that the pilot of the Chinese [[jet fighter]] made three passes at the U.S. propeller-driven [[reconnaissance plane]] over [[international waters]]. On the third pass, the Chinese fighter hit a propeller on the American plane.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.washtimes.com/national//20030912-110434-9850r.htm|title=China blamed in '01 air collision - Washington Times|work=The Washingtion Times|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
* [[Ontario general election, 2003]]: [[Dalton McGuinty]] becomes the new premier of [[Ontario]] when his party defeats [[Ernie Eves]]'s incumbent [[Ontario Progressive Conservative Party|Tories]] and [[Howard Hampton]]'s [[Ontario New Democratic Party|NDP]].

* [[European Union]]: The first bilateral meeting between France and [[Austria]] since 1998 is marked by disagreements over the [[European Constitution]].  Austria heads the group of small countries seeking to make changes to the text, but France wants to leave the draft by [[Valéry Giscard d'Estaing]] broadly untouched.<ref>[http://www.euobserver.com/index.phtml?sid=9&aid=12888] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050217053113/http://www.euobserver.com/index.phtml?sid=9&aid=12888 |date=February 17, 2005 }}</ref>
* [[Mass media|Media]]: A [[Program on International Policy Attitudes]] (PIPA) study conducted with [[California]]-based Knowledge Networks, titled "Misperceptions, the Media, and the Iraq War," is released.  According to this study, misperceptions about the [[2003 invasion of Iraq]] are most common among consumers of [[Fox News Channel]] and supporters of President [[George W. Bush]] and least common among consumers of [[Public Broadcasting Service|PBS]] and [[NPR]] and supporters of Democratic presidential candidates.  The study also found that frequent viewers of Fox News are more likely to believe in the misconceptions than less frequent ones. One example misconception is the belief that [[weapons of mass destruction]] have already been found in Iraq.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atimes.com/atimes/Middle_East/EJ04Ak01.html|title=Asia Times -|publisher=|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref>
