* The security officers of [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]] arrest nearly 30 members of the opposition political party "Samartlianoba" (Justice) and its satellite organizations on suspicion of plotting a coup against the government. The party, which advocates closer political ties with the [[Russia|Russian Federation]], is led by the nation's fugitive security chief [[Igor Giorgadze]] who is wanted by [[Interpol]] for his alleged involvement in the [[1995]] attempt on former Georgian President [[Shevardnadze]]'s life. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/5321040.stm (BBC)] 
* The United States government announces that fourteen suspected terrorists are to be transferred to the [[Guantanamo Bay detainment camp]] and admits that these suspects have been held in [[Central Intelligence Agency|CIA]] [[black site]]s. These people include [[Khalid Sheik Mohammed]], believed to be the No. 3 [[al-Qaida]] leader before he was captured in [[Pakistan]] in 2003; [[Ramzi Binalshibh]], an alleged would-be [[9/11]] hijacker; and [[Abu Zubaydah]], who was believed to be a link between [[Osama bin Laden]] and many al-Qaida cells before he was also captured in Pakistan in March 2002. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/5321606.stm (BBC)]
* The [[Economy of Singapore|Singaporean economy]] tops a list of 175 economies as the most business-friendly economy in the world in a survey conducted by the [[World Bank]]'s [[International Finance Corporation]]. [http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporebusinessnews/view/228852/1/.html (CNA)] [http://www.doingbusiness.org/EconomyRankings/Default.aspx?direction=asc&sort=1 (IFC)].

* [[Japan|Japan's]] [[Princess Akishino|Princess Kiko]] gives birth to a son by [[caesarean section]]. The as-yet unnamed boy is the first male heir born to the Japanese Imperial Family in more than 40 years, and will ultimately become Emperor if the current succession laws are unchanged. The child's father, [[Prince Akishino]] is the second son of [[Akihito|Emperor Akihito]].
