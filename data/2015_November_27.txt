*[[Retreat of glaciers since 1850]]
**Scientific studies confirm more than 90 percent of the world’s [[glacier]]s are retreating, and many of the smaller ones — like the [[alps|alpine]] [[ice sheet]]s of [[Glacier National Park (U.S.)|Glacier National Park]] in the U.S. — are rapidly disappearing. On the other side of world, at [[Khumbu Glacier]] near [[Mount Everest]] in the [[Himalayas]], expanding ponds are merging and forming larger bodies of water. This could threaten settlements downstream if they overflow. Thawing glaciers account for about 20 percent of the [[Sea level rise|sea-level rise]] recorded in the past century, adding to the [[meltwater]] coming from [[polar ice caps]] and [[ice sheets]]. [http://www.washingtonpost.com/sf/national/2015/11/26/ice-worlds/?wpmm=1&wpisrc=nl_headlines (Washington Post)] [http://www.bbc.com/news/science-environment-34928569 (BBC)] [http://www.radionz.co.nz/national/programmes/ourchangingworld/audio/201780508/antarctic-glacier's-past-rapid-retreat (Radio New Zealand)]
