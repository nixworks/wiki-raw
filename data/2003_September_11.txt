* As the United States remembers the deadliest [[terrorist attack]] ever on its shores, the [[United States Department of State]] warns it is seeing "increasing indications that [[al-Qaeda]] is preparing to strike United States interests abroad" and urges [[Citizenship in the United States|citizens]] overseas to take special caution on the second anniversary of the [[September 11 attacks]] amid growing indications that al-Qaeda is planning bigger attacks, "possibly involving [[Unconventional warfare|unconventional weapons]] such as [[Chemical warfare|chemical]] or [[Biological warfare|biological agents]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://asia.reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml?type=topNews&storyID=3427315|title=Business & Financial News, Breaking US & International News - Reuters.com|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/uslatest/story/0,1282,-3134360,00.html|title=Latest US news, world news, sports and opinion from the Guardian - theguardian.com - The Guardian|work=the Guardian|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2003/09/11/national/main572664.shtml|title=Mayors: Terror Defenses Lack Funds|date=11 September 2003|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* [[Sweden|Swedish]] [[Minister for Foreign Affairs (Sweden)|Foreign Minister]] [[Anna Lindh]] dies in the hospital from stab wounds inflicted while she was shopping in a department store in the centre of [[Stockholm]]. In the wake of the incident, both the Yes and No [[Euro]] campaigns suspended their activities.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/3098834.stm|title=BBC NEWS - Europe - Swedish minister dies after stabbing|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>
* The [[Security Cabinet of Israel]] votes in principle to expel [[President of the Palestinian National Authority]] [[Yasser Arafat]] from the [[West Bank]]. According to one source, the cabinet decided to ask the [[Israeli Defense Force]] to draw up a plan to expel Arafat. No timeline was specified, and Israeli government sources say that the decision was not to expel him immediately. The U.S. Department of States criticises such a move as "unhelpful". Thousands of Palestinians travel to the [[Mukataa|presidential compound]] in [[Ramallah]] to protest at the Israeli decision. [[Ahmed Qurei]], [[Prime Minister of the Palestinian National Authority]]-designate, announces in response that he is halting efforts to form a government. [[Minister of Foreign Affairs (Russia)|Russian Foreign Minister]] [[Igor Ivanov]] states an international force may be needed to end spiraling violence in the Palestinian territories.<ref>{{cite web|last=Bennet |first=James |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2003/09/11/international/middleeast/11WEB-MIDE.html?ex=1063944000&en=75f0f839c455e368&ei=5062&partner=GOOGLE |title=Israel Authorizes Removal of Arafat, but Puts Off Action |publisher=NYTimes.com |date=2003-09-11 |accessdate=2015-12-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/3098656.stm|title=BBC NEWS - Middle East - Israel decides to expel Arafat|publisher=|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rte.ie/news/2003/0911/mideast.html|title=Arafat to resist Israeli expulsion threats|date=11 September 2003|work=RTE.ie|accessdate=17 October 2015}}</ref>

At that speed, atoms move about 12&nbsp;cm/second.<ref>[http://www.cbc.ca/stories/2003/09/11/cold_sodium030911 ]{{dead link|date=December 2015}}</ref>
* The [[6-10 Office]], an arm of the [[National Security Bureau (Republic of China)|National Security Bureau]] and [[Public Security Bureau]] of the People's Republic of China responsible for the [[Persecution of Falun Gong|measures against Falun Gong]], changes its name to the "Leading Bureau for the Prevention and Procession of Evil Cults."
