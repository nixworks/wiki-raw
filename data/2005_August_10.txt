* In Chile, special Judge [[Sergio Muñoz]] indicts [[Lucía Hiriart]] and [[Marco Antonio Pinochet]], wife and youngest son of [[Augusto Pinochet]], on charges of tax evasion. Hiriart is confined in a military facility in [[Santiago, Chile|Santiago]] and Marco Pinochet in Santiago's Capuchinos Jail [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/americas/4139388.stm (BBC)].
* The [[Safe, Accountable, Flexible, Efficient Transportation Equity Act: A Legacy for Users|Transportation Equity Act of 2005]] is signed into law in the United States by President [[George W. Bush]]. [http://today.reuters.com/news/newsArticle.aspx?type=domesticNews&storyID=2005-08-10T170803Z_01_N10206006_RTRIDST_0_USREPORT-TRANSPORT-BUSH-DC.XML (Reuters)]
* [[Conflict in Iraq]]: Six [[Military of the United States|US soldiers]] are killed in [[Iraqi insurgency (Iraq War)|insurgent]] attacks throughout Iraq, including four killed in one attack in the northern [[oil]]-producing [[Kurdish people|Kurdish]] town of [[Baiji, Iraq|Baiji]], bringing the total US military death toll in Iraq to 1,841. [http://www.cnn.com/2005/WORLD/meast/08/10/iraq.main/index.html (CNN)] [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/4137362.stm (BBC)]
* A [[Sikorsky S-76]] [[helicopter]] of the Finnish company [[Copterline]] has [[10 August 2005 helicopter crash near Tallinn|crashed]] into the [[Gulf of Finland]] near Estonia's capital [[Tallinn]] with 14 on board; eight [[Finland|Finns]], four [[Estonia]]ns and two Americans. There are no survivors. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/europe/4138170.stm (BBC)]
* The United States and the [[African Union]] have dropped their demands that last week's coup in [[Mauritania]] be reversed. The US is working with the [[military junta]] to ensure that multi-party elections are held as soon as possible [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/4137434.stm (BBC)]
