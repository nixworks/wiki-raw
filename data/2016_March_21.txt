*[[Erasmus bus crash]]
**A [[Catalans|Catalan]] official says the 13 [[Student exchange program|exchange students]] killed in Sunday's accident were 19-to-25-year-old women,  seven from [[Italy]], two [[Germans]], an [[Austrians|Austrian]], a woman from [[France]], a [[Romanians|Romanian]] and an [[Uzbekistan|Uzbek]]. Twenty-four people are  being treated in hospital with one student in critical condition, and six people, including the driver, in serious condition. Reports from officials indicate the driver lost control of the coach and crashed to the other side before running into an oncoming car. The bus driver, who passed alcohol and drug tests, is being investigated for possible negligent homicide as police seek to determine the cause of the crash. *[[United States–Cuban Thaw]]
**At a  55-minute joint press conference, following their two-hour closed door meeting, [[President of the United States|United States President]] [[Barrack Obama]] and [[President of Cuba|Cuban President]] [[Raúl Castro]] agreed this “new day” of openness between the two countries benefits the entire hemisphere as well. Each acknowledged more needs to be done. Obama said he spoke frankly about human rights, free expression, and democracy; Castro said the U.S. needs to remove the [[Guantanamo Bay Naval Base|military base at Guantanamo Bay]], and completely end the U.S. embargo. Obama's experience gave him an advantage when journalists delved into  sensitive areas. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/on-first-full-day-in-cuba-obama-to-meet-with-castro-and-address-the-public/2016/03/20/931e4ff6-eef5-11e5-89c3-a647fcce95e0_story.html?wpmm=1&wpisrc=nl_headlines (''The Washington Post'')] [https://www.nytimes.com/2016/03/22/world/americas/obama-and-raul-castro-to-meet-in-pivotal-moment-for-us-cuba-thaw.html?em_pos=large&emc=edit_nn_20160322&nl=morning-briefing&nlid=11553763&_r=0 (''The New York Times'')]
