* [[President of the United States]] [[George W. Bush]] delivers his 2005 [[State of the Union]] address.  The section on [[Social Security (United States)|Social Security]] reform is booed by some members of Congress, a very rare show of open disdain for the [[President]] during such a speech. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/politics/transcripts/bushtext_020205.html Transcript (WP)]
* A former secret [[United States armed forces|U.S. military]] investigative report on [[Guantanamo Bay detainment camp|Guantanamo Bay]] is revealed to conclude there is no evidence of systemic detainee abuse but cited several cases of questionable physical force documented on videotape. Prisoners released have stated abuse is commonplace, and one former U.S. [[United States National Guard|National Guardsman]] received brain damage after being beaten while posing undercover as a rowdy detainee. All [[Freedom of Information Act (United States)|Freedom of Information Act]] requests by the [[ACLU]] for video and photographs depicting detainee treatment have been denied. [http://www.newsday.com/news/politics/wire/sns-ap-guantanamo-videotapes,0,901965.story (Newsday AP)] 
* The German Federal Labour Agency reports that the [[Germany|German]] [[unemployment]] rate hit 12.1% in January.  More than 5 million people are unemployed today in Germany.  Ignoring the margin of error inherent in the usage of different statistical methods over different areas, this rate is the highest since the [[Great Depression]] and the [[Weimar Republic]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/business/4228739.stm (BBC World)].
