* [[Israeli-Palestinian Conflict]]: [[Israel]]i warplanes and artillery units hit areas in the northern [[Gaza Strip]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/4377190.stm (BBC)]
* [[AIDS pandemic]]: The [[United Nations Children's Fund]] (Unicef), [[UNAIDS]] and other partners launch a global campaign, known as "[[Unite for Children, Unite Against AIDS]]", to spur action for the millions of children affected by [[HIV]]/[[AIDS]]. Fewer than 5 percent of HIV-positive children are receiving treatment. [http://www.unicef.org/uniteforchildren (Unicef)]
* An [[Ireland|Irish]] government-commissioned report, carried out by a retired [[Irish Supreme Court|Supreme Court]] judge, condemns two Irish bishops, the [[Roman Catholic Church]], the [[Garda Siochána]] (police) and health authorities for their failure to deal with [[Roman Catholic Church sex abuse scandal|clerical sex abuse]] over 40 years in the Diocese of Ferns ([[Wexford]]). The report suggested that 10% of priests in the diocese sexually abused children.  [http://www.rte.ie/news/2005/1025/ferns.html (RTÉ)] [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/europe/4376328.stm (BBC)] 
* [[BBC World Service]] announces that it will launch an [[Arabic language]] TV news channel in [[2007]]. Radio services in ten languages, mainly Central European but including [[Kazakh language|Kazakh]] and [[Thai language|Thai]], will be abandoned in 2006 to release funding for the new service. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/tv_and_radio/4374130.stm (BBC)]
