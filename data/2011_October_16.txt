;Armed conflicts and attacks
*[[2011 Libyan civil war]]: Fighting continues in the city of [[Bani Walid]], one of the few remaining towns loyal to the ousted dictator [[Muammar Gaddafi]], between pro-Gaddafi fighters and besieging forces loyal to the [[National Transitional Council]]. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-15330551 (BBC)]
*[[2011 Yemeni protests]]: At least six people are killed in the [[Yemen]]i capital [[Sana'a]] after government troops open fire on protesters. These killings constitute the second incident of fatal protest violence in Sana'a in under 48 hours. [http://www.marketwatch.com/story/at-least-6-dead-in-yemen-protests-2011-10-16 (MarketWatch)]
*[[War in Somalia (2009-)]]: [[Kenyan Army]] troops [[Kenyan invasion of Somalia (2011)|advance up to 100 kilometers]] into [[Somalia]] to pursue [[Al-Shabaab (militant group)|Al-Shabab]] militants. [http://english.aljazeera.net/news/africa/2011/10/20111016115410991692.html (Al Jazeera)]

;Arts and culture
*A ceremony is held to officially re-name the [[England|English]] town of [[Royal Wootton Bassett|Wootton Bassett]] with the prefix "[[List of place names with royal patronage in the United Kingdom|Royal]]" in honour of its role in the repatriation of British military personnel killed in action in [[Iraq War|Iraq]] and [[War in Afghanistan (2001-present)|Afghanistan]]. It is the first town to receive such an honour since 1909. [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-wiltshire-15325154 (BBC)]

*Voters in [[Bolivia]] go to the polls for a [[Bolivian judicial election, 2011|judicial election]] - the first ever held in [[Latin America]] - with opposition politicians urging voters to [[spoilt ballot|spoil their ballots]] in protest against the government of incumbent [[President of Bolivia|President]] [[Evo Morales]].
