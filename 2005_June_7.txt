* Scientists at [[UCLA]] use a [[pyroelectric]] [[crystal]] heated from -34.4 to 7.2 degrees Celsius to produce an [[electric field]] of about 100,000 [[volt]]s, accelerating [[proton|hydrogen nuclei]] and producing [[helium]] nuclei in the subsequent collisions. This [[cold fusion]] experiment has been repeated successfully, and other scientists have confirmed the results. [http://www.christiansciencemonitor.com/2005/0606/p25s01-stss.html (CSMonitor)] [http://www.nature.com/nature/journal/v434/n7037/suppinfo/nature03575.html (Nature)] [http://rodan.physics.ucla.edu/pyrofusion/ (UCLA)] [http://msnbc.msn.com/id/7654627 (MSNBC)]

* A new type of [[sauropod]] has been discovered with a short, stubby neck. Unlike all other known sauropods, whose long necks could get up to four times the length of their backs, ''Brachytrachelopan'''s neck was shorter than its backbone. This species was also unable to lift its neck and head above [[Horizontal plane|horizontal]]. [http://news.nationalgeographic.com/news/2005/06/0601_050601_stubby_dino.html (National Geographic)]
