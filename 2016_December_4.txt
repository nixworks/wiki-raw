*[[Uzbekistani presidential election, 2016]]
**Voters in [[Uzbekistan]] go to the polls for a presidential election, the first since the death of [[Islam Karimov]] who governed the country for 25 years. Acting President [[Shavkat Mirziyoyev]] is expected to win the election. [http://www.dailypress.com/news/nationworld/sns-bc-as--uzbekistan-election-20161204-story.html (AP via ''Daily Press'')]
*[[Austrian presidential election, 2016]]
**Voters in [[Austria]] go to the polls to elect a new [[President of Austria|President]] in a second round run-off between [[The Greens – The Green Alternative|Green]] candidate [[Alexander Van der Bellen]] and [[Freedom Party of Austria]] candidate [[Norbert Hofer]]. [http://www.reuters.com/article/us-austria-election-idUSKBN13S0W0 (Reuters)]
*[[Italian constitutional referendum, 2016]]
**[[Italy|Italians]] go to the polls to for a referendum on whether to approve a constitutional law that amends the [[Constitution of Italy]] to reform the powers of the [[Italian Parliament]]. [[Prime Minister of Italy|Prime Minister]] [[Matteo Renzi]], who introduced the referendum bill, has promised to resign if voters reject the changes. However, he has stated that there will not be a parliamentary election following this referendum, casting doubt on whether or not he will follow through with this promise. [http://www.reuters.com/article/us-italy-referendum-idUSKBN13T019 (Reuters)], [http://www.politico.eu/article/matteo-renzi-no-early-election-after-referendum-2018-poll (POLITICO)]
*Thousands of [[Indonesian people|Indonesians]] protest in Jakarta for tolerance after a large protest rally against [[blasphemy]] on Friday. [http://wtop.com/asia/2016/12/indonesians-rally-for-tolerance-after-blasphemy-protests/ (AP via WTOP)]
