*[[Nuclear program of Iran]]
**The [[United Nations]] finds [[Iran|Iran's]] firing of a [[medium-range ballistic missile]] in October violated a June 2010 resolution banning the Islamic Republic from launches capable of delivering nuclear weapons. The test was not technically a violation of the July nuclear deal between Iran and six world powers.  It's not known if the [[United Nations Security Council|Security Council]] will take any action.  [http://www.csmonitor.com/World/2015/1215/Iran-s-October-missile-test-violated-UN-sanctions-experts-say (AP via ''The Christian Science Monitor'')] [http://www.reuters.com/article/us-iran-missiles-un-exclusive-idUSKBN0TY1T920151215 (Reuters)]
**The U.N. [[International Atomic Energy Agency|International Atomic Energy Agency's]] (IAEA) [[Board of Governors of the International Atomic Energy Agency|Board of Governors]] decides, by consensus unopposed, to close its investigation into whether Iran once had a secret nuclear weapons program. The IAEA will continue to police Tehran's activities. [http://thenewstelegraph.com/2015/12/15/un-closes-the-books-on-decade-long-nuclear-probe-of-iran/ (The News Telegraph)]  [http://in.reuters.com/article/iran-nuclear-idINKBN0TY0UR20151215 (Reuters)]
