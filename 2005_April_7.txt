* [[Israeli-Palestinian conflict]]:
** A [[Palestinian people|Palestinian]]-fired [[Qassam rocket]] hits a [[cemetery]] in the Israeli town of [[Sderot]], causing minimal damage and no injuries. Israeli Defence Minister [[Shaul Mofaz]] says Israel will not let it pass and criticises the [[Palestinian Authority|PA]]'s lack of action. This is the first rocket fired inside the carrot-shaped "[[1949 Armistice Agreements|Green line]]" since late January. [http://www.haaretz.com/hasen/spages/562460.html (Haaretz)]
** Two [[Jew]]s are arrested on suspicion of planting fake bombs in [[Jerusalem]] in an attempt to disrupt the planned [[Israel]]i pullout from the [[Gaza Strip]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/middle_east/4420875.stm (BBC)]
** Environmental groups condemn a plan by [[Israel]] to relocate [[Israeli settlement|Israeli settlers]] from [[Gaza Strip|Gaza]] to the [[Nitzanim]] nature reserve in southern Israel. [[Israeli Prime Minister]] [[Ariel Sharon]] supports the request of some settlers to relocate the entire [[Gush Katif]] to the Nitzanim area and orders experts to start checking this issue thoroughly. Nitzanim is home to [[endangered species|endangered]] [[turtle]]s and around 100 [[gazelle]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/middle_east/4419751.stm (BBC)] [http://www.haaretz.com/hasen/spages/562313.html (Haaretz)]
* [[Ibrahim Jaafari]], a [[Shia]], has replaced [[Iyad Allawi]] as the interim [[prime minister]] of [[Iraq]]. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/middle_east/4420971.stm (BBC)]
* Passenger buses set out from [[India]] to [[Pakistan]] across the [[Indian Kashmir barrier]] through the troubled and controversial [[Kashmir]] region in a symbolic "Caravan of Peace." Some attacks on the buses were reported in the militant-occupied area, but none were successful, according to local media outlets. [http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/7413326/ (MSNBC)]

Canada has unsuccessfully demanded return of Kazemi's body.
