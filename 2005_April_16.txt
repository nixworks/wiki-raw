* [[Najib Mikati]] becomes the new [[List of Prime Ministers of Lebanon|Prime Minister]] of [[Lebanon]], replacing [[Omar Karami]], who has [[resignation|resigned]] for a second time after seven weeks of frustrated efforts to form a [[consensus government]]. * The [[Gaelic Athletic Association]] congress votes overwhelmingly to suspend its controversial rule 42 ban on 'foreign games' being played at its sports venues, to enable Irish soccer and rugby international fixtures to be played at its [[Croke Park]] [[stadium]]. [http://www.rte.ie/news/2005/0416/gaa.html (RTÉ News)]
* A [[Richter scale|magnitude 5.1]] [[earthquake]] hits [[Southern California]]. No injuries or major damage have been reported. [http://www.cnn.com/2005/US/04/16/california.quake.ap/index.html (CNN)]
* The [[G7]] leaders meet in [[Washington, D.C.]] in the [[World Bank]] headquarters. Among the main discussion points are the rising price of [[oil]] and the pegging of the [[Renminbi|Chinese yuan]] to the [[U.S. dollar]]. [http://www.cnn.com/2005/US/04/16/finance.meetings.ap/index.html (CNN)]
