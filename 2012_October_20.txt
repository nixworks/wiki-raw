*[[Syrian civil war]]: UN and Arab League special envoy [[Lakhdar Brahimi]] meets with Syrian [[Minister of Foreign Affairs and Expatriates (Syria)|Foreign Minister]] [[Walid Muallem]] in [[Damascus]] in an attempt to push for a temporary ceasefire during next week's [[Eid al-Adha]] holiday. *[[2011–present Libyan factional fighting]]:
**Authorities in [[Tripoli]] announce the capture of [[Muammar Gaddafi]]'s former spokesman [[Moussa Ibrahim]] near the town of [[Tarhouna]], 40 miles south of the capital [[Tripoli]]. Ibrahim's whereabouts had been unknown since the end of the [[2011 Libyan Civil War|civil war]] in 2011 and there had been several earlier reports of his arrest. Hours later an audio message purported to be by Ibrahim appears online, in which he denies being captured or even being in Libya. [http://www.trust.org/alertnet/news/libya-govt-says-former-gaddafi-spokesman-captured/ (Reuters)] [http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-20016076 (BBC)]
**[[Siege of Bani Walid (2012)]]: Reports emerge that [[Khamis Gaddafi]], the youngest son of Muammar Gaddafi, was killed during the fighting in Bani Walid. The 29-year-old has been proclaimed dead several times since the beginning of last year's civil war. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2012/oct/20/khamis-gaddafi-killed-bani-walid-muammar (''The Guardian'')]
*The [[United Nations Security Council]] announces plans to impose sanctions on the leaders of [[Democratic Republic of the Congo]]'s [[March 23 Movement|M23]] rebel movement due to  violations of an arms embargo. [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2012/10/20121019213753251570.html (Al Jazeera)]
*Suspected members of [[Boko Haram]] attack the northern Nigerian city of [[Potiskum]], destroying several buildings and leaving at least 23 locals dead. [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2012/10/20121020114424963207.html (Al Jazeera)]
*Overnight clashes in southeastern [[Turkey]] leave 9 people dead, including 6 security officers and 3 suspected Kurdish militants. [http://www.aljazeera.com/news/europe/2012/10/2012102095034344938.html (Al Jazeera)]
