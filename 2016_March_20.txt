;International relations
*[[United States–Cuban Thaw]]
**[[President of the United States|U.S. President]] [[Barack Obama]] and the [[First Family of the United States|First Family]] begin their three-day visit to [[Havana]], [[Cuba]], to mark the end of 54 years of tensions in [[Cuba–United States relations]]. Obama is the first sitting U.S. president to visit the country since [[Calvin Coolidge]] in 1928. [http://www.havanatimes.org/?p=117493 (''Havana Times'')] [http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/news/OBAMA2016-03-20T13-29-54 (''Jamaica Observer'')]
**Before the President's arrival, Cuban authorities arrest more than 50 [[Ladies in White]] [[human rights activists]]. The group marches after every Sunday  Mass at a church in the [[Miramar, Havana|Miramar]] [[suburb]] of Havana,  and usually get arrested and detained for hours or days. They had hoped President Obama's visit would change that routine. [http://www.usatoday.com/story/news/world/2016/03/20/cuba-dissidents-protesters-arrested-president-obama-visit/82048950/ (''USA Today'')] [http://www.havanatimes.org/?p=117493 (''Havana Times'')]
